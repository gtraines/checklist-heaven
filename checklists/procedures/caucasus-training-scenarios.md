## Overview

These four scenarios translate the four phases of the [Surface Attack Course](./surface-attack-course.md) into practical DCS World exercises on the **Caucasus** map. The Caucasus offers exceptional terrain variety — from flat Black Sea coastal plains ideal for pattern work, to semi-arid rolling hills suited for precision sensor employment, to narrow river valleys and urban settings demanding strict ROE, to the extreme high peaks of the main Caucasus range for advanced FAC-A operations.

Each scenario builds on the skills of the previous phase. They are direct Caucasus equivalents of the [NTTR training scenarios](./nttr-training-scenarios.md) and share identical academic references.

**Prerequisites:**
- Completion of the [Surface Attack Course](./surface-attack-course.md) academic phases.
- Aircraft-specific type qualification for the chosen platform.
- Familiarity with Caucasus airspace and airfield procedures.

## Scenario 1 — IRON ANVIL 01-C

> **Phase 1: Basic Surface Attack — Unguided Weapons, Visual Delivery, Box Pattern**

### Location: Kobuleti North Training Area — "The Polygon"

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 41°58'00" E 41°52'00" \| MGRS: 37T FH 74 48 |
| **Elevation** | ~30 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Kobuleti (UG5X) — ~5 NM South |

### Why This Location

The flat coastal plain immediately north of Kobuleti airfield is the ideal Caucasus equivalent of an open range. The abandoned industrial strip provides a clearly visible run-in line. Surrounding marshland and the Black Sea coastline give immediate safe-abort options with no terrain obstacles within the pattern area.

### Navigation to Kobuleti North

1. **Depart** Kobuleti (UG5X) RWY 07.
2. **Turn North** immediately onto Hdg 360°. Remain along the coast.
3. **TACAN:** Track KBL Ch 67X 360 Radial at 5 NM DME.
4. **Visual:** The abandoned industrial/airfield area is directly ahead. A column of Red Smoke marks the IP at the southern threshold of the abandoned strip.
5. **Climb** to pattern altitude (1,500 ft MSL / ~1,470 ft AGL) before entering the pattern.

> ⚠️ **Altitude Note:** Due to near-sea-level elevation, pattern altitude is lower than standard NTTR procedures. Do not exceed 20° dive angle below 1,000 ft AGL. Maintain 500 ft AGL minimum recovery altitude throughout.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Coordinates |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1** | Truck convoy (Ural-375 ×4) — primary target | N 41°58'30" E 41°52'00" |
| **IP KOBULETI NORTH** | Red Smoke — 2 NM south of T-1 | N 41°56'30" E 41°52'00" |

### Run-In Heading & Box Pattern

- **Run-In Heading:** 360° (South to North; abandoned strip aligns with this heading).
- **Pattern:** Right-hand box (Downwind Hdg 180°, 1.5 NM east of run-in line over the marshland).
- **IP:** Red Smoke at N 41°56'30" E 41°52'00" — 2 NM south of T-1.

```
        DOWNWIND  (Hdg 180°, 1.5 NM East — over marshland)
            │
◄───────────┘  BASE  (1.5 NM abeam T-1, Hdg 270°)
│
▼   FINAL/RUN-IN  (Hdg 360° — "In" call at IP smoke)
│
[T-1]  TARGET (Truck Convoy — abandoned strip)
│
▲   RECOVERY (4–5 g pull, Egress East over water)
│
└──────────────►  CROSSWIND  (Hdg 090° — rejoin downwind)
```

### Student Objectives

1. Depart Kobuleti, navigate to IP KOBULETI NORTH using TACAN/visual coastline.
2. Fly three (3) box pattern laps to build situational awareness before weapons employment.
3. Conduct one (1) high-angle dive attack (30–45°) with Mk-82 / FAB-100. Transmit **"In"** / **"Off"** calls.
4. Conduct one (1) low-angle strafe pass with gun. Transmit **"Guns Guns Guns"**.
5. Conduct one (1) rocket attack (low-angle, 10–15°).
6. Return to Kobuleti with fuel above Bingo state. Transmit Bingo if reached.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Daytime (1000–1400 local) |
| **Weather** | CAVOK, visibility unrestricted, light sea breeze |
| **Threat** | None — no air defences active |
| **Ordnance (A-10)** | 2× Mk-82, 2× LAU-61 rocket pods, full GAU-8 |
| **Ordnance (Su-25)** | 2× FAB-100, 2× B-8M rocket pods, full GSh-30 |

### Success Criteria

- At least 1 of 2 bombs impacts within 25 m of T-1.
- Strafing rounds observed on or within 10 m of T-1.
- No safety violations (minimum altitude/frag floors).
- Correct **"In"** / **"Off"** brevity calls transmitted on all passes.

## Scenario 2 — PRECISION STRIKE 02-C

> **Phase 2: Intermediate Surface Attack — TGP/Shkval, Precision Guided Munitions**

### Location: Vaziani Training Range

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 41°38'00" E 45°06'00" \| MGRS: 38T MM 25 10 |
| **Elevation** | ~2,500 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Vaziani (UG27) — ~5 NM West; Tbilisi-Lochini (UGTB) — ~10 NM North-West |

### Why This Location

The semi-arid rolling hills east of Vaziani replicate the thermal contrast of a real-world weapons range. The terrain provides high IR/TV contrast for TGP and Shkval sensor acquisition. Sufficient open airspace exists east of the Alazani River valley for high-altitude LGB employment at Angels 12+.

### Navigation to Vaziani Range

1. **Depart** Vaziani (UG27) RWY 13.
2. **Turn East** onto Hdg 100°.
3. **TACAN:** Track VAZ Ch 22X 100 Radial at 6 NM DME.
4. **Visual:** Rolling dry hills with visible geometric earthwork target circles and tank revetments. Targets are marked with orange panels and identification smoke.
5. **Climb** to employment altitude (Angels 12 minimum for LGB work) during transit.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1 BUNKER** | Hardened command bunker (static) | LGB / Kh-29T target |
| **T-2 ARMOUR** | T-72 MBT ×3 (active, weapons hold) | Maverick / Vikhr target |
| **T-3 SOFT** | Ural-375 truck convoy ×4 | Secondary / backup |

Targets are spaced ~0.5 NM apart. T-1 BUNKER is sited in the earthwork revetment. T-2 ARMOUR is positioned in the open for unobstructed sensor acquisition.

### Student Objectives

**A-10C / A-10C II:**
1. TGP Point Track on T-1 BUNKER. Confirm SPI. Employ one (1) GBU-12 (LGB) from level flight at Angels 12+. Transmit **"Pickle"**.
2. TGP designate T-2 ARMOUR. Slave AGM-65 seeker to SPI. Engage one (1) T-72. Transmit **"Rifle"**.
3. Conduct one (1) CCIP dive attack with Mk-82 on T-3 SOFT.

**Su-25T:**
1. Activate Shkval. Lock T-2 ARMOUR. Employ Vikhr (AT-16) against one (1) T-72. Maintain steady flight until impact. Transmit **"Rifle"**.
2. Employ Kh-29T (TV-guided) against T-1 BUNKER from level flight. Transmit **"Rifle"**.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Dawn or dusk (transition lighting) |
| **Weather** | Scattered cloud at 8,000 ft; 15 km visibility |
| **Threat** | Medium — AI IR MANPADS active in target area |
| **Ordnance (A-10C)** | 1× GBU-12, 2× AGM-65D, 2× Mk-82 |
| **Ordnance (Su-25T)** | 2× Vikhr, 1× Kh-29T |

### Success Criteria

- LGB/Kh-29T: Direct hit or within 5 m CEP on T-1 BUNKER.
- Maverick/Vikhr: T-72 killed or mission-killed.
- No break in Vikhr laser beam during flight (Su-25T only).
- Correct brevity codes used for all releases.
- Egress above minimum recovery altitude (2,000 ft AGL).

## Scenario 3 — CLOSE FIGHT 03-C

> **Phase 3: CAS & JTAC Coordination — 9-Line, Keyhole CAS, Abort**

### Location: Gori Valley — City Outskirts

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 41°58'00" E 44°06'00" \| MGRS: 38T MM 25 48 |
| **Elevation** | ~2,000 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Tbilisi-Lochini (UGTB) — ~40 NM South-East |

### Why This Location

Gori sits in a broad east-west valley constrained by hills to the north and south. The fortress hill (Goristsikhe) at the city centre is an unmistakeable visual reference for talk-ons. The Liakhvi River and M27 highway provide additional reference lines. The valley geometry enforces realistic east-west attack axis restrictions, while the proximity of the city to the target area demands strict positive ID before weapons release.

### Navigation to Gori Valley

1. **Depart** Tbilisi-Lochini (UGTB) RWY 31.
2. **Turn North-West** onto Hdg 310°. Follow the M27 highway / Mtkvari River corridor.
3. **Visual:** ~40 NM from Tbilisi. The city of Gori is visible in a wide valley. The fortress hill (Goristsikhe) is immediately recognizable as an elevated rocky outcrop at the city centre.
4. **Climb** to hold altitude (Angels 10) before reaching the Gori valley boundary. Do not descend below Angels 8 until Cleared Hot.

> ⚠️ **ROE:** No ordnance within 300 m of the city centre (Goristsikhe fortress). No ordnance on the M27 highway. Positive ID mandatory before weapons employment — civilian vehicles are present on all roads.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Grid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ALPHA** | Keyhole Hold — North Anchor | N 42°02'00" E 44°06'00" |
| **BRAVO** | Keyhole Hold — East Anchor | N 41°58'00" E 44°14'00" |
| **T-1 VEHICLE** | UAZ-469 technical ×2 at road junction on outskirts | N 41°57'30" E 44°05'00" |
| **T-2 INFANTRY** | Insurgent squad near Liakhvi River bridge | N 41°58'30" E 44°04'30" |
| **JTAC** | Humvee, "OP GORISTSIKHE" on fortress hill | N 41°58'45" E 44°06'30" |
| **FRIENDLIES** | Blue Force element, 350 m north of T-1 | N 41°58'15" E 44°05'00" |

### Keyhole Geometry

```
               [ALPHA]

             (Angels 10)
            /             \
           /               \
[DELTA] ---    GORI VALLEY  --- [BRAVO]
W Hold     [T-1] [T-2]          E Hold
(Angels 10) [JTAC — FORTRESS]  (Angels 10)
           \               /
            \             /
               [CHARLIE]
               S Hold
             (Angels 10)
```

### 9-Line Brief (Pre-Scripted for Instructor)

| Line | Element | Value |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | IP/BP | "IP: GORISTSIKHE FORTRESS" |
| 2 | Heading | "Heading 270" |
| 3 | Distance | "2.5 NM" |
| 4 | Elevation *(read back)* | "Elevation 2,000 ft" |
| 5 | Description | "Two technicals at road junction, west of the fortress" |
| 6 | Coordinates *(read back)* | "38T MM 249 480" |
| 7 | Mark | "Smoke, colour red" |
| 8 | Friendlies | "Friendlies 350 m north, blue smoke" |
| 9 | Egress | "Egress east, follow river" |
| + | Restrictions *(read back)* | "No ordnance within 300 m of fortress. No ordnance on the highway." |

### Student Objectives

1. Depart Tbilisi-Lochini, navigate to Hold ALPHA via M27 highway visual reference.
2. Transmit CAS Check-In (MNPOPCA) to JTAC on freq 133.000.
3. Receive and read back 9-Line. Await JTAC confirmation.
4. Execute one (1) Cleared Hot attack pass on T-1 VEHICLE from ALPHA.
5. When JTAC triggers the abort scenario on the second pass — execute **immediate abort**: pull up, egress east following the river, transmit **"[Callsign] Abort"**.
6. Transmit BDA after cleared-hot pass. Return to Tbilisi-Lochini.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Midday |
| **Weather** | Scattered cloud, 5,000 ft ceiling, 10 km visibility |
| **Threat** | Medium-High — MANPADS and ZSU-23 active in city area |
| **JTAC** | AI JTAC (scripted triggers) at OP GORISTSIKHE |

### Success Criteria

- MNPOPCA check-in complete, all 7 elements present.
- 9-Line read-back: Lines 4, 6, and restrictions verbatim.
- Cleared Hot attack: weapon employment within attack corridor. No ordnance within 300 m of fortress or on highway.
- Abort executed cleanly — no release after abort call.
- BDA transmitted within 30 seconds of egress.

## Scenario 4 — IRON CONTROL 04-C

> **Phase 4: Airborne FAC-A — Stack Management, Marking, Talk-On**

### Location: High Passes North of Sukhumi

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 43°15'00" E 41°15'00" \| MGRS: 37T FH 20 90 |
| **Elevation** | ~8,000 – 12,000 ft MSL (varied extreme terrain) |
| **Airfield** | Sukhumi-Babushara (UGSS) — ~30 NM South |

### Why This Location

The main Caucasus range north of Sukhumi presents the most demanding FAC-A operating environment in the Caucasus map. Deep, narrow valleys run between peaks exceeding 12,000 ft MSL. Terrain masking is constant — targets will intermittently disappear from the FAC-A's line of sight as both the FAC-A and attack aircraft manoeuvre. The FAC-A must actively reposition to maintain observation, making the "Wedding Cake" deconfliction problem three-dimensional. High altitude degrades aircraft performance (reduced engine thrust, increased turn radius), making precise stack management critical.

> ⚠️ **Performance Warning:** At Angels 15+, attack aircraft will notice significantly degraded performance. Brief students on density altitude effects before the sortie. Minimum fuel planning must account for the extended climb from Sukhumi (~30 NM at low level, then climb to Angels 20+).

### Navigation to the High Passes

1. **Depart** Sukhumi-Babushara (UGSS) RWY 12.
2. **Turn North**. Follow the Kodori River valley — it leads directly into the high mountain passes.
3. **Visual:** The main Caucasus ridgeline is visible immediately to the north. The operating area is the first major north-south valley past the ridgeline.
4. **Climb** aggressively on departure — FAC-A must reach Angels 14 before entering the high valley.

### Threat Environment

| Unit | Type | Status | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SA-18 IGLA (×4)** | MANPADS | Active | Mountain peaks, distributed |
| **ZU-23 (×2)** | AAA | Active | Valley floor, north and south ends |

> ⚠️ **The MANPADS on the peaks dominate the low-altitude airspace. Attack aircraft descending below Angels 8 in the valley are in the MANPADS engagement envelope. The FAC-A must hold all aircraft above this floor until the attack axis is confirmed clear.**

### Multi-Ship Stack (pre-spawned AI)

| Callsign | Aircraft | Altitude | Anchor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **TIGER 1-1** | A-10C (Student FAC-A) | Angels 14 (Working) | Orbit south of ridgeline |
| **BOAR 1-1 / 1-2** | A-10C ×2 (AI) | Angels 18 | Hold ALPHA — West |
| **HAZE 2-1 / 2-2** | Su-25T ×2 (AI) | Angels 20 | Hold BRAVO — East |

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1 COLUMN** | BMP-2 ×3 convoy moving on mountain road | Primary FAC-A target — moving |
| **T-2 RADAR** | P-19 Flat Face EWR (static) on north ridge | Precision mark target |
| **T-3 TROOPS** | Infantry section dug into hillside, no road access | Talk-On target (no mark available) |

### Wedding Cake Altitudes

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TOP LAYER:    Angels 20+                   │  HAZE 2-1/2-2 (Transit/Fuel)
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  MIDDLE LAYER: Angels 15–19                 │  BOAR 1-1/1-2 (CAS Stack Hold)
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LOW LAYER:    Angels 14                    │  TIGER 1-1 (FAC-A Working)
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
               Below Angels 8:  MANPADS envelope — DO NOT descend
               Below Angels 12: Cleared Hot attack zone (valley floor)
               Terrain:         Peaks 10,000–12,000 ft MSL — obstacle
```

### Student Objectives (as FAC-A TIGER 1-1)

1. **Check-In** both flights (BOAR and HAZE). Receive MNPOPCA. Assign Keyhole anchors and hold altitudes. Brief MANPADS threat and minimum safe altitude.
2. **Brief** BOAR 1-1 with a complete 9-Line for T-1 COLUMN (moving convoy). Await read-back confirmation.
3. **WP Mark:** Fire WP rocket to mark T-1 COLUMN. Transmit *"Mark is on deck."* Control BOAR 1-1 to Cleared Hot. Account for the valley geometry — attack heading must be along the valley axis.
4. **Sparkle Mark (night phase):** Illuminate T-2 RADAR with IR designator. Confirm *"Contact Sparkle"* from HAZE 2-1 before issuing Cleared Hot.
5. **Talk-On (no marks):** Guide HAZE 2-2 onto T-3 TROOPS using the 4-step funnel. T-3 is in defilade — reference points (ridgeline, stream, treeline) must be used precisely. Student must achieve attacker Tally within three steps.
6. **Stack Management:** Maintain altitude deconfliction throughout. No aircraft below Angels 8 unless Cleared Hot on the valley-floor attack axis. Sequence BOAR and HAZE so only one flight is in the attack corridor at a time.
7. **BDA:** Receive and record BDA from all attacking flights.

### Suggested Talk-On Script (T-3 TROOPS)

*Use only if student requires coaching:*

1. *"Reference the main ridgeline running east-west at your 12 o'clock."*
2. *"On the south face of the ridge, find the prominent rocky outcrop — the only bare rock visible. It's approximately 2 NM north of the valley floor."*
3. *"200 metres west of that outcrop is a small treeline running downhill. Follow the treeline to its bottom."*
4. *"Target is an infantry section dug into the hillside at the base of that treeline, 50 metres east of a dry stream bed."*

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Night (NVG environment) |
| **Weather** | Clear, unlimited visibility; possible cloud on peaks |
| **Threat** | High — SA-18 MANPADS on peaks; ZU-23 in valley |
| **FAC-A Platform** | A-10C (TGP for sparkle / WP mark via rocket pod) |

### Success Criteria

- All check-ins received; anchors correctly assigned; MANPADS threat briefed.
- 9-Lines complete and read-back confirmed for both flights.
- WP mark placed within 100 m of T-1. Attack Cleared Hot with correct valley-axis orientation.
- Sparkle confirmed Contact before Cleared Hot (no premature clearance).
- Talk-On: attacker achieves Tally within three description steps.
- Zero altitude conflicts in stack. No aircraft below Angels 8 without explicit Cleared Hot.
- BDA received from all attacking flights.
