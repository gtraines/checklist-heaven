## Overview

These four scenarios translate the four phases of the [Surface Attack Course](./surface-attack-course.md) into practical DCS World exercises on the Nevada Test and Training Range (NTTR) map. Each scenario builds on the skills of the previous phase and uses a specific NTTR location selected for its terrain and existing infrastructure.

**Prerequisites:**
- Completion of the [Surface Attack Course](./surface-attack-course.md) academic phases.
- Aircraft-specific type qualification for the chosen platform.
- Familiarity with NTTR airspace and airfield procedures.

**NTTR Scenario Map:**

| Scenario | Phase | Location | Airfield |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| IRON ANVIL 01 | Phase 1 — Basic SA | Range 63B (The Containers) | Creech AFB (KINS) |
| PRECISION STRIKE 02 | Phase 2 — Precision | Range 64 (Dreamland) | Groom Lake (KXTA) |
| CLOSE FIGHT 03 | Phase 3 — CAS/JTAC | Goldfield | Tonopah Test Range (KTNX) |
| IRON CONTROL 04 | Phase 4 — FAC-A | Tolicha Peak ECR | Tonopah Test Range (KTNX) |

## Scenario 1 — IRON ANVIL 01

> **Phase 1: Basic Surface Attack — Unguided Weapons, Visual Delivery, Box Pattern**

### Location: Range 63B — "The Containers"

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 36°58'00" W 116°02'30" \| MGRS: 11S NV 86 92 |
| **Elevation** | ~4,200 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Creech AFB (KINS) — ~25 NM Southeast |

### Navigation to Range 63B

1. **Depart** Creech AFB RWY 31/13.
2. **Turn North-West** onto approx. Hdg 315°. Follow US-95 North as a visual reference.
3. **TACAN:** Track BTY (Beatty) 140 Radial at 15 NM DME. Range 63B will be on your left (west).
4. **Visual Acquisition:** Look for the large, distinct rectangular grid of steel shipping containers in the open desert valley north of the Spotted Range hills. A column of Red Smoke marks the IP (run-in start).
5. **Descend** to pattern altitude (5,000 ft MSL / ~800 ft AGL) before entering the pattern.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Coordinates |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1** | Truck convoy (Ural-375 ×4) — primary target | N 36°58'00" W 116°02'30" |
| **IP RANGE 63** | Red Smoke — 3 NM south of target | N 36°55'00" W 116°02'30" |

### Run-In Heading & Box Pattern

- **Run-In Heading:** 360° (South to North; targets on the north end of range).
- **Pattern:** Right-hand box (Downwind Hdg 180°, 2 NM east of run-in line).
- **IP:** Red Smoke at N 36°55'00" W 116°02'30" — 3 NM south of targets.

```
        DOWNWIND  (Hdg 180°, 2 NM East)
            │
◄───────────┘  BASE  (2 NM abeam T-1, Hdg 270°)
│
▼   FINAL/RUN-IN  (Hdg 360° — "In" call)
│
[T-1]  TARGET (Truck Convoy)
│
▲   RECOVERY (4–5 g pull, Egress East)
│
└──────────────►  CROSSWIND  (Hdg 090° — rejoin downwind)
```

### Student Objectives

1. Depart Creech AFB, navigate to IP RANGE 63 using TACAN/visual.
2. Fly three (3) box pattern laps to build situational awareness before weapons employment.
3. Conduct one (1) high-angle dive attack (30–45°) with Mk-82. Transmit **"In"** / **"Off"** calls.
4. Conduct one (1) low-angle strafe pass with gun. Transmit **"Guns Guns Guns"**.
5. Conduct one (1) rocket attack (low-angle, 10–15°).
6. Return to Creech AFB with fuel above Bingo state. Transmit Bingo if reached.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Daytime (1000–1400 local) |
| **Weather** | CAVOK, visibility unrestricted, winds calm |
| **Threat** | None — no air defences active |
| **Ordnance (A-10)** | 2× Mk-82, 2× LAU-61 rocket pods, full GAU-8 |
| **Ordnance (Su-25)** | 2× FAB-100, 2× B-8M rocket pods, full GSh-30 |

### Success Criteria

- At least 1 of 2 bombs impacts within 25 m of T-1.
- Strafing rounds observed on or within 10 m of T-1.
- No safety violations (minimum altitude/frag floors).
- Correct **"In"** / **"Off"** brevity calls transmitted on all passes.

## Scenario 2 — PRECISION STRIKE 02

> **Phase 2: Intermediate Surface Attack — TGP/Shkval, Precision Guided Munitions**

### Location: Range 64A — East of Groom Lake

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 37°18'00" W 115°40'00" \| MGRS: 11S PV 25 35 |
| **Elevation** | ~4,500 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Groom Lake (KXTA) — ~10 NM West; or **air-start** recommended |

> ⚠️ **Note:** Groom Lake airfield is present in the NTTR map but restricted. Students may depart Creech AFB (~60 NM transit) or use an **air-start** at Angels 15 over the range. Air-start is recommended for this scenario to preserve sortie time for weapons work.

### Navigation to Range 64A

1. **Air-start:** Spawn at N 37°18'00" W 115°40'00", Angels 15, Hdg 090°.
2. *Alternate (from Creech):* Depart North, follow US-93 North-East. ~60 NM transit.
3. **TACAN:** Track GFK (Groom Lake) 090 Radial at 12 NM DME.
4. **Visual:** The flat, featureless dry lake beds east of the main Groom Lake facility.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1 BUNKER** | Hardened command bunker (static) | LGB/JDAM target |
| **T-2 ARMOUR** | T-72 MBT ×3 (active, weapons hold) | Maverick / Vikhr target |
| **T-3 SOFT** | Truck convoy ×4 (Ural-375) | Secondary |

Targets are spaced ~0.5 NM apart to allow individual sensor designation without gates interfering.

### Student Objectives

**A-10C / A-10C II:**
1. TGP Point Track on T-1 BUNKER. Confirm SPI. Employ one (1) GBU-12 (LGB) from level flight at Angels 12+. Transmit **"Pickle"**.
2. TGP designate T-2 ARMOUR. Slave AGM-65 seeker to SPI. Engage one (1) T-72. Transmit **"Rifle"**.
3. Conduct one (1) CCIP dive attack with Mk-82 on T-3 SOFT.

**Su-25T:**
1. Activate Shkval. Lock T-2 ARMOUR. Employ Vikhr (AT-16) against one (1) T-72. Maintain steady flight until impact. Transmit **"Rifle"**.
2. Employ Kh-29T (TV-guided) against T-1 BUNKER from level flight. Transmit **"Rifle"**.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Dawn or dusk (transition lighting) |
| **Weather** | Scattered cloud at 8,000 ft; 15 km visibility |
| **Threat** | Medium — AI IR MANPADS active in target area |
| **Ordnance (A-10C)** | 1× GBU-12, 2× AGM-65D, 2× Mk-82 |
| **Ordnance (Su-25T)** | 2× Vikhr, 1× Kh-29T |

### Success Criteria

- LGB/Kh-29T: Direct hit or within 5 m CEP on T-1 BUNKER.
- Maverick/Vikhr: T-72 killed or mission-killed.
- No break in Vikhr laser beam during flight (Su-25T only).
- Correct brevity codes used for all releases.
- Egress above minimum recovery altitude (2,000 ft AGL).

## Scenario 3 — CLOSE FIGHT 03

> **Phase 3: CAS & JTAC Coordination — 9-Line, Keyhole CAS, Abort**

### Location: Goldfield

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 37°42'30" W 117°14'00" \| MGRS: 11S MV 79 73 |
| **Elevation** | ~5,700 ft MSL |
| **Airfield** | Tonopah Test Range (KTNX) — ~20 NM South-East |

### Navigation to Goldfield

1. **Depart** Tonopah (KTNX) RWY 32.
2. **Turn North-West** onto Hdg 315°. Follow US-95 North-West.
3. **TACAN:** Track TPH (Tonopah) 310 Radial at 22 NM DME.
4. **Visual:** A distinct small town grid in a valley. The highway (US-95) runs through it — highly visible. Climb to hold altitude (Angels 18) before reaching the town boundary.

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Grid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ALPHA** | Keyhole Hold — North Anchor | N 37°46'00" W 117°14'00" |
| **BRAVO** | Keyhole Hold — East Anchor | N 37°42'30" W 117°08'00" |
| **T-1 VEHICLE** | UAZ-469 technical ×2 in street | N 37°42'30" W 117°14'00" |
| **T-2 INFANTRY** | Insurgent squad near crossroads | N 37°42'00" W 117°14'30" |
| **JTAC** | Humvee, "OP ALPHA" on hill north of town | N 37°44'00" W 117°14'00" |
| **FRIENDLIES** | Blue Force, 400 m north of T-1 | N 37°43'30" W 117°14'00" |

> ⚠️ **ROE:** No ordnance within 200 m of FRIENDLIES. No ordnance on the main highway (US-95). Neutral civilian vehicles present on the highway — positive ID mandatory.

### Keyhole Geometry

```
              [ALPHA]
              N Hold
            (Angels 18)
           /            \
          /              \
[DELTA] ---   GOLDFIELD  --- [BRAVO]
W Hold    [T-1] [T-2]        E Hold
(Angels 18) [JTAC ON HILL]  (Angels 18)
          \              /
           \            /
              [CHARLIE]
              S Hold
            (Angels 18)
```

### 9-Line Brief (Pre-Scripted for Instructor)

Use the following 9-Line to brief the student before the attack:

| Line | Element | Value |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | IP/BP | "IP: HIGHWAY JUNCTION" |
| 2 | Heading | "Heading 200" |
| 3 | Distance | "3.5 NM" |
| 4 | Elevation *(read back)* | "Elevation 5,700 ft" |
| 5 | Description | "Two technicals in the street" |
| 6 | Coordinates *(read back)* | "11S MV 790 730" |
| 7 | Mark | "Smoke, colour red" |
| 8 | Friendlies | "Friendlies 400 m north, blue smoke" |
| 9 | Egress | "Egress east" |
| + | Restrictions *(read back)* | "No ordnance within 200 m of blue smoke" |

### Student Objectives

1. Depart Tonopah, navigate to Hold ALPHA.
2. Transmit CAS Check-In (MNPOPCA) to JTAC on freq 138.150.
3. Receive and read back 9-Line. Await JTAC confirmation.
4. Execute one (1) Cleared Hot attack pass on T-1 VEHICLE from ALPHA.
5. When JTAC triggers the abort scenario on the second pass — execute **immediate abort**: pull up, egress east, transmit **"[Callsign] Abort"**.
6. Transmit BDA after cleared-hot pass. Return to Tonopah.

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Midday |
| **Weather** | Scattered cloud, 5,000 ft ceiling, 10 km visibility |
| **Threat** | Medium-High — MANPADS and ZSU-23 active |
| **JTAC** | AI JTAC (scripted triggers) at OP ALPHA |

### Success Criteria

- MNPOPCA check-in complete, all 7 elements present.
- 9-Line read-back: Lines 4, 6, and restrictions verbatim.
- Cleared Hot attack: weapon employment within attack corridor. No ordnance within 200 m of FRIENDLIES.
- Abort executed cleanly — no release after abort call.
- BDA transmitted within 30 seconds of egress.

## Scenario 4 — IRON CONTROL 04

> **Phase 4: Airborne FAC-A — Stack Management, Marking, Talk-On**

### Location: Tolicha Peak Electronic Combat Range (ECR)

| Item | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinates** | N 37°17'00" W 116°47'00" \| MGRS: 11S NV 22 28 |
| **Elevation** | ~4,000 – 6,000 ft MSL (varied terrain) |
| **Airfield** | Tonopah Test Range (KTNX) — ~30 NM North |

### Navigation to Tolicha Peak

1. **Depart** Tonopah (KTNX) RWY 14.
2. **Turn South-East** onto Hdg 135°.
3. **TACAN:** Track BTY (Beatty) 340 Radial at 18 NM DME.
4. **Visual:** Distinctive rugged peaks with visible radar domes (ECR emitters) on the summits. The target valley is visible as a lower depression between the peaks.
5. **Climb** to FAC-A working altitude (Angels 14) before entering the operating area.

### Threat Environment

| Unit | Type | Status | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SA-6 GAINFUL** | Radar SAM | Weapons Hold (training) | Valley Floor |
| **ZSU-23-4 SHILKA** | Radar AAA | Active (suppressed) | North Ridge |

> ⚠️ **The SA-6 is in Weapons Hold but represents a live threat axis. The student FAC-A must deconflict attack headings to avoid placing attacking aircraft in the SAM's engagement envelope.**

### Multi-Ship Stack (pre-spawned AI)

| Callsign | Aircraft | Altitude | Anchor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **COLT 1-1** | A-10C (Student FAC-A) | Angels 14 (Working) | Orbit over ECR |
| **TUSK 1-1 / 1-2** | A-10C ×2 (AI) | Angels 18 | Hold ALPHA North |
| **HAWG 2-1 / 2-2** | A-10A ×2 (AI) | Angels 20 | Hold BRAVO East |

### Target Set

| Designator | Description | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-1 COLUMN** | T-72 ×3 convoy moving on valley road | Primary FAC-A target |
| **T-2 RADAR** | P-19 Flat Face EWR (static) | Precision mark target |
| **T-3 TROOPS** | Infantry squad in defilade | Talk-On target (no mark available) |

### Wedding Cake Altitudes

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TOP LAYER:    Angels 20+                   │  HAWG 2-1/2-2 (Transit/Fuel)
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  MIDDLE LAYER: Angels 15–19                 │  TUSK 1-1/1-2 (CAS Stack Hold)
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LOW LAYER:    Angels 14                    │  COLT 1-1 (FAC-A Working)
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
               Below Angels 12:  Cleared Hot attack zone
               Below Angels 5:   Tolicha Peak terrain / AAA envelope
```

### Student Objectives (as FAC-A COLT 1-1)

1. **Check-In** both flights (TUSK and HAWG). Receive MNPOPCA. Assign Keyhole anchors and hold altitudes.
2. **Brief** TUSK 1-1 with a complete 9-Line for T-1 COLUMN. Await read-back confirmation.
3. **WP Mark:** Fire WP rocket at T-1 COLUMN. Transmit *"Mark is on deck."* Control TUSK 1-1 to Cleared Hot.
4. **Sparkle Mark (night phase):** Illuminate T-2 RADAR with IR designator. Confirm *"Contact Sparkle"* from HAWG 2-1 before issuing Cleared Hot.
5. **Talk-On (no marks):** Guide HAWG 2-2 onto T-3 TROOPS using the 4-step funnel (Anchor → Funnel → Reference → Target) without any mark. Student must achieve attacker Tally within three steps.
6. **Stack Management:** Maintain altitude deconfliction throughout — no conflicts. Sequence TUSK and HAWG so only one flight is in the attack corridor at a time.
7. **BDA:** Receive and record BDA from all attacking flights.

### Suggested Talk-On Script (T-3 TROOPS)

*Use only if student requires coaching:*

1. *"Reference Tolicha Peak — the highest summit at your 12 o'clock."*
2. *"From the peak, come down the east ridge to the dry creek bed at the base."*
3. *"Follow the creek bed south 0.5 NM to the road junction."*
4. *"Target is infantry dug in on the south bank of the creek, 100 m west of the junction."*

### Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | Night (NVG environment) |
| **Weather** | Clear, unlimited visibility, winds calm |
| **Threat** | Medium — SA-6 (weapons hold), ZSU-23 active |
| **FAC-A Platform** | A-10C (TGP for sparkle / mark) |

### Success Criteria

- All check-ins received; anchors correctly assigned.
- 9-Lines complete and read-back confirmed for both flights.
- WP mark placed within 100 m of T-1. Attack Cleared Hot with correct attacker orientation.
- Sparkle confirmed Contact before Cleared Hot (no premature clearance).
- Talk-On: attacker achieves Tally within three description steps.
- Zero altitude conflicts in stack throughout.
- BDA received from all attacking flights.
