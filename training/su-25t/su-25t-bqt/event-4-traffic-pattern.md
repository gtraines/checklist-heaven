## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Fly a consistent rectangular traffic pattern without significant altitude or speed deviations
- [ ] Execute three touch-and-go landings with proper drag chute and go-around technique
- [ ] Perform one no-flap (emergency) approach and landing
- [ ] Demonstrate drag chute deployment timing and progressive braking technique
- [ ] Execute a go-around from any point on the approach if directed or if unstabilized

## Ground Brief

### Why Pattern Work?

Pattern work is repetitive by design. The student flies multiple circuits to:
1. Build muscle memory for speed and configuration management
2. Learn to judge distances, altitudes, and sink rates visually
3. Develop anticipatory power management (critical due to engine spool-up lag)
4. Practice the landing flare and drag chute sequence until it is automatic

### The Spool-Up Threat on Final

Reiterate: the R-195 takes **9–12 seconds** to respond to a throttle advance. On a 3° glidepath at 260 km/h, the aircraft descends approximately **120–150 m** during that spool-up window. This means:

- If you are below glidepath and add power late, you will impact the ground before the engine responds
- The correct technique is to **scan your glidepath 15 seconds ahead** and make small, anticipatory corrections
- A go-around decision must be made **before** you are low and slow — not after

### Configuration Summary

| Phase | Flaps | Gear | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upwind (climb-out) | UP (after 300 km/h / 150 m AGL) | UP (positive rate) | 400 km/h climb |
| Crosswind | — | — | 400 km/h |
| Downwind | LANDING | DOWN (abeam threshold) | 400 → 350 km/h |
| Base | LANDING | DOWN | 300–320 km/h |
| Final | LANDING | DOWN (3 green) | 260 km/h (light) |
| Touchdown | — | — | ~240 km/h |

## Traffic Pattern Geometry

```
                          CROSSWIND LEG
                   ← → ← → ← → ← → ← →
                  ↑                      ↓
           UPWIND ↑                      ↓ DOWNWIND
          (T/O →) ↑                      ↓ (← 400 km/h)
                  ↑                      ↓
  RUNWAY   ===== ↑          ← BASE ← ←  ↓
  THRESHOLD  ↑                            ↓ FINAL
             ↑← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ↓
```

### Pattern Altitudes

| Leg | Altitude AGL |
|---|---|
| Downwind | 600 m |
| Base | 400 m |
| Final (runway threshold) | 60–80 m |
| Decision height (go-around or land) | 30 m |

### Pattern Distances
- **Downwind offset from runway centerline:** ~2–3 km
- **Base turn point:** When runway threshold is approximately 45° behind the wingtip
- **Final length:** 5–8 km for a stable approach

## Pattern Procedures — Normal Configuration

### Upwind (After Takeoff)
- [ ] Positive rate — **Gear UP** (`G`)
- [ ] > 300 km/h and > 150 m AGL — **Flaps UP** (`F`)
- [ ] Climb to pattern altitude: **600 m AGL**
- [ ] Trim for climb speed: 400 km/h

### Crosswind Turn
- [ ] Turn 90° perpendicular to runway heading
- [ ] Continue climb to 600 m AGL
- [ ] Brief leg — turn downwind when clear of runway extended centerline

### Downwind
- [ ] Parallel to runway, offset ~2–3 km, altitude **600 m AGL ± 30 m**
- [ ] Speed: reduce to **400 km/h**, then trim
- [ ] Abeam runway threshold:
  - [ ] **Flaps** — LANDING position (`LShift + F`)
  - [ ] **Gear** — DOWN (`G`) — verify three green lights
  - [ ] Speed: 350–400 km/h

### Base Turn
- [ ] Turn base when threshold is ~45° behind the wing
- [ ] Reduce power — descend to **400 m AGL**
- [ ] Speed: **300–320 km/h**
- [ ] Trim for approach

### Final Approach
- [ ] Turn final — align with runway extended centerline
- [ ] Target speed: **260 km/h** (light fuel)
- [ ] Target glidepath: **3–4°** visual slope (threshold at base of windscreen)
- [ ] **Must be stabilized** by 500 m from threshold — if not, execute go-around

### Stabilized Approach Check (500 m from threshold)
- [ ] Speed: 260 ± 10 km/h
- [ ] Descent rate: constant (not accelerating downward)
- [ ] Centerline: aligned ± 5 m
- [ ] Gear: 3 green
- [ ] If any criterion is NOT met → **GO-AROUND** (see below)

### Landing Flare
- [ ] At **20–30 m AGL** — slight back pressure to check descent rate
- [ ] Do not pull aggressively — a slight pitch increase is all that is needed
- [ ] Touchdown: **~240 km/h**, on centerline, firm but not hard

## Touch-and-Go Landings

For the first 3 circuits, execute touch-and-go landings.

### Touch-and-Go Sequence
After touchdown:
1. - [ ] **Drag Chute** — DEPLOY (`P`) — immediately on touchdown
2. - [ ] **Full Power** — ADVANCE both throttles to maximum simultaneously with chute deployment
3. - [ ] **After acceleration:** at > 200 km/h, **release chute** (`P` again to jettison if equipped, or chute releases on its own as lift is generated on the next takeoff roll)
4. - [ ] Continue takeoff roll — rotate at 210 km/h
5. - [ ] Fly upwind, re-enter pattern

**Instructor Note:** The touch-and-go sequence tests a critical skill: the student must commit to both landing and immediate takeoff in quick succession. Common failure: the student deploys the chute and then hesitates on adding power, allowing the aircraft to slow too much.

### Go-Around Procedure
Execute a go-around if the approach is unstabilized, directed by instructor, or if decision criteria are not met at 30 m AGL.

1. - [ ] **Full Power** — ADVANCE both throttles immediately
2. - [ ] Maintain runway heading — do **not** bank until above 100 m AGL
3. - [ ] **Gear UP** — at positive rate
4. - [ ] **Flaps** — remain in landing position until ≥ 300 m AGL and > 300 km/h, then retract
5. - [ ] Climb to pattern altitude and re-enter the pattern at crosswind

> **Warning:** Do not attempt a go-around below 30 m AGL if at or below approach speed with idle power. Engine spool-up lag may not permit sufficient thrust before runway contact. Land and stop.

## Pattern Procedures — Emergency Configuration

**Circuit 4 is flown no-flap (simulated flap failure).**

### No-Flap Approach Differences

| Parameter | Normal | No-Flap |
|---|---|---|
| Final approach speed | 260 km/h | **290–300 km/h** |
| Glidepath angle | 3–4° | Shallower — extends landing distance |
| Flare | Slight check | More pronounced flare to arrest high sink rate |
| Landing rollout | Normal | **Longer** — plan for full runway use |

### No-Flap Procedure
- [ ] Downwind: **Gear DOWN only** — do not extend flaps
- [ ] Maintain extra speed throughout — 300 km/h on base
- [ ] Final approach: **290–300 km/h**
- [ ] Landing: higher sink rate — more deliberate flare
- [ ] **Drag chute critical** — higher touchdown speed makes chute essential
- [ ] Progressive braking — allow more runway distance

**Instructor Note:** The most common error in a no-flap approach is flying the same speed as a flapped approach. Without flap lift, 260 km/h is too slow — the aircraft will sink through the flare.

## Full-Stop Landing Technique

The final circuit is a full stop. Focus on perfecting drag chute and braking technique.

### Drag Chute Timing
- [ ] Deploy **immediately** on main gear touchdown — `P` key
- [ ] Chute inflates within 1–2 seconds and immediately arrests forward acceleration
- [ ] Do **not** wait to see if you need it — always deploy on landing

### Braking Sequence
| Speed | Action |
|---|---|
| Touchdown (~240 km/h) | Chute deployed; no wheel brakes |
| 200 km/h | Begin **light, progressive** brake application |
| 150 km/h | Normal braking — smooth and steady |
| < 100 km/h | Heavy braking acceptable; full stop |

> **Tire Blowout Conditions:** Full brake application above 200 km/h **will blow tires**. This closes the runway for subsequent operations. Technique is everything.

### De-Crab Technique (Crosswind)
If landing in a crosswind (circuit 4 if crosswind is set):
- [ ] Maintain crab angle on final (turn into wind to track centerline)
- [ ] At 3–5 m AGL — **de-crab** with rudder to align aircraft with runway
- [ ] Touch down wings-level and aligned
- [ ] Any significant side-load on touchdown **will collapse the gear** in DCS

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Pattern altitude | Downwind ± 50 m of 600 m AGL |
| Pattern speed (downwind) | 380–420 km/h |
| Final approach stability | Stabilized by ≥ 500 m from threshold |
| Final approach speed | 255–270 km/h (normal); 285–305 km/h (no-flap) |
| Touchdown point | Within first 500 m of runway |
| Drag chute | Deployed on every landing, within 3 sec of touchdown |
| Tire integrity | No blown tires on any circuit |
| Go-around (if executed) | Initiated correctly; no runway contact after go-around decision |
| No-flap landing | Completed without gear collapse or runway excursion |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| High and fast on final | Begin descent earlier; don't wait until turning final |
| Low and slow on final | Anticipate early; add power 15 sec before you need it |
| Failing to deploy chute on touchdown | Build the habit: landing = chute, every time |
| Braking above 200 km/h | Wait for chute to do the work — feel the deceleration first |
| Crabbed touchdown (gear collapse) | De-crab must be complete before main gear contact |
| Rushed touch-and-go takeoff | Confirm positive rate before gear retraction — even in the pattern |
