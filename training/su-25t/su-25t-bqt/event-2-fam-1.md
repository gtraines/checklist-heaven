## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Complete a cold-start engine start without exceeding EGT limits or skipping steps
- [ ] Taxi to and from the runway using nose wheel steering without departing the taxiway
- [ ] Execute a normal takeoff and achieve a clean aircraft configuration by 500 m AGL
- [ ] Perform basic airwork: 30° and 45° banked turns, 500 ft/min climbs and descents
- [ ] Fly a rectangular traffic pattern and execute one full-stop landing

---

## Ground Brief

### Mission Profile
This is a dual-phase flight:
1. **Phase 1 (Ground):** Cold start and taxi
2. **Phase 2 (Air):** Takeoff, basic airwork at medium altitude, one traffic pattern, full-stop landing

### Key Numbers for This Flight

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Fuel Load | 50% (training load) |
| Stores | Clean (no weapons) |
| Vr (Rotation) | 210 km/h (clean/light) |
| Vlof (Liftoff) | ~240 km/h |
| Cruise Speed | 500–550 km/h |
| Pattern Speed (Downwind) | 400 km/h |
| Final Approach Speed | 260 km/h (light) |
| Touchdown Speed | ~240 km/h |

### Engine Spool-Up Reminder
Before takeoff, demonstrate the spool-up lag on the ramp: advance both throttles to maximum and count seconds until RPM stabilizes. This should take **9–12 seconds**. This is the most important number you will carry into every landing.

## Part 1 — Cold Start & Cockpit Checks

### Step 1: Electrical Power
- [ ] **Battery** — ON (`RShift + L`)
- [ ] Confirm EKRAN panel illuminates and performs self-test
- [ ] Allow EKRAN warnings to clear (normal: battery cautions extinguish within 30 sec)

### Step 2: Canopy
- [ ] **Canopy** — CLOSE AND LOCK (`LCtrl + C`)
- [ ] Verify closed visually

### Step 3: Left Engine Start
- [ ] **Left Engine Start** — INITIATE (`RAlt + Home`)
- [ ] Monitor N1 RPM — must reach > 30% before throttle advance
- [ ] Verify **no EGT overtemp** (no red warning on EKRAN)
- [ ] Left engine settles at idle: **~36–40% N1 / ~60–65% N2**

### Step 4: Right Engine Start
- [ ] **Right Engine Start** — INITIATE (`RCtrl + Home`)
- [ ] Monitor N1 RPM — same procedure as left engine
- [ ] Verify right engine at idle parameters
- [ ] Confirm all EKRAN cautions clear

### Step 5: Post-Start Checks
- [ ] **Hydraulics** — check caution lights extinguished
- [ ] **Flaps** — set to Takeoff (`LShift + F`) — verify position
- [ ] **Speedbrakes** — IN (`LCtrl + B` to toggle if deployed)
- [ ] **Trim** — center pitch, roll, and rudder trim
- [ ] **Shkval / Avionics** — powered (warm-up may be indicated; wait if required)
- [ ] **Canopy** — locked (re-confirm)

**Instructor Note:** EKRAN is your first and most important scan after power-on. If a caution light does not clear, identify it before taxiing. Never taxi with an unresolved warning.

## Part 2 — Taxi

### Taxi Procedures
- [ ] **Nose Wheel Steering (NWS)** — ENGAGE (`/` numpad, or NWS button)
- [ ] Verify NWS engaged — light on in cockpit
- [ ] **Brakes** — RELEASE (`W` or rudder pedal brake axis)
- [ ] Add **small throttle input** to begin rolling (~15–20% N1 may be needed)
- [ ] Maintain **max 20–30 km/h** on straight taxiways
- [ ] Reduce to **< 10 km/h** before turns

### Nose Wheel Steering Technique
The Su-25T NWS is notoriously sensitive. **Tap** rudder inputs — do not hold. At low taxi speed, small inputs produce large turns.

> **Technique:** Use `Z` (left rudder) and `X` (right rudder) as brief taps. If using keyboard, a single keypress is usually sufficient for a gentle turn. Hold only for sharper turns on narrow taxiways.

- [ ] **Brake Check** — apply brakes at slow speed, verify both main gear braking
- [ ] Taxi to runway hold-short line

### Run-Up (Hold Short)
- [ ] **Throttles** — advance to ~70% N1 briefly, verify RPM responsive
- [ ] Return throttles to IDLE
- [ ] **Trim** — confirm pitch trim neutral to slight nose-up
- [ ] **Flaps** — confirm TAKEOFF position
- [ ] Confirm runway clear before proceeding

## Part 3 — Takeoff & Departure

### Takeoff Roll
- [ ] **Line up** on runway centerline
- [ ] **Throttles** — ADVANCE TO MAXIMUM (`PgUp` or throttle axis to 100%)
- [ ] Maintain centerline with **rudder** (NWS disengages above ~50 km/h — use rudder only)
- [ ] Monitor airspeed — call "70" mentally at 70 km/h, start positive control crosscheck

### Rotation
- [ ] At **210 km/h** — smooth back pressure, rotate to **10–12° pitch attitude**
- [ ] Do **not** hold the nose high — a gentle rotation is all that is required
- [ ] Aircraft will fly off at ~230–240 km/h

> **Warning:** Rotating too aggressively ("The Frog Leap") will cause the aircraft to porpoise. Smooth and deliberate inputs only.

### Clean-Up
- [ ] **Positive rate of climb** — confirm on VSI
- [ ] **Gear UP** (`G`) — at positive rate, speed < 400 km/h
- [ ] **Flaps UP** (`F`) — at > 300 km/h and > 150 m AGL
- [ ] **Trim** — adjust pitch trim as flaps retract (nose will pitch with flap change)

### Departure
- [ ] Climb at **400–450 km/h** to assigned altitude (5,000 m recommended for airwork)
- [ ] Trim continuously as speed increases
- [ ] Confirm clean aircraft configuration before leveling off

## Part 4 — Basic Airwork

Conduct the following maneuvers at **5,000 m MSL** with adequate recovery altitude.

### Exercise A: Level Turns
Perform three turns in each direction (left and right).

| Maneuver | Standard |
|---|---|
| 30° bank turn | Maintain altitude ± 100 m, coordinated ball |
| 45° bank turn | Maintain altitude ± 150 m, coordinated ball |
| 60° bank turn (demo) | Observe increased back pressure requirement |

**Technique:** Use rudder to keep the slip/skid ball centered. Unlike the A-10, the Su-25T requires active rudder coordination. As bank increases, add back pressure and appropriate rudder.

### Exercise B: Climbs and Descents
- [ ] **500 ft/min climb** — trim to maintain, note required pitch/power change
- [ ] **500 ft/min descent** — power reduction, trim forward, maintain 450–500 km/h
- [ ] **Level off** — anticipate level-off altitude by 100 m; trim to cruise

### Exercise C: Trim Demonstration
- [ ] Accelerate from 400 to 600 km/h — trim required (nose heavy)
- [ ] Decelerate from 600 to 300 km/h — trim required (nose light)
- [ ] Note: every significant speed change requires trim input

**Instructor Note:** If a student is using constant stick pressure to maintain attitude, they are not trimming. A properly trimmed aircraft requires near-zero stick force in cruise.

## Part 5 — Return & Landing

### Descent & Approach Setup
- [ ] Descend to **1,500 m AGL** within 15–20 km of the field
- [ ] Reduce speed to **400 km/h** on downwind
- [ ] Fly rectangular pattern: **downwind, base, final**

### Downwind Configuration
- [ ] Abeam runway threshold — reduce throttle, maintain 400 km/h
- [ ] **Flaps** — LANDING (`LShift + F` second notch, if equipped)
- [ ] **Gear DOWN** (`G`) at < 400 km/h — confirm three green lights
- [ ] Trim for approach: slight nose-up

### Base Leg
- [ ] Turn base at appropriate point (when runway is ~45° behind the wing)
- [ ] Reduce to **300 km/h**
- [ ] Begin descent

### Final Approach
- [ ] Turn final — wings level
- [ ] Target speed: **260 km/h** (light aircraft)
- [ ] Stabilize: constant speed, constant descent rate (≈ 3–4° glidepath)
- [ ] **Do not allow speed to decay below 250 km/h on final** — power correction lag means recovery may not be possible

### Landing Flare & Touchdown
- [ ] At 20–30 m AGL — **slight** back pressure to check descent rate (do not over-flare)
- [ ] **Touchdown** at approximately **240 km/h**
- [ ] Maintain directional control with rudder and differential brakes

### Post-Touchdown
- [ ] **Drag Chute** — DEPLOY immediately on touchdown (`P`)
- [ ] Allow speed to decrease to < 200 km/h before applying wheel brakes
- [ ] **Do not slam brakes** — gradual application prevents tire blowout
- [ ] Maintain centerline to full stop

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Engine start | No EGT overtemp; all warnings clear before taxi |
| Taxi | Remain on taxiway; no runway incursion; taxi speed ≤ 30 km/h |
| Rotation | Within ± 15 km/h of Vr; no runway excursion |
| Clean-up | Gear and flaps retracted within 30 sec of positive rate |
| Airwork turns | ± 150 m altitude in 30° and 45° turns |
| Approach | Stabilized on final ≥ 300 m from threshold |
| Touchdown | Speed 230–260 km/h; on runway centerline ± 5 m |
| Braking | No tire blowout; stopped within runway length |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Departing taxiway during NWS turns | Use brief tap inputs; reduce taxi speed before turns |
| Rotating too aggressively | Smooth rotation to 10–12° — no higher |
| Gear/flaps not retracted on schedule | Build the habit: "positive rate, gear up; 300 km/h, flaps up" |
| Slow on final with no correction | Due to engine lag — add power **before** speed drops, not after |
| Over-flaring and hard landing | Slight check of descent rate only — do not pull nose aggressively |
| Slamming wheel brakes | Always deploy chute first; brake only after < 200 km/h |
