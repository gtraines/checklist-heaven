## Check Ride Overview

The check ride is the final evaluation for the OH-58D Basic Qualification Training course. It is a **pass/fail evaluation** conducted by the evaluating instructor. The student will demonstrate all skills learned in Events 1–7 without coaching or prompting.

**Check Ride Philosophy:** The evaluator is looking for a student who is **safe and proficient** — not perfect. Minor deviations that are recognized and corrected are not failing items. Unsafe acts, failure to recognize hazards, incorrect boldface, or gross procedural deviations are.

**Duration:** 1.5–2.5 hours total (oral + flight)

> **Evaluator is NOT a safety hazard.** The evaluator will intervene if an unsafe situation develops. However, interventions count against the student's grade as "Unsafe Act" or "Incomplete Item" depending on severity.

---

## Prerequisites for Check Ride

The student may not proceed to the check ride with any of the following outstanding:

- [ ] Any REPEAT grade outstanding from Events 1–7
- [ ] More than 3 unresolved Incomplete Items (IIs)
- [ ] Incomplete boldface (must be verified by the instructor who signed off Event 7)
- [ ] Less than one full calendar day since the last training event (review period required)

**Evaluator Discretion:** If the evaluator observes evidence of poor preparation during the oral exam (inability to state limitations, incorrect boldface), the check ride may be terminated at the oral phase. A ground evaluation "Down" may be issued.

## Oral Examination

The oral exam is conducted before engine start. It is not open-book. The student must answer from memory.

### Section 1 — Boldface Recall

All five boldface procedures must be recited verbatim. **Any error in boldface = check ride terminated (Oral Down).**

| # | Emergency | First Step (trigger word) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Engine Failure | Collective |
| 2 | Engine Fire in Flight | Throttle |
| 3 | Settling with Power | Cyclic |
| 4 | LTE Recovery | Pedal |
| 5 | Hydraulic Failure | Expect |

### Section 2 — Operating Limitations

The evaluator will ask any combination of the following without warning:

| Question Category | Example Questions |
|---|---|
| **Nr limits** | What is the normal power-on Nr range? Autorotation Nr range? Nr overspeed limit? |
| **TOT limits** | TOT start abort limit? TOT continuous limit? TOT transient limit? |
| **Torque limits** | Continuous? Transient (duration)? |
| **Vne** | Vne with doors on? Doors off? |
| **Weight** | Maximum gross weight? |
| **G-limits** | Maximum positive G? Maximum negative G? |
| **Slope limits** | Side slope limit? Nose-down limit? |
| **Autorotation speeds** | Best autorotation speed (min RoD)? Best glide distance speed? |
| **ETL** | At what airspeed does ETL develop? |

### Section 3 — Systems Knowledge

| Question | Answer Expected |
|---|---|
| "The FADEC fails and you're in manual mode. You increase collective. What do you do?" | Add throttle to maintain Nr at 100%; monitor TOT |
| "You lose the hydraulic system in cruise. Walk me through your response." | Expect high forces; airspeed 40–60 KIAS; plan run-on landing; avoid hover; land ASAP |
| "What is the single most important action when the engine fails?" | Collective down — immediately. Within 1–2 seconds. |
| "Define Settling with Power. What are the three conditions?" | Vertical flight with power applied; > 300 FPM descent; < 30 KIAS; 20–100% torque |
| "What is LTE? What conditions make it more likely?" | Tail rotor loses authority; low airspeed, high power, tailwind or specific crosswind quadrant |
| "What is mast bumping and what causes it?" | Rotor hub contacts mast; low-G/pushover/aggressive lateral cyclic at low airspeed; potentially fatal |
| "Describe the upslope skid first technique for slope landing. Why?" | Upslope first reduces rollover moment; reduces the G-load on the mast if skid contact; prevents dynamic rollover |
| "You are on a steep approach and the aircraft starts vibrating and sinking despite adding collective. What is happening?" | Settling with Power — excessive rate of descent + power below 30 KIAS = vortex ring state; forward cyclic immediately |

### Section 4 — Aerodynamics

| Topic | Student Must Explain |
|---|---|
| **ETL** | What it is; airspeed range; aircraft behavior; pilot response |
| **Translating tendency** | Direction of drift; cause; compensation |
| **Retreating blade stall** | Cause; symptoms; Vne relationship |
| **Dynamic rollover** | Cause; at what point it becomes unrecoverable; prevention |

## Flight Profile

The evaluator will direct the student through the following profile in the order and manner the evaluator chooses. Not all items need to be performed in every check ride — the evaluator selects a representative sample. Items marked **[REQUIRED]** must be evaluated in every check ride.

### Startup [REQUIRED]

- Full cold-dark startup without coaching
- TOT monitored; no exceedance
- AHRS/GPS alignment completed before hover
- Rotor engaged correctly

**Evaluator Notes:** The evaluator will call out any missed switches at the end of each phase. Missed battery switch before starter attempt = Incomplete Item. Hot start (TOT approaching 843°C without student action) = Down.

### Pre-Takeoff Checks [REQUIRED]

- All checks completed without prompting
- Controls checked
- AHRS/GPS aligned; heading stable

### Hover & Hover Taxi [REQUIRED]

- Stable 3–5 ft hover within ±2 ft altitude; ±3 ft drift
- Hover taxi to a designated point at < 20 kts; 3–5 ft AGL
- Set down and hover pick-up without instructor intervention

### Normal Takeoff [REQUIRED]

- ETL managed (no unexpected pitch excursion)
- Climb established at 55–70 KIAS
- Departure turn (if directed) coordinated; ball centered

### Basic Airwork

Evaluator selects 2–3 of the following:

| Maneuver | Standard |
|---|---|
| Level flight at 80 KIAS | ±100 ft altitude; ±5 KIAS |
| 30° banked turn | Ball centered; ±100 ft altitude |
| Climb at Vy (60 KIAS) | ±5 KIAS; positive rate of climb; no roll or yaw excursion |
| Descent at 70 KIAS | ±5 KIAS; controlled rate |
| Slow flight at 30–40 KIAS | ±100 ft altitude; no LTE setup; no SWP |

### Navigation [if directed]

- Navigate to an evaluator-designated point using GPS/MFD
- Heading within ±5° of track; altitude ±100 ft
- Land area identified on MFD before visual acquisition

### Traffic Pattern and Approaches [REQUIRED]

Evaluator directs 2–3 pattern laps with specified approach type.

| Approach | Standard |
|---|---|
| Normal (1 rep minimum) | 8–12°; terminate within 15 ft of point |
| Steep (1 rep) | 14–20°; no SWP onset |
| Go-around (1 rep — initiated by evaluator) | Immediate execution; through ETL; climb established |

### Emergency Procedures [REQUIRED]

Evaluator will direct simulated emergencies from the following:

| Emergency | Method | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Engine failure** (2 reps minimum) | Evaluator command | Collective down < 2 sec; Nr 90–107%; airspeed 60–80 KIAS |
| **LTE** (verbal scenario) | Evaluator describes scenario | Student recites recovery action correctly |
| **SWP** (verbal scenario or simulated) | Evaluator describes or sets up conditions | Student recognizes and recovers / describes recovery |
| **One simulated emergency of evaluator's choice** | Any of the 5 boldface items | Correct procedural response |

### Autorotation to Landing [REQUIRED]

One full autorotation from 800–1,000 ft AGL to actual touchdown.

| Phase | Standard |
|---|---|
| Entry | Collective down < 2 sec; Nr in range |
| Steady state | 60–80 KIAS; Nr 90–107% |
| Flare | 150–200 ft AGL |
| Touchdown | Survivable; within 50 ft of area; no post-landing ground resonance |

### Shutdown [REQUIRED]

- Complete shutdown without prompting
- Cooldown completed before throttle OFF
- All switches OFF in correct sequence

## Grading Standards

### Pass Criteria

The student receives a **PASS** grade when:
- All [REQUIRED] items are completed to standard
- No more than two Incomplete Items (IIs) are issued during the check ride
- No Unsafe Acts are observed
- All 5 boldface procedures were recited correctly in the oral

### Incomplete Item (II) Criteria

An II is issued when one specific standard is not met but the overall safety and competence of the sortie is not in question.

| II Examples |
|---|
| Missed one checklist item during startup (evaluator had to prompt) |
| Altitude deviation > 100 ft during level flight (recognized and corrected within 15 sec) |
| Autorotation termination > 50 ft but < 100 ft from the designated area |
| Approach termination > 15 ft but < 30 ft from landing point |
| AHRS/GPS: hovered before full alignment complete |

### Down (Failure) Criteria

A **DOWN** is issued when:

| Down Criteria |
|---|
| Any boldface recited incorrectly or out of order (oral phase) |
| Hot start without corrective action (TOT approaching 843°C; student does not react) |
| Engine failure — collective delay > 4 seconds |
| Nr below 80% during autorotation entry or steady state |
| Nr above 115% during any phase |
| Autorotation to landing — hard landing, rollover, or tail strike |
| LTE onset — uncontrolled yaw without recovery attempt |
| SWP — student adds collective as first response (worsens SWP) |
| Approach below 50 ft AGL at > 40 KIAS with no go-around |
| Dynamic rollover — lateral overload during landing |
| Any act requiring evaluator intervention for safety |

## Incomplete Items (IIs) and Downs

### II Disposition

- All IIs issued during the check ride must be debriefed
- The student must re-demonstrate each II item in a follow-on session before receiving a final PASS
- If the II item cannot be re-demonstrated in one session, the check ride is considered REPEAT and must be reflown in its entirety

### Down Disposition

- A Down requires a full debrief with the evaluator
- The student may not re-fly the check ride until all events from 1–7 have been reviewed with an instructor
- A second Down on the same check ride profile requires instructor review and may indicate the student is not ready for the BQT standard

---

## Check Ride Results

Upon completion of the check ride with a PASS grade:

| Result | Student Status |
|---|---|
| **PASS (no IIs)** | Basic Qualified — OH-58D (Simulation). Ready for advanced courses. |
| **PASS (IIs resolved)** | Basic Qualified — OH-58D (Simulation) after II re-demonstration. |
| **REPEAT** | Not qualified; return to Events 1–7 review; re-schedule check ride. |
| **DOWN** | Not qualified; mandatory debrief; instructor review; remedial training. |

**Basic Qualification Standard:** A student holding OH-58D BQT qualification is authorized to fly solo DCS OH-58D sorties in day VMC conditions, conduct navigation, pattern work, and practice emergency procedures. Combat employment (weapons, CCA tactics, RSTA missions) requires the follow-on qualification course.
