## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Recite all five boldface emergency procedures verbatim without reference to notes
- [ ] Respond to a simulated engine failure with collective down within 2 seconds
- [ ] Establish 60–80 KIAS autorotation airspeed and maintain Nr within 90–107%
- [ ] Recognize and verbally call out simulated LTE onset; execute recovery actions correctly
- [ ] Recognize and verbally call out simulated SWP onset; execute recovery actions correctly
- [ ] Demonstrate FADEC manual mode operations with collective changes
- [ ] Execute a full autorotation to landing from 500 ft AGL minimum

### Safety Brief for This Event

> **BEFORE ANY EMERGENCY PROCEDURE SIMULATION:** The instructor will ensure the area is clear, the altitude is sufficient for recovery, and the student understands what will happen. No simulated emergency will be conducted below minimum safe altitudes.

| Emergency | Minimum Altitude to Begin | Minimum Recovery Altitude |
|---|---|---|
| Simulated engine failure (autorotation entry) | 1,000 ft AGL | 300 ft AGL (recovery call) |
| Full autorotation to landing | 500 ft AGL | Complete to landing |
| LTE simulation | 300 ft AGL (hover altitude) | Recovery at any altitude |
| SWP simulation | 1,000 ft AGL | Recovery immediate; < 500 ft AGL not permitted |
| FADEC manual mode | 1,000 ft AGL | Normal recovery at any altitude |

## Part 1 — Boldface Oral Review

**This is a gate item.** The student must recite all five boldface procedures verbatim before the event continues. Incorrect or incomplete boldface = no flight for this event. Schedule a repeat.

**Test Format:** Instructor calls the emergency; student recites steps without hesitation or reference.

| # | Emergency | Student Must Recite |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ENGINE FAILURE | Collective LOWER / Airspeed 60–80 KIAS / Throttle OFF / Mayday / Execute autorotation |
| 2 | ENGINE FIRE IN FLIGHT | Throttle OFF / Autorotate EXECUTE / Land ASAP / Fuel Boost Pump OFF / Battery OFF |
| 3 | SETTLING WITH POWER | Cyclic FORWARD aggressively / Collective REDUCE momentarily / Recover |
| 4 | LTE RECOVERY | Pedal FULL LEFT / Collective REDUCE / Cyclic FORWARD / If unrecoverable: AUTOROTATE |
| 5 | HYDRAULIC FAILURE | Expect HIGH CONTROL FORCES / Airspeed 40–60 KIAS / Avoid hover / Land ASAP |

## Part 2 — Simulated Engine Failure

Three repetitions of autorotation entry from cruise flight. The instructor initiates each by saying: **"Engine failure — autorotate."**

The student does NOT use the throttle for these reps unless directed — the "engine failure" is simulated by the instructor calling it out; the engine continues to run. The student practices the collective action and establishes the autorotation profile, then the instructor calls "recovery" and the student applies power and climbs out.

### Rep 1 — Straight-Ahead Autorotation Entry

| Step | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"Engine failure — autorotate"** from instructor | T+0 |
| 2 | Collective DOWN | T+0 to T+2 sec (immediate) |
| 3 | Airspeed → 60–80 KIAS (cyclic adjustment) | T+2 to T+5 sec |
| 4 | Verbally call out Nr | "Nr is ninety-four percent" |
| 5 | Select landing area ahead | Within 10 seconds |
| 6 | Instructor: "Recovery" | At 300 ft AGL or instructor decision |
| 7 | Apply collective; throttle (if needed) to FLY; climb out | — |

### Rep 2 — Turn Required (Off-Heading Landing Area)

Setup: The instructor designates a landing area that requires a **turn of 30–60°** from the current heading before it can be reached.

- Student must perform all entry actions first, THEN turn toward the designated area
- Nr must remain in range throughout the turn
- Student calls: "Landing area is at my 10 o'clock, 600 meters"

### Rep 3 — From Low Altitude (500 ft AGL)

This rep is done at lower altitude to emphasize the time pressure of a real engine failure at low altitude.

- Student will have far less time from entry to "decision altitude"
- The purpose is not to complete a landing — it is to demonstrate the urgency of immediate action

### Autorotation Entry Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Collective down | < 2 seconds from command |
| Nr at stabilization | 90–107% (neither low nor overrevving) |
| Airspeed established | 60–80 KIAS within 10 seconds |
| Landing area selected | Identified within 15 seconds |

## Part 3 — LTE Recognition and Recovery

LTE simulation requires careful setup at low airspeed and high power. The instructor will configure the scenario; the student must recognize and respond.

### LTE Setup

- Low airspeed: hover or slow flight at 0–20 KIAS
- High power (torque): 70–85%
- Wind condition: tailwind or left-side crosswind component (instructors may use a low/slow cruise turn to the right to generate LTE conditions)

### LTE Recognition

The instructor will say **"You are experiencing LTE"** or will maneuver the aircraft into a low-speed right yaw condition that cannot be arrested with normal pedal inputs. The student must:

1. **Call it:** "LTE — applying full left pedal"
2. **Apply:** Full left pedal
3. **Reduce collective:** Reduce torque
4. **Forward cyclic:** Gain airspeed to increase tail rotor authority
5. **If unrecoverable:** Call "Autorotating" and execute

**Verbal Callout Required:** During the event, the student must call out every action.

### LTE Recognition Cues

| Cue | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Uncommanded right yaw | Tail rotor unable to maintain directional control |
| Full left pedal not stopping the yaw | LTE established — classical symptom |
| Aircraft yawing toward the nose-right position | Torque is dominating the tail rotor |

> *⚠ Realism Divergence: In real OH-58D operations, LTE can develop suddenly at low airspeed and high power in specific wind conditions. DCS models LTE aerodynamics, but the onset may be less sudden than the real aircraft. The student should practice recognizing the yaw tendency early and applying corrective pedal before reaching full LTE.*

## Part 4 — Settling with Power Recognition

SWP simulation must be set up above 1,000 ft AGL. The instructor will create the setup conditions and the student must recognize onset and recover.

### SWP Setup Conditions

The instructor will:
1. Configure the aircraft at 10–15 KIAS in a descent with 30–50% torque applied
2. Have the student maintain this condition as rate of descent increases
3. When the student can feel/see the characteristic SWP cues — call for recovery

### SWP Onset Cues

| Cue | Description |
|---|---|
| **Increased vibration** | Rotor entering its own vortex ring |
| **Uncommanded descent** | Rate of descent increases despite no collective change |
| **Collective non-responsive** | Adding collective worsens the condition (deepens into vortex) |
| **Airspeed < 15 KIAS** | Combined with power and descent = SWP |

### SWP Recovery

**At onset (still > 200 ft AGL):**
1. Student calls: "Settling with power — forward cyclic"
2. **Cyclic FORWARD aggressively** — gain airspeed immediately
3. **Collective REDUCE** (momentarily if altitude permits) — break vortex state
4. Aircraft exits vortex state at 20–30 KIAS; lift restored
5. Recover from nose-low attitude

**If at low altitude (< 200 ft AGL) — SWP is potentially unsurvivable:** The student must recognize onset EARLY at altitude during this event to understand why early recognition is critical.

## Part 5 — FADEC Failure (Manual Mode)

This evolution practices engine management when the FADEC is in manual mode.

### Setup

- From cruise at 80 KIAS, 1,000 ft AGL
- Instructor simulates FADEC failure by directing student to **manually switch FADEC to MANUAL mode** (or instructor note: DCS may not model switchable FADEC modes — instructor briefs per DCS module behavior)
- Student practices correlating throttle with collective in manual mode

### Manual Mode Operations

| Situation | Throttle Action |
|---|---|
| **Increasing collective** (adding power) | Add throttle to maintain Nr at 100% |
| **Decreasing collective** (reducing power) | Reduce throttle to prevent Nr overspeed |
| **Maintaining level flight** | Hold throttle position that maintains Nr 98–100% |

**Cross-Check:** In manual mode, scan is Nr → TOT → Cyclic/Collective coordination. Nr is the primary indicator of whether the throttle/collective relationship is correct.

**Recovery (FADEC failure — cannot restore AUTO mode):**
1. Maintain manual throttle control
2. Fly at moderate power settings (avoid large collective changes if possible)
3. Plan a landing at the nearest suitable area
4. Maintain constant scan: Nr, TOT, Torque

> *⚠ Realism Divergence: The DCS Polychop OH-58D models FADEC failure to varying degrees. If the DCS module does not allow direct FADEC mode switching, the instructor will demonstrate the manual throttle correlation as a what-if scenario. The academic understanding of the procedure is the primary objective.*

## Part 6 — Hydraulic Failure Simulation

This is a descriptive and abbreviated simulation since DCS may limit the ability to disable hydraulics mid-flight.

### Academic Review (In-Flight)

While at cruise:
1. Instructor says: "Hydraulic failure — your boost is gone"
2. Student recites: "Hydraulic failure. Expect high control forces. Airspeed 40–60 KIAS for run-on. Avoiding hover. Land ASAP."
3. Student demonstrates awareness of the implications by planning a run-on landing at a suitable area

### Behavioral Changes to Expect

| Item | Normal | Hydraulic Failure |
|---|---|---|
| Cyclic forces | Light | Very heavy (two-handed input) |
| Collective forces | Moderate | Heavy |
| Hover capability | Normal | Avoid — too difficult to control |
| Landing technique | Hover to touchdown | Run-on at 20–40 KIAS |
| Approach type | Normal | Shallow / run-on |

## Part 7 — Autorotation to Landing

The full autorotation to landing is the culmination of Event 7. The student executes a complete autorotation from entry to touchdown — **no simulated recovery**. This may be done at the airfield (into the runway or a designated area) or in a suitable open field.

### Setup

- 800–1,000 ft AGL over the designated landing area
- 80 KIAS cruise
- Engine running (DCS simulation)
- Landing area briefed and confirmed clear

### Full Autorotation Phases

**Phase 1 — Entry** (on command)
- Collective DOWN (immediate)
- Airspeed → 60–80 KIAS
- Nr → 90–107%
- Landing area selected/confirmed ahead

**Phase 2 — Steady Autorotation** (from entry to 200 ft AGL)
- Maintain 60–80 KIAS (60 KIAS = minimum RoD; 80 KIAS = max glide)
- Monitor Nr — adjust collective slightly to stay in 90–107% range
- Adjust heading to remain on approach path to landing area
- Rate of descent: 1,500–2,000 FPM typical

**Phase 3 — Flare** (200–50 ft AGL)
- Apply **aft cyclic** to raise the nose
- This bleeds airspeed and exchanges forward kinetic energy for lift
- Nr increases slightly during flare — manage with small collective adjustments
- Rate of descent decreases; forward speed decreases
- Target: arriving at ~20–30 ft AGL with low forward speed and reduced descent rate

**Phase 4 — Power Recovery / Cushion** (50–0 ft AGL)
- As aircraft decelerates to near-hover conditions (10–15 KIAS)
- Apply **collective** to use remaining Nr as a cushion
- This is the **"Nr energy exchange"** — the kinetic energy stored in the spinning rotor is converted to lift at the moment of touchdown
- Timing is critical: too early = run out of Nr before touchdown; too late = hard landing
- Target: soften the touchdown to a survivable rate (< 300 FPM)

**Phase 5 — Touchdown**
- Maintain directional control with pedals
- Allow aircraft to settle
- Immediately lower collective to flat pitch after skid contact

### Autorotation to Landing Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Entry — time | Collective down < 2 sec from command |
| Nr — steady state | 90–107% throughout |
| Airspeed — steady state | 60–80 KIAS; consistent |
| Flare — altitude | Initiated 150–200 ft AGL |
| Touchdown | Both skids contact near simultaneously; no Nr rundown below 90% before touchdown |
| Landing area | Within 50 ft of designated area |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| **Hesitation on collective at engine failure call** | "There is no pause. COLLECTIVE DOWN is reflexive. Every time you slow to respond is Nr that's gone." |
| **Nr allowed to exceed 107% in autorotation** | "Too fast on the rotor. Add a little collective to load the blades and reduce Nr. Don't let it race." |
| **Nr below 90% in autorotation** | "You waited too long to get collective down, or you pulled too much collective during the flare. Monitor Nr continuously." |
| **Flaring too early** | "If you flare at 500 feet you'll run out of airspeed and Nr before you get to the ground. Flare at 150-200 feet." |
| **Flaring too late** | "No flare = no cushion = hard landing. 150-200 feet: nose back, Nr up, cushion at 20 feet." |
| **Pulling collective too early in cushion** | "All your rotor energy spent at 30 feet. Nothing left at touchdown. Collective pull at 10-20 feet — not above." |
| **Panicking during SWP entry** | "Calm. Forward cyclic. Break the state. You have more time than you think — if you started the recovery above 500 feet." |
| **Not calling out actions verbally** | "Say what you're doing. 'Collective down — airspeed 70 — Nr ninety-five.' The callout builds the habit and tells your instructor you know the procedure." |
