## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Execute a normal takeoff from hover through ETL to climb attitude without excessive altitude gain or loss
- [ ] Maintain straight-and-level flight within ±100 ft altitude and ±5 KIAS at cruise speeds
- [ ] Execute coordinated turns at standard rate (approximately 15° bank) and medium bank (approximately 30° bank) while maintaining altitude ±100 ft and ball centered
- [ ] Execute climbs and descents at assigned airspeeds and power settings
- [ ] Demonstrate slow flight at 30–40 KIAS without altitude loss or heading deviation
- [ ] Apply force trim correctly after each airspeed or attitude change
- [ ] Enter a practice autorotation from cruise at assigned airspeed, establish 60–80 KIAS, and maintain Nr within autorotation range (90–107%)
- [ ] Execute a normal decelerating approach from cruise to a hover at a designated point

## Part 2 — Normal Takeoff

The normal takeoff transitions the aircraft from a stationary hover through ETL into a climbing departure.

### Normal Takeoff — Step by Step

| Step | Action | Monitor |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | From 3-ft hover — hover check: all instruments normal | Torque, TOT, Nr |
| 2 | Hover to a clear departure path | Confirm forward area clear of obstacles |
| 3 | Lower nose slightly with **forward cyclic** | Aircraft begins accelerating |
| 4 | Maintain altitude with **collective** as airspeed builds | Altitude stays at ~3–5 ft as speed builds |
| 5 | At **16–24 KIAS** (ETL): aircraft pitches up and tries to climb | Expect this — it is normal |
| 6 | Counter ETL pitch-up with **forward cyclic** | Maintain attitude; do not let nose rise to extreme |
| 7 | Reduce collective slightly as ETL provides additional lift efficiency | Prevents rapid altitude gain |
| 8 | As airspeed approaches **50–55 KIAS** — establish climb attitude | Nose slightly above horizon; collective adjusted for climb |
| 9 | Climb at **55–70 KIAS (Vy ~60 KIAS)** | Best rate of climb; monitor torque ≤ 100% |
| 10 | At target altitude, level off | Lower nose to cruise attitude; reduce collective slightly |

### ETL Recognition

At 16–24 KIAS, the student should **feel** and **anticipate** ETL:
- Airframe vibration pattern changes
- Aircraft tends to pitch up
- Power requirement drops noticeably

This is not an emergency. It is a normal and expected aerodynamic transition.

**Student Test:** Instructor will ask during debrief: "At what airspeed did you feel ETL? What did the aircraft do? What did you do to counteract it?"

## Part 3 — Basic Airwork

Once at the practice area altitude (1,000–2,000 ft AGL):

### Straight-and-Level Flight

| Airspeed | Power Setting | Expected Torque |
|---|---|---|
| 60 KIAS | Low cruise | 50–65% Q |
| 80 KIAS | Medium cruise | 60–70% Q |
| 100 KIAS | High cruise | 70–85% Q |

**Straight-and-Level Cross-Check:**
- Horizon → Airspeed → Altitude → VSI → Back to horizon
- Cycle every 2–3 seconds
- Use outside visual reference (horizon) as primary attitude reference; instruments confirm

**Trim after every airspeed change.** See Part 5 for trim technique.

### Coordinated Turns

| Turn Type | Bank | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Rate** | ~15° | Ball centered; altitude ±100 ft |
| **Medium Bank** | 30° | Ball centered; altitude ±100 ft |
| **Steep Bank** (demo only) | 45° | Ball centered; altitude ±150 ft |

**Coordinated Turn Technique:**
1. Apply cyclic in the direction of the turn
2. Increase collective **slightly** as bank increases (more bank = less vertical lift component)
3. Add **small pedal** in the direction of the turn if ball deflects
4. Monitor altitude — in a turn, you are losing vertical lift; collective must compensate
5. Roll out 10° early for a 30° banked turn; 15° early for 45° banked

**Ball centered = coordinated.** If the ball goes left, add left pedal. If it goes right, add right pedal.

### Climbs

| Technique | Airspeed | Power |
|---|---|---|
| **Best Rate (Vy)** | 60 KIAS | Max continuous |
| **Best Angle (Vx)** | 50 KIAS | Max continuous |
| **Cruise Climb** | 70–80 KIAS | Moderate; comfortable monitoring |

**Climb Entry:** Apply collective to increase power → aircraft will yaw right → apply left pedal → lower nose slightly if airspeed overshoots → trim.

### Descents

| Technique | Airspeed | Power |
|---|---|---|
| **Normal descent** | 70–80 KIAS | Reduce collective; maintain airspeed |
| **Power-off descent** | 60 KIAS (autorotation speed) | Collective full down = practice auto |
| **Steep descent** | 60–70 KIAS | Shallow collective; monitor Nr in shallow power settings |

**Descent Entry:** Lower collective → torque reduces → yaw left tendency → apply right pedal → maintain airspeed with cyclic → trim.

## Part 4 — Slow Flight

Slow flight develops low-airspeed control proficiency and increases awareness of the aerodynamic hazards that emerge below 30 KIAS.

### Slow Flight Procedure

| Step | Action | Monitor |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | From cruise (80–100 KIAS) — smoothly reduce collective | Airspeed begins decreasing |
| 2 | As airspeed decreases — raise nose slightly to maintain altitude | Collective and cyclic coordinated |
| 3 | Target airspeed: **30–40 KIAS** | Hold with cyclic; collective for altitude |
| 4 | Note: increased collective required at low airspeed for same altitude | Torque higher at 30 KIAS than 80 KIAS for same altitude |
| 5 | Maintain heading with pedals | Active yaw correction required |
| 6 | Practice gentle turns at 30 KIAS — **≤ 15° bank** | Steep turns not permitted in slow flight |

### Slow Flight Hazard Awareness

At 30–40 KIAS, the following risks are elevated:
- **LTE potential** — below 30 KIAS, tail rotor effectiveness decreases
- **SWP potential** — any significant collective reduction at this speed + any power = SWP conditions
- **Retreating blade stall** — NOT a concern at 30 KIAS (this is low airspeed, not high)

**Exit Slow Flight:** Lower nose (forward cyclic) → airspeed builds → as ETL passes (16–24 kts, in reverse) → power requirement changes → reduce collective slightly → trim at cruise airspeed.

## Part 5 — Trim Technique

Proper trim is the foundation of hands-off aircraft management. The OH-58D uses **force trim** (magnetic brake on cyclic and pedals) rather than traditional fixed-wing trim tabs.

### Force Trim Procedure

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Establish desired airspeed and attitude |
| 2 | Press and hold **Force Trim Release** button (cyclic grip) |
| 3 | While holding, move controls to the exact desired position |
| 4 | Release the button — trim is now set at this position |
| 5 | If hands are removed from cyclic, aircraft holds trim reference |

### When to Re-Trim

- After **every airspeed change** (cruise to slow flight and back)
- After **every power change** (climb power to cruise power)
- After **every configuration change**
- Any time you are holding **constant control pressure** — that pressure means you are out of trim

**Test:** In cruise flight, remove hands from the cyclic. If the aircraft maintains attitude, you are in trim. If it pitches or rolls, you are out of trim.

### Common Trim Errors

| Error | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Aircraft wanders after speed change | Not re-trimming | Trim after every speed or power change |
| High workload in level flight | Flying against trim | Trim proactively; you should be making small corrections, not large ones |
| Trim set but aircraft still wanders | Trim set before stabilization | Stabilize attitude first, then trim |

## Part 6 — Practice Autorotation Entry

The practice autorotation entry trains the student to recognize engine failure and immediately take the correct initial action. In this event, the student **does not complete the autorotation to landing** — the instructor will call "recovery" at 200–300 ft AGL minimum.

### Autorotation Entry — Step by Step

**Setup:**
- 80–100 KIAS at 1,500–2,000 ft AGL
- Over a suitable landing area (open field)
- Collective at cruise power

**Entry (on command from instructor: "Engine failure — autorotate"):**

| Step | Action | Time | Monitor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **COLLECTIVE — LOWER immediately** | < 1 second | Nr must be maintained |
| 2 | **Airspeed — 60–80 KIAS** (apply cyclic) | Within 3 seconds | Establish best autorotation airspeed |
| 3 | **Throttle — OFF** (if engine has failed) | Concurrent | Engine spools down |
| 4 | Nr check — **90–107%** | Continuous | Nr below 90% = too slow; above 107% = too fast |
| 5 | Select **landing area** | Within 5 seconds | Identify field ahead |
| 6 | Turn toward landing area if necessary | As needed | Gentle bank; avoid excessive Nr reduction |
| 7 | Instructor calls "RECOVERY" | 200–300 ft AGL | Do not attempt actual landing |

**Recovery from Practice Autorotation:**
1. Apply collective smoothly — add power
2. Throttle → FLY (if throttle was moved to OFF)
3. FADEC resumes Nr control as power is restored
4. Climb away from designated area
5. Reset for next maneuver

### Autorotation Entry Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Time to collective down | < 2 seconds from entry command |
| Nr at stabilization | 90–107% (must not exceed 107% or drop below 90%) |
| Airspeed established | 60–80 KIAS within 10 seconds of entry |
| Landing area selected | Identified and pointed toward within 15 seconds |

> *The autorotation entry is a reflex action. "Engine failure — collective down" must be practiced until it is instantaneous. Hesitation costs altitude and Nr that cannot be recovered.*

## Part 7 — Normal Approach and Landing

The normal approach returns the aircraft from cruise to a landing at the departure airfield.

### Pattern Entry

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Descend to traffic pattern altitude (500–800 ft AGL) |
| 2 | Enter a left-hand pattern (or as required by field) |
| 3 | Abeam landing point — 70–80 KIAS |
| 4 | Base leg turn — begin deceleration to 50–60 KIAS |
| 5 | Final — 40–50 KIAS; established on a 8–12° glidepath |

### Normal Decelerating Approach

The normal approach is a smooth transition from cruise to hover over the touchdown point.

| Phase | Airspeed | Altitude | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Final Entry** | 50–60 KIAS | 300–400 ft AGL | Establish 8–12° glidepath; nose slightly low |
| **Mid-Final** | 40–50 KIAS | 150–200 ft AGL | Continue deceleration; collective to maintain glidepath |
| **Short Final** | 20–30 KIAS | 50–100 ft AGL | Aircraft approaching ETL in reverse — expect pitch change |
| **Flare** | 10–15 KIAS | 20–40 ft AGL | Aft cyclic to arrest forward speed; collective to arrest descent |
| **Termination** | 0–5 KIAS hover | 3–5 ft AGL | Stabilize in hover over touchdown point |
| **Touchdown** | 0 KIAS | 0 ft | Lower collective smoothly to set down |

**Key Cue:** As the aircraft decelerates through 20–24 KIAS on final, it exits ETL (in reverse). The lift efficiency decreases — more collective is required to maintain altitude. Anticipate this and add collective smoothly.

### Approach Standards for This Event

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Glidepath | 8–12° throughout final |
| Airspeed control | Steady deceleration; no sudden speed changes |
| Termination point | Hover within 15 ft of designated landing point |
| Hover height | 3–5 ft stabilized hover |
| Touchdown | Smooth; both skids simultaneously |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| **Allowing ETL to pitch nose up on takeoff** | "Anticipate ETL at 16–24 knots. Pre-position forward cyclic as you accelerate through that band. Don't be surprised by it." |
| **Not trimming after each maneuver** | "Trim is not optional. Every speed change, trim the aircraft. If you're holding pressure, you're not trimmed." |
| **Dropping altitude in turns** | "You're trading vertical lift component for turn. Add collective in the turn. Come out, reduce. Every time." |
| **Late collective on autorotation entry** | "Engine failure — collective is your first and immediate action. Not in 3 seconds. NOW. Practice this until it is reflexive." |
| **Nr out of range on autorotation entry** | "Rotor above 107% — reduce collective. Below 90% — you waited too long. Immediate action prevents both." |
| **Too steep/too shallow on approach** | "8-12 degrees. Too steep and you're a dart — too shallow and you overshoot. Fly the glidepath, not the airspeed." |
| **Not adding collective through ETL exit on final** | "Coming through 20 knots on final, ETL disappears. The aircraft wants to sink. Add collective before it sinks." |
