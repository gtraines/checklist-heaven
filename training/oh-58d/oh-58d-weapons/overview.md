## Course Description

The OH-58D Kiowa Warrior Weapons Employment Course (WEC) qualifies a pilot to employ the full OH-58D weapons suite in DCS World: AGM-114 Hellfire missiles (K/M/N variants), Hydra 70 unguided rockets, APKWS laser-guided rockets, M3P .50 caliber machine gun, and AIM-92 Stinger air-to-air missile. The course also covers countermeasures employment, Close Combat Attack (CCA) tactics, and integrated mission planning.

> **DCS Module Note:** The DCS OH-58D (Polychop Simulations) is a full-fidelity, clickable cockpit module. All weapons systems, MFD pages, and MMS sensor controls are interactable. This course requires precise cockpit interaction and HOTAS configuration for efficient weapons employment. Students must configure HOTAS bindings for MMS slew, sensor toggle, laser, and weapon selection before commencing Event 3.

Upon successful completion, the student will be qualified to:
- Operate the Mast Mounted Sight (MMS) in all sensor modes (TV/FLIR) for target acquisition, identification, and laser designation
- Employ the AGM-114 Hellfire in LOBL and LOAL modes from hull-down battle positions
- Deliver Hydra 70 unguided rockets using CCIP in diving, running, and hover fire profiles
- Employ APKWS (AGR-20A) laser-guided rockets as a precision alternative to Hellfire
- Employ the M3P .50 caliber machine gun for suppression and anti-soft-target fires
- Execute AIM-92 Stinger self-defense engagements and apply rotary-wing defensive doctrine
- Plan and execute Close Combat Attack missions including battle position operations, engagement area management, and team tactics
- Develop a complete pre-mission plan including threat analysis, loadout selection, and communication deconfliction

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this course, the student must:
- [ ] Have completed the **OH-58D Basic Qualification Training (BQT)** or demonstrate equivalent proficiency in: cold-and-dark startup, AHRS/GPS alignment, stationary hover, takeoff/landing, basic navigation, and emergency procedures
- [ ] Be able to operate all cockpit systems from memory: engine start, avionics power-up, MFD page navigation
- [ ] Have a functional HOTAS with collective, cyclic, anti-torque, and **at minimum**: a trigger/pickle button, a laser designate button, and analog slew axes for MMS
- [ ] Have read the **Polychop OH-58D DCS module documentation** relevant to the MMS, weapons systems, and armament panel
- [ ] Be able to maintain a stable hull-down hover for 30 seconds without significant drift (±3 ft lateral/longitudinal, ±2 ft altitude)

> **HOTAS Configuration:** Efficient weapons employment requires pre-mapped HOTAS bindings for: MMS slew (X/Y analog), FOV toggle, sensor toggle (TV/FLIR), laser designate, LRF pulse, weapon cycle, and countermeasures (chaff/flare). **Verify all bindings before Event 3.** Relying on mouse-click cockpit interaction during tactical engagements is not acceptable.

> **Recommended:** Complete a self-study session on the MFD MMS page before Event 1, ensuring you can power on the MMS, switch between TV/TIS, toggle FOV, and fire the LRF using HOTAS without reference to the manual.

## Syllabus Events

Complete events **in sequence**. Each event must pass before progressing.

| Event | Title | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Event 1](event-1-mms-operations.md) | MMS Operations & Targeting | Academics/Sim | MMS sensor suite, power-on, boresight, TV/TIS modes, laser operations, tracking modes, target acquisition workflow |
| [Event 2](event-2-weapons-management.md) | Weapons Management System | Academics/Sim | MFD weapons pages, Master Arm sequence, emergency/selective jettison, HUD/MFD symbology, weapon BIT |
| [Event 3](event-3-hellfire-employment.md) | AGM-114 Hellfire Employment | Flight | Hellfire variants, LOBL and LOAL engagement sequences, launch envelopes, hull-down technique, buddy lase |
| [Event 4](event-4-hydra-70-rockets.md) | Hydra 70 Rocket Employment | Flight | Rocket warhead selection, CCIP delivery, diving/running/hover fire profiles, dispersion management |
| [Event 5](event-5-apkws-rockets.md) | APKWS Laser-Guided Rockets | Flight | APKWS system overview, seeker basket concept, employment sequence with laser designation, comparison to Hellfire/unguided |
| [Event 6](event-6-50cal-gun.md) | M3P .50 Caliber Gun Employment | Flight | Fixed-mount gun characteristics, CCIP pipper, diving/running/hover fire, recoil management, ammunition discipline |
| [Event 7](event-7-stinger-air-to-air.md) | AIM-92 Stinger Air-to-Air | Academics/Sim | Stinger system overview, engagement envelope, employment sequence, self-defense doctrine, threat environment |
| [Event 8](event-8-countermeasures.md) | Countermeasures & Defensive Systems | Academics/Sim | Dispenser system, RWR threat display, defensive maneuvering, threat-specific reactions, SEAD integration |
| [Event 9](event-9-cca-tactics.md) | CCA Tactics & Engagement Area Operations | Flight | CCA doctrine, battle position operations, engagement area planning, team tactics (Scout/Attack, Hunter/Killer), target handoff |
| [Event 10](event-10-mission-planning.md) | Mission Planning & Loadout Optimization | Academics/Planning | Threat analysis, loadout selection, engagement planning, communication/laser code deconfliction, DCS Mission Editor |
| [Event 11](event-11-check-ride.md) | Weapons Employment Check Ride | Evaluation | Full-mission pass/fail evaluation: planning, MMS operations, two or more weapons systems employed, CCA tactics |

**Estimated Completion:** 10–16 simulator sessions.

> **Note:** Events 1 and 2 are primarily academic/simulator familiarization events and may be conducted from ramp-start or hot-start. Events 3 through 9 begin from cold-and-dark unless otherwise specified. Event 10 is a ground planning exercise with optional flight execution. Event 11 (Check Ride) begins cold-and-dark with a full mission profile.

## Grading Standards

### Grades

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All standards met; student may progress to the next event |
| **REPEAT** | One or more standards not met; event must be re-flown before progressing |
| **DOWN** | Unsafe act observed; event grounded pending debrief and instructor review |

### Incomplete Item (II)

An Incomplete Item (II) is issued when the student fails one specific standard but otherwise passes the event. All IIs must be resolved before the Check Ride (Event 11). A student accumulating more than three unresolved IIs is considered not ready for the check ride.

### Grading Tolerances — Weapons Employment

| Task | Standard |
|---|---|
| **MMS boresight** | Completed before first engagement; LRF range confirms alignment within acceptable margin |
| **Laser armed before designation** | 100% compliance — no exceptions; failure to arm laser before lasing = Incomplete Item |
| **PRF code verification** | Verified on MFD before every laser-guided weapon engagement |
| **Master Arm discipline** | ARM only during active engagement; SAFE immediately after; ≥ 95% compliance |
| **Hellfire LOBL lock before fire** | LOCK indication confirmed before pickle; no fire without lock in LOBL mode |
| **Laser maintained through impact** | Continuous designation from lock until confirmed impact; no early cessation |
| **LOAL lasing timeline** | Lasing commenced ≥ 60% of TOF; no missiles fired without subsequent laser initiation |
| **Hull-down discipline** | Fuselage masked throughout engagement; MMS clearance only; no over-unmasking |
| **Hull-down time** | ≤ 60 seconds per unmask cycle; REPEAT standard if student consistently exceeds 90 seconds |
| **Alternate BP displacement** | Displaced to alternate BP after each 1–2 engagement pairs from same position |
| **BDA** | Conducted after every engagement; results reported to instructor within 15 seconds of impact |
| **Rocket delivery** | At least 1 direct hit per 3 rockets fired in diving fire profile at ≤ 2,000 m; PASS standard |
| **Gun CCIP accuracy** | Tracer impacts within 10 m of target at standard engagement ranges |
| **Ammunition discipline** | No more than 3 seconds sustained gun fire per burst; reported when ≤ 100 rounds remaining |
| **CM HOTAS binding** | Chaff and flare mapped to HOTAS before every flight; verification is event standard |
| **Stinger lock before fire** | High-pitch lock tone confirmed before trigger press (if modeled in DCS) |
| **Emergency jettison** | Correct procedure demonstrated without coaching when simulated emergency is called |
