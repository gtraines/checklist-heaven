## Course Description

The Mi-24P Hind Weapons Qualification Course (WQC) qualifies a pilot/WSO team to safely and effectively employ all primary and secondary weapons systems installed on the DCS Mi-24P Hind-F. The course covers fixed-forward cannon, anti-tank guided missiles (Shturm and Ataka), unguided rockets (S-5, S-8, S-13, S-24), air-to-air missiles (R-60 and R-73), defensive countermeasures (SPO-15 RWR, UV-26 chaff/flares), and combined-arms close combat attack tactics.

> **DCS Module Note:** The DCS Mi-24P (Eagle Dynamics — native module) is a **full-fidelity, dual-seat, clickable cockpit** module. Weapons employment divides between seats: the **WSO (front cockpit)** operates the PKV-1 optical sight, manages ATGM SACLOS guidance, selects weapon modes, and controls countermeasures; the **Pilot (rear cockpit)** flies the attack platform, aims and fires the GSh-30K cannon, and controls AAM launch from the rear panel. This course is structured for single-player using DCS's seat-switching feature (`1` = WSO front, `2` = Pilot rear). Multiplayer dual-seat with a separate WSO is fully compatible.

Upon successful completion, the student will be qualified to:
- Operate all weapons switches and controls in both the Pilot and WSO cockpits
- Operate the PKV-1 optical sight for target acquisition, tracking, and weapons delivery in all available modes
- Employ the GSh-30K 30mm cannon against soft and light armored targets using correct attack profiles
- Engage armored targets at range using 9M114 Shturm and 9M120 Ataka ATGMs with SACLOS guidance
- Deliver unguided rockets (S-5, S-8, S-13, S-24) using CCIP and manual delivery profiles against appropriate target types
- Employ R-60 and R-73 air-to-air missiles for helicopter self-defense
- Operate the SPO-15 Beryoza RWR to detect, identify, and respond to radar threats
- Employ the UV-26 chaff/flare system with appropriate countermeasures programs
- Plan and execute close combat attack (CCA) missions against defended ground targets
- Select correct weapon-to-target pairings for all common target types
- Perform emergency stores jettison procedures

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this course, the student must:
- [ ] Hold a **Mi-24P Basic Qualification Training (BQT)** qualification or demonstrate equivalent proficiency — ability to start, fly, and land the Mi-24P without instructor assistance
- [ ] Be able to operate all cockpit systems in both seats including electrical, avionics, communications, and autopilot
- [ ] Understand and apply DCS seat-switching procedures (`1`/`2`) and be able to operate both cockpits
- [ ] Have read the **DCS Mi-24P Eagle Dynamics module documentation** weapons sections
- [ ] Have a functional HOTAS configured with at least: collective, cyclic, anti-torque pedals, trigger, and weapons release button

> **Recommended:** Review the [Mi-24P Weapons Research Dossier](../../../.github/research/mi-24p-hind-weapons.md) prior to Event 1. The academic load in Event 1 is significant — pre-reading reduces ground time substantially.

> **Note on Cockpit Division:** This course teaches **both seats**. In DCS single-player, the student regularly switches from Pilot to WSO and back. The student is expected to engage the autopilot before switching seats to prevent loss of aircraft control during the transition.

> **Note on Weapons Availability:** DCS Mi-24P weapon options may vary by game version and module updates. Verify available stores in the DCS Mission Editor loadout screen for your specific version. All exercises in this syllabus can be completed with the baseline loadout options available in the standard module.

## Syllabus Events

Complete events **in sequence**. Each event must pass before progressing.

| Event | Title | Type | Key Systems | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Event 1](event-1-weapons-academics.md) | Weapons Systems Academics | Ground | All | Weapons inventory, cockpit switchology, pylon stations, arming procedures, crew coordination |
| [Event 2](event-2-pkv1-sight.md) | PKV-1 Sight Operations | Ground/Sim | PKV-1, LRF | Sight modes, reticle, zoom, range estimation, target acquisition, laser rangefinder |
| [Event 3](event-3-cannon.md) | GSh-30K Cannon Employment | Flight | GSh-30K | Cannon attack profiles, CCIP, soft/light armor targets, ammunition management |
| [Event 4](event-4-atgm.md) | ATGM Employment | Flight | Shturm, Ataka | SACLOS guidance, engagement envelopes, terrain masking, multi-shot employment |
| [Event 5](event-5-rockets.md) | Unguided Rocket Employment | Flight | S-5/S-8/S-13/S-24 | Rocket attack profiles, CCIP, weapon-to-target matching, area suppression |
| [Event 6](event-6-defensive-systems.md) | Defensive Systems | Flight | SPO-15, UV-26 | RWR threat identification, countermeasures programs, threat reaction procedures |
| [Event 7](event-7-air-to-air.md) | Air-to-Air Self-Defense | Flight | R-60, R-73 | IR missile employment, air threat recognition, defensive engagement |
| [Event 8](event-8-cca-mission-planning.md) | CCA Tactics & Mission Planning | Ground/Flight | All | Attack doctrine, mission planning, weight/performance, combined-arms integration |
| [Event 9](event-9-check-ride.md) | Integrated Weapons Check Ride | Evaluation | All | Full-profile pass/fail evaluation — mission planning through weapons employment and recovery |

**Estimated Completion:** 12–18 simulator sessions.

> **Note:** Events 2 through 9 each begin from a running aircraft (engines online, systems active) unless otherwise noted. A complete cold-dark startup is not required for weapons events — however, the student must demonstrate weapons switches **SAFE** before requesting range clearance in any graded event.

> **Dual-Seat Note:** If flying with a dedicated WSO, the WSO is solely responsible for front cockpit weapons operations. Crew coordination calls are **graded** in Events 3 through 9. In single-player, the pilot demonstrates both cockpit roles by switching seats and verbalizing crew calls.

## Grading Standards

### Grades

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All standards met; student may progress to the next event |
| **REPEAT** | One or more standards not met; event must be re-flown before progressing |
| **DOWN** | Unsafe act observed; event grounded pending debrief and instructor review |

### Incomplete Item (II)

An Incomplete Item (II) is issued when the student fails one specific standard but otherwise passes the event. All IIs must be resolved **before the Check Ride (Event 9)**. A student accumulating more than three unresolved IIs is not ready for the check ride.

### Weapons Employment Standards

| Maneuver / Task | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Weapons arming sequence** | Correct order, no step omitted, Master Arm last |
| **Emergency jettison** | Initiated within 3 sec of emergency declared; all stores cleared |
| **PKV-1 mode selection** | Correct mode for weapon type selected before attack |
| **Cannon engagement range** | First engagement within 400–1,200 m |
| **Cannon accuracy (practice)** | ≥50% of bursts on target or within 25 m of aim point |
| **ATGM guidance (Shturm/Ataka)** | Missile guided to within 3 m CEP of target; no guidance break during flight |
| **ATGM platform stability** | ≤5°/sec pitch/roll during SACLOS guidance phase |
| **Rocket delivery (CCIP)** | Salvo center of impact within 25 m of aim point |
| **S-24 delivery (manual)** | Impact within 50 m of aim point |
| **R-60 launch parameters** | Tone confirmed, uncaged, within envelope before launch |
| **Countermeasures (threat response)** | Correct program selected; initiated within 3 sec of threat indication |
| **Weapon-to-target matching** | Correct weapon selected for target type; no tank engagement with cannon only |
| **Crew coordination calls** | Target, weapon, firing, and impact calls made per standard |

### Safety Standards (Automatic DOWN)

- Firing weapons while outside safe employment parameters (minimum range, wrong heading)
- Fratricide (engaging friendly forces)
- Continuing attack into SAM engagement zone without commander's authorization
- Failure to confirm SAFE on all weapons before landing
- Overrunning minimum safe range for S-24 or S-13 rockets
