## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Establish and maintain level flight at cruise speeds across the Mi-24P's envelope (100–260 km/h)
- [ ] Execute coordinated turns at various bank angles up to 45°, ball centered throughout
- [ ] Perform climbs and descents using all three profiles (best rate, best angle, cruise)
- [ ] Fly in the slow flight regime (below ETL) while maintaining controlled flight
- [ ] Use force trim (STU) correctly to relieve control pressure and hold attitude
- [ ] Engage and disengage the autopilot and transition between hand-flying and AP-assisted flight
- [ ] Describe the difference in control feel and aircraft handling at 100, 150, 200, and 260 km/h

## Ground Brief

### The Mi-24P Is Two Different Aircraft

One of the most important things to internalize during this event: the Mi-24P **handles completely differently** at different airspeeds.

| Speed Range | Character | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **0–50 km/h (hover / hover transition)** | Pure helicopter | Rotor-dominated; high power; sensitive to gusts |
| **50–100 km/h** | Transition zone | ETL onset; wings beginning to contribute; control feel changes |
| **100–180 km/h** | Mixed — helicopter + airplane | More stable; wings offloading rotor; getting easier |
| **180–260 km/h** | Hind in its element | Stable, fast, efficient; airplane-like stability |
| **260–300 km/h** | Fast / approaching VNE | Smooth but powerful; VNE = 300 km/h; do not approach in training |

The student will explore each regime deliberately during this event.

### Force Trim (STU) Technique

The Mi-24P uses **irreversible hydraulic flight controls**. You get no aerodynamic feedback. The force trim system (STU) provides artificial centering force via magnetic brakes on the control linkage.

**Standard technique:**
1. Maneuver the controls to the desired position
2. **Press and release the force trim button** (on cyclic grip)
3. Controls now "feel" like they belong in that position
4. Aircraft will tend to return to trimmed attitude if controls are released

> **Tip:** Re-trim frequently. Trim after every major speed or attitude change. A properly trimmed Mi-24P will fly hands-off at cruise speed.

### Autopilot Use

The 4-channel autopilot (SAU-V4 / VUAP-1) works with the trimmed control position:

| AP Interaction | Method |
|---|---|
| **Engage all 4 channels** | Engage each channel individually via cockpit switches |
| **Disengage** | Press trim button (force trim) — momentarily interrupts AP; or switches OFF |
| **Making attitude changes with AP on** | Press and hold force trim; maneuver; release — AP accepts new position |
| **Single-seat transition** | Engage AP before switching seat `1`↔`2` |

**AP limitations to teach this event:**
- Do not engage below 50 m AGL
- AP does not prevent limit exceedance — pilot still responsible
- AP in heading mode holds current heading — change heading by pressing trim, turning, releasing

## In-Sim Procedures

### Exercise 1 — Level Flight Speed Exploration

Begin from a running takeoff. Climb to 500 m, establish level flight.

At each airspeed, student will:
- Establish level flight
- Note torque required
- Note control feel
- Note trim required

| Airspeed | Typical Torque | Control Feel | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **100 km/h** | 75–80% | Moderate; slight lateral sensitivity | Wings contributing little; rotor doing most work |
| **150 km/h** | 70–75% | Improved; aircraft more stable | ETL fully established; comfortable cruise |
| **200 km/h** | 65–70% | Good; responsive but stable | Hind cruise regime; wings carrying ~20% lift |
| **260 km/h** | 75–80% | Firm; airplane-like | Pushing toward high speed; adequate margins remain |

**Exercise sequence:**
1. Establish 100 km/h level — hold 1 minute, note instruments
2. Increase to 150 km/h — hold 1 minute
3. Increase to 200 km/h — hold 1 minute
4. Increase to 260 km/h — hold 30 seconds, then reduce (do not approach VNE)
5. Return to 180 km/h for subsequent exercises

### Exercise 2 — Coordinated Turns

At 180 km/h, 500 m altitude:

| Turn | Bank Angle | Duration | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shallow turn** | 15° | 360° | Ball centered; altitude ±50 m |
| **Medium turn** | 30° | 360° | Ball centered; altitude ±50 m |
| **Training max turn** | 45° | 180° | Ball centered; altitude ±50 m; G within limits |

**Technique:**
1. Establish bank with lateral cyclic
2. Add back cyclic as bank increases (needed to maintain altitude in turns)
3. Increase collective slightly in turns > 30° bank (greater G load = more collective needed)
4. Maintain ball centered with pedal pressure throughout
5. Roll out on target heading, releasing back pressure and collective addition

**G-load reminder:** 45° bank = ~1.4 G. Max training limit is +2.5 G. 60° bank = 2.0 G — avoid in BQT.

### Exercise 3 — Climbs

Practice each climb type from 200 m, climb to 600 m:

| Profile | Airspeed | Power | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Best Angle (Vx)** | 100–110 km/h | 75–80% torque | Slow, steep — obstacle clearance |
| **Best Rate (Vy)** | 120–140 km/h | 75–80% torque | Fastest altitude gain — standard |
| **Cruise Climb** | 160–180 km/h | 70–75% torque | Comfortable, maintains speed |

**Sequence:**
1. Establish entry altitude (200 m)
2. Slow/accelerate to entry airspeed
3. Apply climb power — note torque
4. Maintain airspeed — adjust collective
5. Level off at 600 m

### Exercise 4 — Descents

Practice each descent type from 600 m, descend to 200 m:

| Profile | Airspeed | Rate | Power | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cruise descent** | 180–220 km/h | 3–5 m/s | Reduced collective | Normal transit |
| **Normal approach angle** | 120–140 km/h | 3–5 m/s | Reduced collective | Approach simulation |
| **Steep descent** | 100–120 km/h | 5–8 m/s | Low collective | Practice for steep approach |

**Caution:** At descent rates > 5 m/s, airspeed < 80 km/h, and high collective = settling with power conditions. Maintain airspeed during all steep descents.

### Exercise 5 — Slow Flight

The purpose is to understand the Mi-24P's behavior below ETL (below 50 km/h) and near the hover transition. This regime is demanding because wings lose effectiveness and rotor must compensate.

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | At 500 m altitude — reduce collective gradually | Airspeed decays |
| 2 | Through 100 km/h — note control feel change | Becoming more helicopter-like |
| 3 | Through 50 km/h (ETL) — note power demand increase | Torque increases as wings stop contributing |
| 4 | Stabilize at 30 km/h | High torque; aircraft sensitive |
| 5 | Hold 30 km/h for 30 seconds | Note torque (will be 75–85%) |
| 6 | Accelerate back to 120 km/h (Vy) | Apply forward cyclic; through ETL again |

**Do not decelerate to a hover** during this exercise — that is covered in hover events. The purpose here is to understand the speed regime transition.

### Exercise 6 — Force Trim Technique

Dedicated drill to master force trim:

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish 180 km/h, level flight | |
| 2 | Place controls in neutral, note aircraft attitude | |
| 3 | **Press trim button** — release | This is the baseline trim position |
| 4 | Release cyclic — aircraft should maintain attitude | If trimmed correctly, aircraft flies hands-off |
| 5 | Apply back pressure — pitch nose up 5° | Hold for 3 seconds |
| 6 | **Press trim** — release | Re-trim in new position |
| 7 | Release cyclic — aircraft should hold new nose-up attitude | |
| 8 | Return to level with forward cyclic — trim in level position | |
| 9 | Practice bank changes with trim | Trim after each bank change |

**Common mistake:** Not re-trimming. The aircraft will fight the pilot if controls are held against the trimmed position for extended periods.

### Exercise 7 — Autopilot Operations

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish 180 km/h, level flight, trimmed | |
| 2 | Engage **Pitch** channel | Note aircraft holds pitch |
| 3 | Engage **Roll** channel | Note aircraft holds bank (wings level) |
| 4 | Engage **Heading** channel | Note aircraft holds current heading |
| 5 | Engage **Altitude** channel | Note aircraft holds altitude |
| 6 | Take hands off — aircraft flies itself | AP maintaining all 4 axes |
| 7 | Make a heading change: press trim → turn → release | AP accepts new heading |
| 8 | Disengage all channels | Hand-fly again |
| 9 | Re-engage 2 channels (pitch + roll only) | Partial AP use |
| 10 | Practice transition: AP on → hand-fly → AP on | Smooth transitions |

> **Single-seat protocol:** Press `1` to switch to WSO seat. Before doing so, engage all 4 AP channels with the aircraft trimmed and level. The aircraft will hold while you work WSO cockpit. Switch back `2` before any maneuver.

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Level flight** | ±50 m altitude, ±10 km/h airspeed at each test speed |
| **Coordinated turns** | Ball centered throughout; ±50 m altitude in all training turns |
| **Max bank** | Does not exceed 45° during graded turns |
| **Climbs** | Within ±10 km/h of target airspeed; reaches target altitude |
| **Descents** | Airspeed maintained; no settling with power onset |
| **Slow flight** | Demonstrates control below ETL; no loss of control |
| **Force trim** | Correctly trimmed for hands-off cruise; re-trims after attitude changes |
| **Autopilot** | Successfully engages/disengages all 4 channels; makes heading change with AP on |

## Common Errors

| Error | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Not adding back cyclic in turns | Altitude loss in turns | "Turning costs altitude. Back pressure in the turn — match the bank angle" |
| Not re-trimming after speed changes | Control forces build up; fatigue; imprecise flight | "Every major change = re-trim. Trim is maintenance, not an event" |
| Decelerating too aggressively into slow flight | Rapid power demand increase; potential Nr droop | "Gradual deceleration below 80 km/h. Let the aircraft slow down, don't force it" |
| Forgetting collective in turns | Altitude loss; excess G buildup in steep turns | "Collective up slightly in turns > 30°. Altitude is your feedback" |
| Fighting the AP in heading change | AP fights inputs; aircraft oscillates | "Trim button breaks the AP hold. Use it for attitude changes with AP on" |
| Not checking ball (slip indicator) in turns | Skidding / slipping turns; increased drag | "Ball is your turn quality referee. Center it — always" |
