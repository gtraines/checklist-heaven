## Learning Objectives

The check ride evaluates mastery of all prior BQT events. The student must demonstrate **independent** proficiency — the check ride IP will not provide instructional assistance. The student is expected to:
- [ ] Recite all boldface emergency procedures from memory before the flight
- [ ] Perform a complete startup from cold and dark independently, without prompting
- [ ] Demonstrate precise hover and hover taxi within the grading tolerances
- [ ] Fly a navigation leg using dead reckoning or radio navigation to a pre-briefed checkpoint
- [ ] Execute normal approaches and at least one go-around decision on their own (no IP cue required)
- [ ] Complete a confined area or slope landing with full verbal recon prior to approach
- [ ] Respond correctly to at least two IP-initiated emergency procedures (one must be an autorotation entry)
- [ ] Complete normal shutdown and post-shutdown walkthrough
- [ ] Demonstrate professional crew resource management, discipline, and radio procedures throughout

## Check Ride Overview

### Format

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Practical evaluation — no instructional assistance provided |
| **Evaluator** | Qualified IP acting as evaluator (no coaching during the ride) |
| **Pre-ride oral** | Briefed at start; student must pass boldface exam before the flight begins |
| **Duration** | ~60–90 minutes in the simulator |
| **EP insertions** | Minimum 2 emergencies, at least 1 autorotation entry |
| **Grading** | Go/No-Go per task; single Unsatisfactory in critical task = Bust |

### Unsatisfactory vs. Marginal vs. Satisfactory

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Satisfactory (S)** | Task completed within all grading tolerances; safe, coordinated, professional |
| **Marginal (M)** | Task completed but outside tolerances; safe; noted for debrief; too many Marginals = overall Unsatisfactory |
| **Unsatisfactory (U)** | Task not completed, unsafe action, exceeded limits, or required IP intervention to prevent damage |
| **Not Observed (N/O)** | Task not evaluated during this flight |

### Overall Result

| Result | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Pass** | All critical tasks Satisfactory; no more than 2 Marginals on non-critical tasks; no IP intervention required |
| **Partial Bust** | 3+ Marginals on non-critical tasks, or 1 Unsatisfactory on a non-critical task; re-ride on that specific task area |
| **Bust** | 1 Unsatisfactory on a critical task, or any IP intervention to prevent unsafe condition; repeat full Event 8 after additional training |

### Critical Tasks (Bust on Any Unsatisfactory)

- Boldface oral exam (any item incorrect = bust before the flight begins)
- Autorotation entry — collective lowering within limits; Nr maintained ≥294 RPM
- LTE or VRS recovery (if assigned as an EP)
- Go/no-go decision making — must go-around from an unstable approach without IP cue
- Emergency procedure sequence — correct order, no missed steps

## Pre-Ride Oral Examination

> **No flight without passing oral. The evaluator will quiz every boldface item. Partial credit is not given.**

### Oral Exam Topics

| Area | Questions | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Boldface** | Recite all 7 items | 100% correct — verbatim, in order |
| **Operating limits** | Nr, torque, EGT | Within ±5% of published values |
| **VRS / LTE** | Entry conditions, recognition, recovery | Must identify all three VRS conditions; must state forward cyclic for both LTE and VRS recovery |
| **Autorotation airspeed** | Best glide and min sink | 65 KIAS and 55 KIAS |
| **Mast bumping** | Prevention and what aft cyclic does | Must state: "Aft cyclic reloads the disc" and "never push forward cyclic under low-G" |
| **Land ASAP vs. ASAP** | Define both phrases | Must correctly distinguish between immediate landing and expedient landing |

### Operating Limits (Required for Oral)

| Parameter | Normal | Caution | Limit | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nr** | 321–327 RPM | 294–320 RPM | <294 (low) / >345 (overspeed) | TM 55-1520-210-10 |
| **Torque** | 0–40 PSI continuous | 40–48 PSI (time limited) | >50 PSI (structural) | TM 55-1520-210-10 |
| **EGT** | ≤610°C continuous | 610–625°C (5 sec limit) | >720°C during start (10 sec) | TM 55-1520-210-10 |
| **Vne** | 124 KIAS (sea level) | — | 124 KIAS | TM 55-1520-210-10 |
| **Max GW** | 9,500 lb (varies) | — | Per loading tables | TM 55-1520-210-10 |

## Mission Profile

The evaluator briefs the following mission before startup. The student is given 10 minutes to review the mission parameters and build a kneecard.

```
SAMPLE CHECK RIDE MISSION BRIEF

Callsign: HUEY 01
Takeoff: [Airfield or helipad per DCS mission setup]
Mission: Complete the BQT check ride profile

1. Startup and ground checks at the ramp
2. Hover taxi to the departure pad; hover check prior to departure
3. Normal takeoff; climb to 1,500 ft AGL
4. Navigate dead reckoning to CHECKPOINT ALPHA (IP-briefed bearing + ~10 NM)
5. Return to the airfield; fly 2 traffic patterns
   - First pattern: full-stop landing; student decides to go-around on pattern 2 if applicable
   - Student calls go-around independently if approach is unstable
6. Proceed to confined area (or slope, at IP's election)
7. Return to airfield
8. IP introduces at least 2 emergencies (timing not disclosed; minimum 1 autorotation)
9. Normal landing, hover taxi to ramp
10. Normal shutdown

Grading altitude/airspeed references:
- Hover: 3–5 ft / ±0 KIAS
- Cruise: 1,500 ft ±150 ft / 80–90 KIAS ±10
- Traffic pattern: 500 ft ±100 ft
- Landing spot tolerance: ±15 ft of intended landing point

Weather: [DCS mission setup — evaluate at or below 2,000 ft base]
```

## Part 1 — Startup and Ground Operations

### Evaluation Standard

The student performs the complete startup checklist from memory, using the checklist card only to verify — not as a step-by-step read-ahead. The evaluator observes but does not assist.

| Task | Standard | Bust Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-start checks completed | All switches verified before start | Missing battery/fuel valve = Unsatisfactory |
| Starter engagement | Starter held until self-sustaining; released correctly | Early release causing failed start = Marginal |
| Throttle advance (idle stop) | Idle stop released (`LShift + PgDn`) before throttle advance | Advancing throttle without releasing idle stop = Unsatisfactory |
| EGT monitoring | Student calls out EGT; recognizes hot start threshold (720°C) | Not monitoring EGT = Marginal |
| Post-start sequence | Generator ON → Governor ON → RPM Warning ON → gauge checks | Out of order = Marginal; missing generator/governor = Unsatisfactory |
| Before-takeoff checks | Completed before departing the parking spot | Skipping the hover check = Marginal |

## Part 2 — Hover and Hover Taxi

### Evaluation Standards

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pick-up to hover** | Smooth 3–5 ft hover within 5 ft of desired spot | Hover at 6–10 ft or drifted 6–15 ft from spot | >10 ft altitude; >15 ft lateral drift; ground contact during pick-up |
| **Heading** | ±10° of assigned heading | ±11–20° | >20° or loss of heading control |
| **Hover stability** | Stable hover ≥5 seconds without drift | Controlled drift; recovers before 10 ft deviation | Uncontrolled drift; requires IP input |
| **Power check** | Torque noted verbally; within limits | Torque not verbalized but within limits | Torque >40 PSI in hover; or failed to check |
| **Hover taxi** | Follows designated taxi path; maintains 3–5 ft; transitions smoothly | Altitude ±2 ft; minor deviations from path | Ground contact; extreme altitude excursion |

### Hover Check (Before Departure)

Before departing hover taxi for takeoff, the student must verbalize:

> *"Hover power is [X] PSI torque. Available power margin is [Y] PSI. [GO / NO-GO for the planned flight profile]."*

## Part 3 — Takeoff and Cruise Flight

### Takeoff

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Takeoff direction** | Into wind (or assigned direction) | ±15° of assigned | >20° deviation |
| **Acceleration through ETL** | Smooth power; controlled nose attitude; no ETL bump/porpoising | Slight porpoise through ETL | Abrupt ETL transition; mast bumping risk indicated |
| **Climb airspeed** | 60 KIAS climbing; 80 KIAS cruise | ±15 KIAS | >25 KIAS deviation |
| **Vne** | Never exceeded | — | Any Vne exceedance = Unsatisfactory |

### Cruise Navigation

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cruise altitude** | 1,500 ft ±150 ft | ±151–300 ft | >300 ft deviation without recovery |
| **Cruise airspeed** | 80–90 KIAS ±10 | ±11–20 KIAS | >20 KIAS deviation |
| **Level turns** | Bank ≤30°; altitude ±150 ft; no skid | Bank >30°; altitude ±200 ft | Bank >45°; altitude deviation >300 ft |
| **Force trim use** | Trim used appropriately for steady-state flight | Trim used inconsistently | Failure to trim resulting in pilot fatigue/oscillations |
| **Scan** | Instrument scan maintained throughout; HALT/T scan verbalized | Scan intermittent | Spatial disorientation; fixation on one instrument |

## Part 4 — Navigation Leg

| Task | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Kneecard** | Completed before flight; displayed during navigation leg | Must show: departure heading, checkpoint bearing, distance, ETE, altitude |
| **Departure heading** | Correct heading from departure point | Within ±10° |
| **Checkpoint identification** | Identifies IP-designated checkpoint within acceptable range | Within ±1 NM or within visual range at 1,500 ft AGL |
| **Radio navigation (if assigned)** | Tunes and correctly interprets ADF or VOR | Correct frequency; correct bearing interpretation |
| **Dead reckoning** | Corrects heading for drift; adjusts ETA based on track error | Reasonable corrections; not "random steering" |
| **Position reporting** | Provides position report when requested | Correct format: callsign, position, altitude, intentions |

## Part 5 — Landings and Pattern Work

### Traffic Pattern

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pattern altitude** | 500 ft ±100 ft | ±101–150 ft | >150 ft deviation |
| **Downwind airspeed** | 80–90 KIAS ±10 | ±11–20 KIAS | >20 KIAS deviation |
| **Radio calls** | All required calls on proper frequency | Missing one call | No calls; wrong frequency |
| **Final approach** | Stabilized on glidepath; controlled deceleration | Minor deviations; recovers | Unstable approach not corrected; no self-initiated go-around |

### Normal Approach to Hover

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Landing spot** | Within ±15 ft of intended point | ±16–25 ft | >25 ft; missed point entirely |
| **Hover altitude** | 3–5 ft skid height at termination | 6–8 ft | >8 ft at termination or hard contact |
| **ETL transition** | Smooth collective increase; controlled decel | Slight power oscillation | Power deficiency through ETL; aircraft falls through glidepath |
| **Touchdown** | Both skids simultaneously; no lateral drift | One skid first; minor drift | Hard contact; significant lateral drift |

### Go-Around Decision

> **The evaluator is looking for the student to self-initiate a go-around from an unstable approach.** If the student does not call a go-around on an approach that is clearly unstable (high, fast, or significantly off-centerline), the evaluator will note this as Unsatisfactory on "judgment."

| Task | Satisfactory |
|---|---|
| **Self-initiated go-around** | Student calls "Going around" independently when approach is unstable; correct sequence |
| **IP-directed go-around** | Clean execution; correct sequence; no altitude loss below entry altitude |

## Part 6 — Confined Area or Slope Landing

> **At evaluator's discretion — one of the following will be evaluated. The student does not know which in advance.**

### Confined Area (If Selected)

| Task | Standard | Bust Condition |
|---|---|---|
| High recon | Performed; obstacles, wind, approach/departure paths verbalized | No recon = Unsatisfactory |
| Low recon | Approach path flown; wire hazard addressed | Skipped = Unsatisfactory |
| Power check | Hover torque checked; GO/NO-GO verbalized | Skipped = Marginal |
| Approach | Steep approach; obstacle clearance adequate; VRS avoided | VRS entry = Unsatisfactory |
| Landing | Stable hover; touchdown within zone | Uncontrolled landing = Unsatisfactory |
| Escape route | Verbalized before committing | Not verbalized = Marginal |

### Slope Landing (If Selected)

| Task | Standard | Bust Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Approach | From uphill side; into wind | Approaching from downhill = Unsatisfactory |
| Set-down | Uphill skid contacts first; slow collective reduction | Downhill skid first = Marginal |
| Dynamic rollover | No roll excursion; correct response if roll tendency begins | Roll >10°/sec without collective-down response = Unsatisfactory |
| Departure | Uphill skid lifts last; controlled liftoff | Abrupt liftoff; lateral excursion = Marginal |

## Part 7 — Emergency Procedures (IP-Initiated)

> **The evaluator will introduce at least 2 emergencies without warning. The student must respond correctly from memory — no boldface checklist reference during the emergency.**

### Emergency 1 — Autorotation Entry (Required)

> The evaluator will close the DCS engine failure trigger (or verbally call "engine failure") from one of the following conditions: cruise flight, pattern, or approach.

| Task | Satisfactory | Marginal | Unsatisfactory |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Collective** | Lowered within **2 seconds** | 2–3 seconds | >3 seconds; Nr drops below 294 RPM |
| **Throttle** | Closed correctly | Delayed >2 sec after collective | Not closed; autorotation flown with engine running |
| **Airspeed** | 65 KIAS established within 5 seconds | 55–70 KIAS | <50 or >80 KIAS; uncontrolled airspeed |
| **Nr** | Maintained 294–324 RPM | Momentary 290–293 RPM | <290 RPM; or >345 RPM |
| **Landing area selection** | Suitable area identified within 15 seconds | Identified late; suboptimal but safe | No area selected; IP must redirect |
| **Flare** | 75–100 ft AGL | ±20 ft | <50 ft or >120 ft |
| **Power recovery** | Level attitude; smooth collective pull | Abrupt pull; slight hard contact | Hard landing; Nr depleted before contact |

### Emergency 2 — IP's Choice

The evaluator selects one of the following based on prior performance in Events 2–7:

| Option | What the Student Must Demonstrate |
|---|---|
| **LTE** | Forward cyclic + reduce collective; yaw controlled; airspeed gained |
| **VRS** | Forward cyclic + lower collective; airspeed above 30 KIAS; recovery completed |
| **Governor failure** | Gov OFF; throttle manual; Nr 324 maintained for minimum 2 minutes; plan to land |
| **Hydraulic failure** | Speed adjusted to 60–80 KIAS; run-on landing planned and executed |
| **Engine fire** | Boldface in order: throttle close, fuel shutoff, cabin heat, autorotate, battery |
| **Tail rotor failure** | Boldface in order: autorotate, maintain airspeed above ETL, close throttle for run-on |

## Part 8 — Shutdown

The student performs normal shutdown from the final landing without prompting.

| Task | Standard | Bust Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Cooldown period** | 2 minutes at idle minimum; student times and verbalizes | Skipping cooldown = Marginal |
| **Shutdown sequence** | Throttle cutoff → EGT monitor → Nr/N1 monitor → wait for rotor stop → fuel valve off → generator off → battery off → all switches off | Missing battery or fuel valve step = Marginal; shutting down with rotor spinning at speed = Unsatisfactory |
| **Rotor stop** | Remains seated until rotor fully stopped | Exiting before rotor stops = Unsatisfactory |
| **All switches off** | All confirmed OFF; verified | Switches left on = Marginal |

## Common Bust Conditions

| Bust Condition | Reason |
|---|---|
| Boldface incorrect during oral | Must be 100% — these are the lifesaving responses |
| Nr drops below 294 RPM during autorotation | Means the student did not lower collective in time; energy is lost; real-world hard landing or crash |
| Vne exceeded | Structural limit; automatic Unsatisfactory regardless of how brief |
| No self-initiated go-around from an unstable approach | Demonstrates inability to exercise judgment independently — the most important single skill |
| Confined area landing without recon | Entering an unknown area without obstacle/wind/escape route assessment — unacceptable in any conditions |
| IP intervention required to prevent ground contact, VRS entry, or loss of control | The IP should never touch the controls during a check ride |
| Rotor not fully stopped before exiting the aircraft | Tail rotor at low RPM is invisible and can kill ground personnel; discipline is absolute |

## Debrief Format

The evaluator debriefs the student immediately after the flight using the grading matrix:

1. **Admin** — State overall result (Pass / Partial Bust / Bust) at the beginning, not the end. The student deserves to know the result before the detailed review.
2. **Strengths** — Note 2–3 specific areas of demonstrated proficiency.
3. **Task-by-task review** — Work through the grading matrix in order; explain any Marginal or Unsatisfactory grades with specific examples from the flight.
4. **Priority items** — If a re-ride is required, identify exactly which tasks must be re-demonstrated and to what standard.
5. **Recommendation** — If passed: sign off on BQT completion and recommend student for solo currency or next qualification step. If busted: prescribe specific additional training events before re-ride.
