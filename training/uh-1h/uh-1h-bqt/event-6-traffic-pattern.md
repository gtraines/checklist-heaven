## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Fly the complete UH-1H traffic pattern (upwind, crosswind, downwind, base, final) at the correct altitudes and airspeeds, making all required radio calls
- [ ] Execute a normal approach from the pattern to a stable hover at 3–5 ft over the intended landing point, stabilizing within ±10 ft of the target
- [ ] Recognize an unstable approach and execute a go-around from any point on final, including from below 100 ft AGL
- [ ] Perform a steep approach at ~12–15° to a confined area or simulated obstacle-bordered landing zone
- [ ] Execute a shallow/running landing when directed (power-limited scenario), maintaining translational lift until touchdown
- [ ] Perform a crosswind approach; demonstrate appropriate crab or sideslip technique near the ground
- [ ] Execute a slope landing on a left slope and right slope; describe the dynamic rollover hazard and prevention
- [ ] Describe confined area recon requirements (high recon, low recon, obstacle identification, escape route, power check) before committing to a landing
- [ ] Perform a night approach using the radar altimeter and landing light for altitude reference

## Ground Brief

### Mission Profile

1. Standard startup and pre-takeoff checks
2. Departure and traffic pattern — normal approach to hover and landing (minimum 3 patterns)
3. Deliberate go-around practice — one IP-initiated at low altitude
4. Steep approach to a designated simulated confined area
5. Running landing (IP assigns scenario: power-limited condition)
6. Crosswind pattern (use a simulated or DCS-generated crosswind condition)
7. Slope landing (find or simulate a sloped surface; at least one left slope)
8. Confined area — student performs recon, identifies escape route, conducts approach and landing
9. Night approach (if time permits — transition to night; one approach with landing light)
10. Shutdown

### Key Numbers Table

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Pattern altitude** | 500 ft AGL | Standard helicopter pattern |
| **Upwind turn** | 200–300 ft AGL | Begin crosswind turn after clear of runway |
| **Downwind airspeed** | 80–90 KIAS | Level flight; 500–800 ft lateral offset from runway |
| **Base airspeed** | 60–70 KIAS | Decelerating; begin descent |
| **Final approach (entry)** | 50–60 KIAS | Aligning with landing spot |
| **ETL transition** | ~16–24 KIAS | Increase collective here; expect nose-up pitch |
| **Hover over landing spot** | 3–5 ft skid height | Stabilize before touchdown |
| **Normal approach angle** | 6–10° | Visual angle; aim point should be ~60–80% down the glidepath angle |
| **Steep approach angle** | 12–15° | More aggressive; higher rate of descent |
| **Shallow/running approach** | 3–6° | Maintains airspeed and translational lift to touchdown |
| **Max slope (UH-1H)** | ~5–7° | Field-manual limit for slope landings |
| **Torque continuous max** | 40 PSI | Monitor throughout all approach phases |
| **Vne** | 124 KIAS (sea level) | Do not exceed on any downwind leg |

### Approach Angle Visual Reference (Normal)

A 6–10° approach angle has the following practical visual cues:
- The aim point (landing spot) appears **below** the center of your windshield.
- If the aim point appears to be rising in the windshield, you are too shallow.
- If it appears to be dropping out of view below the glidepath, you are too steep.
- The aim point should remain stationary relative to your windshield — if it moves, adjust.

## Part 1 — Traffic Pattern and Normal Approach to Hover

### Pattern Legs — Altitudes and Airspeeds

| Leg | Altitude AGL | Airspeed | Turn | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Upwind** | Climbing 200 → 500 ft | 60 KIAS → 80 KIAS | — | Normal takeoff; climb straight ahead |
| **Crosswind** | Level at 500 ft | 70–80 KIAS | 90° from upwind | Level off; pattern altitude |
| **Downwind** | 500 ft | 80–90 KIAS | Parallel to runway | ~500–800 ft lateral offset; make downwind call |
| **Base** | Descending from 500 ft | 60–70 KIAS decelerating | 90° toward landing area | Begin gradual descent; reduce collective |
| **Final** | Descending to hover | 50–60 KIAS → hover | Aligned with landing spot | Aim at landing point; controlled deceleration |

### Pattern Entry (From the Training Area)

1. Enter the pattern at a **45° angle to the downwind leg** at pattern altitude (500 ft AGL).
2. Contact tower (VHF-AM) before entering: *"[Tower], [Callsign], [position], request full stop landing, runway [X]."*
3. Fly the downwind leg until abeam the landing point.
4. Reduce power and begin deceleration on base.

### Normal Approach — Step-by-Step

> Begin from the base-to-final turn.

| Step | Phase | Action | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Base turn** | Reduce collective slightly; turn toward landing area | Speed reduces to ~60 KIAS; descent begins |
| 2 | **Final alignment** | Roll wings level aligned with landing spot | Aim point in windshield below center |
| 3 | **Initial final** (300 ft, ~60 KIAS) | Set approach power; aim at landing spot | 6–10° glidepath; controlled descent |
| 4 | **Short final** (100 ft, ~30–40 KIAS) | Begin decelerating through ETL | **Increase collective progressively** as translational lift diminishes |
| 5 | **ETL transition** (~20 KIAS) | Power demand increases; nose pitches up | Forward cyclic to prevent nose-high; left pedal for increasing torque; continuous collective increase |
| 6 | **Termination to hover** | Arrive at 3–5 ft over the landing spot at ~0 KIAS | Collective holding hover altitude; position centered over target |
| 7 | **Stabilize** | Hold hover for ≥5 seconds before touchdown | ±10 ft of intended spot; altitude 3–5 ft |
| 8 | **Touchdown** | Slowly lower collective | Both skids contact simultaneously; no lateral drift at touchdown |

### After Landing Radio Call

> *"[Airfield] Tower, [Callsign], runway [X], clear. [Request taxi or next pattern request]."*

## Part 2 — Go-Around (Wave-Off)

> **The go-around must be an immediate, unconditional response to a recognition of an unstable approach. Practice early go-arounds (from downwind, base) and late go-arounds (from below 100 ft AGL).**

### Go-Around Criteria

A go-around is required any time:
- The approach is unstable (too fast, too slow, too high, too low) by the base-to-final turn
- Obstacle or traffic conflict develops
- Crosswind exceeds aircraft capability
- Power required approaching power available (aircraft sinking despite full collective)
- Any doubt whatsoever about the safety of the approach

### Go-Around Procedure

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Decision** — call "Go around" verbally | Announced clearly; no hesitation |
| 2 | **Collective** — smoothly increase to climb power | Watch torque — do not exceed 40 PSI; do not snatch collective |
| 3 | **Cyclic** — lower nose slightly to accelerate | Establish forward speed; prevent settling |
| 4 | **Pedals** — left pedal as power increases | Heading maintained |
| 5 | **Airspeed** — accelerate to 60 KIAS (Vy) | Positive climb rate; obstacles clearing |
| 6 | **Announce on radio** | *"[Callsign], going around"* — tower acknowledges |
| 7 | **Re-enter pattern** | Climb to pattern altitude; request another approach or make pattern calls |

### IP-Initiated Late Go-Around

The IP will call "go-around" once during the event from below 100 ft AGL while the aircraft is on a valid approach. Student must execute immediately and correctly. This tests the student's ability to go around from a low altitude without fixating on the landing and forcing the approach.

> **"Go around early, go around often." There is no penalty for a go-around. There is a severe penalty for forcing a bad approach.**

## Part 3 — Steep Approach

> **Used for:** Confined areas with obstacles on the approach path; limited overshoot area.

### Approach Profile

| Phase | Altitude | Airspeed | Angle | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Final entry** | ~400 ft AGL | 50–60 KIAS | Begin steeper glidepath | Higher than normal approach entry relative to distance |
| **Mid-approach** | ~200 ft AGL | 40–50 KIAS | 12–15° | Rate of descent ~700–1,000 fpm |
| **Short final** | ~100 ft AGL | 30–40 KIAS | — | Decelerate; power demand increasing rapidly |
| **Termination** | 3–5 ft hover | 0 KIAS | 0° | Stable hover over landing point |

### Procedure

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify landing area and obstacles on approach; note commit point | Plan go-around path before starting approach |
| 2 | Establish steep approach angle; reduce airspeed early | Do not exceed ~60 KIAS at approach entry |
| 3 | Monitor rate of descent — higher than normal (700–1,000 fpm initially) | This is expected; manage with collective |
| 4 | As airspeed decreases, **aggressively raise collective** to arrest rate of descent | Power demand is higher than normal approach due to steeper angle |
| 5 | **Maintain airspeed above 30 KIAS until final deceleration to hover** | Below 30 KIAS with high descent rate = VRS entry risk |
| 6 | Final deceleration — transition to hover | More aggressive flare required than normal approach |
| 7 | Stabilize hover → touchdown | As normal approach |

**VRS Hazard during Steep Approach:**
- High descent rate + low airspeed + moderate-to-high power = **VRS entry conditions**.
- The steep approach brings the aircraft close to these conditions.
- If the aircraft begins to sink and **does not respond to increasing collective**, VRS may have entered.
- **Recovery:** Apply forward cyclic immediately to gain airspeed above 30 KIAS. Do not continue adding collective.

**Commit Point:**
The commit point is the altitude/position below which a go-around becomes increasingly hazardous due to obstacles. Identify it before the approach and declare it: *"Commit point is [X] ft AGL at the tree line."* Below this point, a landing is the primary plan; go-around is possible but requires clearing the obstacles behind you.

## Part 4 — Shallow / Running Landing

> **Condition:** IP assigns a "power-limited" scenario. The student must recognize that hover power is not available and execute a running landing.

### Scenario Brief

Student is told: "For this approach, assume hover power is not available — you have insufficient power margin to terminate to a hover. Execute a running landing."

### Procedure

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Plan a normal approach profile but with a shallower angle (~3–6°) | Aim for the approach end of the landing surface |
| 2 | On final, maintain a **slightly higher airspeed** than normal | Do not decelerate to hover — plan to touch down with forward speed |
| 3 | Maintain airspeed ~15–25 KIAS on short final | Translational lift maintained to near-touchdown |
| 4 | At ~5–10 ft AGL with ~15–20 knots ground speed — allow skids to contact the ground | Both skids contact simultaneously with forward motion |
| 5 | After touchdown — **lower collective fully** | Do not attempt to hover; let the aircraft slide forward on the skids |
| 6 | Skids decelerate on the surface until stopped | Maintain heading with pedals; keep cyclic centered |

**Hazards:**
- Surface must be smooth and firm — running landings on soft ground can cause skids to dig in and the aircraft to pitch forward.
- Maintain a straight ground track during the roll-out — lateral skid catch can cause dynamic rollover.
- Do not attempt to "save it" with a hover at the end of the run — if insufficient power, commit to the run-on.

## Part 5 — Crosswind Approach

### Crosswind Hover Technique

| Wind Source | Correction | Pedal Input |
|---|---|---|
| **Wind from left** | Right cyclic to prevent left drift | Left pedal (wind reduces tail rotor demand from behind; slightly less pedal needed) |
| **Wind from right** | Left cyclic to prevent right drift | Right pedal (wind increases tail rotor demand) |
| **Wind from front** | Aft cyclic to prevent forward drift | Normal left pedal for torque |
| **Wind from rear** | Forward cyclic to prevent rearward drift | **Highest LTE risk** — avoid hovering with tailwind when possible |

### Crosswind Approach Technique

| Phase | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Downwind** | Correct for wind to maintain ground track parallel to runway | Crab angle on downwind; maintain 500 ft offset |
| **Base** | Turn earlier or later to compensate for wind on base | Crosswind on base affects distance to final |
| **Final** | **Crab into the wind** to maintain ground track aligned with landing spot | Crab angle holds ground track; aircraft heading is offset |
| **Short final** | Transition from crab to **sideslip** below ~50 ft | Lower the upwind cyclic; hold heading with opposite pedal — aircraft aligned with landing area, correcting for drift |
| **Touchdown** | **Upwind skid contacts first** | Then lower downwind skid; lower collective to plant aircraft; do not allow aircraft to slide sideways |

> **If you run out of pedal in a crosswind hover**, the wind exceeds the aircraft's anti-torque capability for that direction. Do not force it — turn the nose into the wind or execute a go-around.

## Part 6 — Slope Landing (Left and Right Slope)

> **Max safe slope for UH-1H: approximately 5–7°.** Do not attempt steeper slopes.

### Left Slope Landing (Left Skid Uphill)

| Step | Action | Critical Monitoring |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Approach from the uphill side; approach into the wind | Uphill side approach |
| 2 | Terminate to a hover **above and slightly uphill** of the landing point | 3–5 ft above the surface |
| 3 | Very slowly lower collective | Aircraft descends toward the sloped surface |
| 4 | **Uphill (left) skid contacts ground first** | Maintain fuselage level with right cyclic as the left skid settles |
| 5 | Continue slowly lowering collective | Right (downhill) skid descends toward surface; apply left cyclic to keep fuselage level |
| 6 | **Both skids on the ground** | Collective continues down to fully seat the aircraft; roll tendency must be monitored continuously |
| 7 | **Full collective down** — aircraft firmly on slope | Engine at idle; aircraft seated |

**Dynamic Rollover Prevention:**

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Aircraft begins to roll downhill (right) during set-down | **Immediately lower collective** to seat both skids; do not fight with cyclic alone |
| Roll rate building | **Collective DOWN** — this is the priority; not cyclic correction |
| Roll rate exceeds ~10°/sec | **Collective to full down immediately** — cyclic alone cannot stop it at this rate |

> **Rule:** If the aircraft begins to roll on a slope, **collective DOWN** is the immediate response. Raising collective to try to escape will only increase the roll tendency.

### Right Slope Landing (Right Skid Uphill)

Mirror of left slope procedure. Additional consideration:
- **Tail rotor clearance:** The tail rotor is on the left side of the tail boom. On a right slope, the tail rotor hangs lower on the left (downhill) side. Verify sufficient clearance from the terrain before setting down.

### Slope Departure

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Collective up slowly — aircraft lifts **uphill skid first** (uphill skid stays on the ground until uphill side lifts) |
| 2 | Continue raising collective — aircraft lifts to a hover above slope |
| 3 | Maintain wings level with cyclic; pedals for heading |
| 4 | Once in a clear hover above the slope, transition to normal departure |

## Part 7 — Confined Area Operations

> **No confined area landing without a completed recon. The IP will not allow a landing unless the student has verbalized all required recon elements.**

### Recon Sequence

| Phase | Altitude | Action | Required Verbalization |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Recon** | 500–1,000 ft AGL | Orbit or overfly the area; assess the big picture | "Wind from [direction]. LZ appears [suitable/unsuitable]. Primary obstacles: [identify]. Approach path: [direction]. Departure path: [direction]." |
| **Low Recon** | Above obstacles (~100–200 ft AGL above obstacles) | Fly the intended approach path; confirm obstacle heights and clearances | "Approach path [clear/blocked at X]. LZ surface appears [firm/soft/sloped]. Wires: [present/absent]. Escape route: [direction]." |
| **Power check** | IGE hover prior to commitment | Check hover torque vs. max continuous | "Hover torque [X] PSI, margin [Y] PSI, [GO/NO-GO]." |
| **Commit** | Beginning final approach | Final verbal commitment | "I am committing to this landing. Escape route is [X]." |

**Wire awareness:** Wires are nearly invisible in DCS and in real life. Assume wires exist near any road, building, or utility pole on the approach path. If you cannot confirm wires are absent, treat the approach path as wire-present.

### Confined Area Approach

After recon, use a **steep approach** as the standard technique:
- The steep approach minimizes the time spent over the obstacle-bounded area.
- Decelerate to hover over the landing spot.
- If any element becomes unsatisfactory — **go-around on the escape route**.

## Part 8 — Night Approach (Introduction)

> **Single night approach; instructor demonstrates first. Requires transition to DCS night time setting.**

### Night Approach Setup

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Switch DCS time to night (if not already night in mission) |
| 2 | Activate **Landing Light** (`LCtrl + L`) | Illuminates the approach path and landing area |
| 3 | Set **Radar Altimeter bug** to 50 ft AGL | Warning triggers below this altitude — additional cue on approach |
| 4 | Reduce cockpit lighting to a comfortable level | Avoid washing out night vision |

### Night Approach Technique

| Phase | Primary Reference | Secondary Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Pattern** | Outside lights (runway, VASI if present, horizon) | Instruments |
| **Final** | Landing light illumination of terrain | Airspeed, radar altimeter |
| **Short final** | Landing point illuminated by landing light | Radar altimeter AGL (key reference) |
| **Termination to hover** | Visual reference from landing light + radar altimeter | 3–5 ft — trust the radar altimeter |
| **Touchdown** | Visual and feel | Lower collective when radar altimeter reads 0–2 ft |

**Key principle:** Depth perception is significantly reduced at night. The **radar altimeter is the authoritative altitude reference** on approach below 200 ft AGL. Do not trust your visual altitude judgment alone — cross-check with the radar altimeter throughout.

**Night go-around threshold:** Go around earlier at night than during the day. Any uncertainty about the approach profile at night is a go-around.

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Traffic pattern** | All legs flown at correct altitudes (±100 ft) and airspeeds (±10 KIAS); all required radio calls made |
| **Normal approach** | Stabilized hover within ±10 ft of the intended landing spot; altitude 3–5 ft at hover; smooth touchdown — no hard contact, no lateral drift |
| **Minimum patterns** | Student performs minimum 3 complete patterns to a full-stop landing, with go-arounds counted toward traffic pattern practice but not toward landing count |
| **Go-around** | Executes without hesitation from any IP-initiated call; correct sequence (collective, cyclic, pedal, airspeed, radio); no altitude loss below the entry hover altitude |
| **Steep approach** | Approach angle visually steeper than normal; arrives at stable hover over target; no VRS symptoms (sinking without response to collective); commit point identified verbally before approach |
| **Running landing** | Translational lift maintained to touchdown; no bounce or hard contact; controlled roll-out |
| **Slope landing** | Uphill skid touches first; aircraft seats without roll excursion; correct dynamic rollover prevention verbalized |
| **Confined area** | All recon phases verbalized before committing; obstacles and escape route identified; power check performed; approach completed without entering VRS or exceeding power limits |
| **Night approach** | Radar altimeter used as primary altitude reference below 200 ft AGL; landing light used; stable hover established before touchdown |

> **Go/No-Go for Event 7:** Student must demonstrate a stable normal approach (hover within ±10 ft of spot) independently on at least 2 of 3 evaluated patterns, and must successfully complete a go-around from below 100 ft AGL. Unsafe approaches or inability to execute a go-around will require repetition of Event 6 before proceeding.

## Common Errors

| Error | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Approaching too fast on final | Too much speed at the bottom; overshoots landing spot | Begin slowing on base turn — not at the end of final |
| Not adding collective through ETL | Aircraft falls through the glidepath on short final; undershoots | Brief every approach: "At 20 knots, pull collective. Don't wait." Practice this at altitude (slow flight) |
| Not going around on an unstable approach | Forced landing; possible obstacle or overshoot | Positive reinforcement every time the student goes around. "Good decision." Make the threshold for go-around early and clearly defined |
| VRS entry during steep approach | Aircraft sinks despite collective; may not recover at low altitude | Keep airspeed above 30 KIAS until final deceleration; if sinking without response → forward cyclic immediately |
| Raising collective during dynamic rollover | Roll accelerates | "Roll starts — collective DOWN. That is the only correct action." Drill repeatedly in briefing |
| No recon before confined area | Landing in an unknown area; wire strikes, surface problems, power-limited situation | Enforce the verbalization: if the student cannot state wind, obstacles, surface, and escape route, the approach does not begin |
| Looking inside on night approach | Spatial disorientation; lose visual references | "Eyes outside for references; scan down to radar altimeter every 3 seconds on approach" |
