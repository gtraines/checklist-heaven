## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Pick up to a stable 3–5 ft hover from the ground and maintain position, heading, and altitude within ±2 ft altitude, ±10° heading, and ±3 ft lateral drift over a 30-second evaluation period
- [ ] Describe the hover scan pattern and demonstrate its use — approximately 80% outside references, 20% instruments
- [ ] Perform a hover power check; state the current hover torque, the continuous limit, and the power margin; make a GO or NO-GO declaration
- [ ] Execute 360° left and right pedal turns in a stationary hover while maintaining position within 3 ft and altitude within ±2 ft
- [ ] Explain LTE awareness during pedal turns and state the immediate recovery action for an uncommanded yaw rate
- [ ] Perform forward hover taxi with ground track within 5 ft of centerline for a minimum 200 ft
- [ ] Demonstrate and describe the hazards and required crew coordination for sideward and rearward hover taxi
- [ ] Execute a normal takeoff — from hover through ETL to climbing flight at 60 KIAS (Vy) — with coordinated control inputs through the ETL transition
- [ ] Describe the no-hover takeoff and running takeoff techniques and state the conditions that require each

## Ground Brief

### Mission Profile

1. Engine start (reference Event 2 procedure — abbreviated callouts expected)
2. Pick-up to hover — stabilization exercise (10 min minimum)
3. Hover power check
4. Pedal turns — 360° left and right
5. Forward hover taxi — 200 ft min each direction
6. Sideward hover taxi — 50 ft each direction (instructor demonstration first)
7. Rearward hover taxi — 30 ft (instructor demonstration first)
8. Normal takeoff — depart to the traffic pattern entry or a designated practice area
9. IP demonstration of no-hover takeoff and running takeoff (student observation)
10. Return and land; shutdown per Event 2 procedure

### Key Numbers Table

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Nr normal** | 321–327 RPM | Dual tach needles married |
| **Nr low warning** | 294 RPM | Audio tone — lower collective or increase throttle |
| **Torque continuous max** | 40 PSI | Do not exceed without intention |
| **Hover altitude (training)** | 3–5 ft skid height | IGE; consistent height required |
| **ETL onset** | ~16–24 KIAS | Aircraft gets light; nose pitches up; left yaw |
| **Vy (best rate of climb)** | 60 KIAS | Normal departure climb speed |
| **Hover scan ratio** | 80% outside / 20% instruments | Primary reference is always outside |
| **Stabilizer bar lag** | ~0.5–1 sec | Input → disc response delay; wait before correcting |
| **LTE risk wind azimuth** | 210°–330° relative | Highest risk during right pedal turns in hover |
| **Sideward/Rearward speed limit** | ~20 knots | Structural limit for non-forward hover flight |

### Control Principles — Briefing Points

Before flying, brief these foundational control principles:

**Four-control coordination:**
- Collective up → more torque → more left pedal needed
- Collective down → less torque → more right pedal needed
- Forward cyclic → nose dips, aircraft accelerates forward
- Aft cyclic → nose rises, aircraft decelerates

**Stabilizer bar behavior:**
- The stabilizer bar delays rotor disc response to cyclic inputs by ~0.5–1 second.
- Make an input; wait; observe the result; then decide if correction is needed.
- Inputs made before the aircraft responds to the previous input cause oscillations.

**Hover eyes:**
- Primary reference: a fixed point on the ground **50–100 ft ahead**.
- Do not look at the ground immediately below the aircraft — this causes fixation and loss of positional awareness.
- Peripheral vision detects drift, altitude change, and heading change.

## Part 1 — Pick-Up to Hover

> **Prerequisites:** Engine at flight RPM; before-takeoff check complete; rotor 324 RPM; all caution lights out.

| Step | Action | Control Inputs | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Choose reference point** — pick a spot on the ground or ahead 50–100 ft | Visual setup | Reference point confirmed; will use for hover position reference |
| 2 | **Cyclic** — centered; pre-trim for hover | Centered or slight right (for left translating tendency) | Cyclic neutral |
| 3 | **Pedals** — slight left of center (anticipate torque) | Pre-position left pedal | Heading will be easier to hold when torque increases |
| 4 | **Collective** — begin slow, smooth raise | 1 inch/second rate maximum | Torque begins to rise; aircraft becomes light on skids |
| 5 | Continuously add **left pedal** as collective rises | Pedals track collective | Heading maintained; nose not yawing right |
| 6 | **Aircraft becomes light on skids** | Collective still rising slowly | Aircraft rocks slightly; skids still on or barely touching |
| 7 | Continue collective until **skids clear** — ~1–2 ft | Small collective increase | Aircraft airborne; small position corrections begin |
| 8 | Continue collective to **3–5 ft** skid height | Smooth increase | 3–5 ft established; avoid overshooting to 10+ ft |
| 9 | **Stabilize** — hold altitude, heading, and position | Constant small corrections on all axes | Reference point stable in view; horizon level; altitude steady |

> **If the aircraft climbs past 5 ft:** Do not dump the collective. Gently reduce collective; maintain heading with pedals; wait for the aircraft to stabilize at the correct height.
>
> **If the aircraft drifts:** Small opposite cyclic; wait for the response (stabilizer bar lag); correct again if needed. Do not chase drift aggressively.

## Part 2 — Hover Stabilization

> **Minimum 10 minutes** of hover practice before moving to power check. No departure during this phase.

### Hover Scan Pattern

```
Reference Point (50-100 ft ahead)
        ↕
   Peripheral Vision (drift, altitude)
        ↕
  Brief Instrument Check (Nr, torque, EGT)
        ↕
   Back to Reference Point
```

**Scan ratio:** Approximately 80% outside; 20% instruments. Return to the reference point every 2–3 seconds.

### Hover Control Technique

| Axis | Correction Technique | Common Error |
|---|---|---|
| **Altitude** | Smooth collective; wait for response | Rapid collective changes cause oscillation; over-controlling height |
| **Heading** | Pedal inputs track torque changes; continuous adjustment | Delayed pedal response → nose wanders; "set and forget" pedal position |
| **Lateral position** | Cyclic; small input; wait 0.5–1 sec; assess before correcting | Chasing the aircraft with rapid lateral corrections → PIO |
| **Longitudinal position** | Cyclic fore/aft; same patience principle | Inadvertent acceleration starts from too much forward cyclic |

### Instructor Exercises — Hover Phase

| Exercise | Procedure | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| **Hands-off (IP demonstrates)** | Make a deliberate small cyclic disturbance; remove hands | Show that the aircraft settles — the stabilizer bar provides passive stability |
| **Induced oscillation recovery** | Student over-corrects and creates PIO; IP directs "hands off" | Teach: stop fighting the aircraft; let the stabilizer bar work |
| **Altitude challenge** | IP calls "2 ft" or "4 ft" — student must transition to new altitude | Practice proportional collective inputs |

## Part 3 — Hover Power Check

> **Complete before any takeoff. Required callout.**

**Procedure:**

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish stable hover at **3–5 ft** IGE | Hover must be stable before reading torque |
| 2 | Read **torque gauge** (PSI) | Note the current hover torque value |
| 3 | Note **maximum continuous torque** (40 PSI) | Aircraft limit |
| 4 | Calculate **power margin**: 40 − hover torque = margin (PSI) | Callout: "Hover torque [X] PSI, max 40 PSI, margin [Y] PSI" |
| 5 | Make **GO / NO-GO declaration** | Based on margin and planned operation |

**Power Margin Assessment:**

| Margin | Assessment | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **>5 PSI** | Good margin | Normal hover takeoff; OGE operations likely available |
| **3–5 PSI** | Marginal | Normal hover takeoff possible; OGE hover may not be available. Consider no-hover takeoff. |
| **<3 PSI** | Insufficient for normal hover ops | Running takeoff required; reduce gross weight or wait for cooler conditions; OGE hover not available |
| **At or above max continuous** | Performance limited | Cannot sustain hover; running takeoff mandatory; consider mission abort if obstacles require OGE climb |

**OGE Check (when required by mission):**
1. From stable IGE hover, slowly climb vertically.
2. Monitor torque continuously during ascent.
3. At ~24 ft AGL (½ rotor diameter), ground effect is minimal — this is the OGE hover torque requirement.
4. If torque reaches max continuous before 24 ft → OGE hover not available at current conditions.

## Part 4 — Hover Turns (Pedal Turns)

> **Start with left turns only. Right pedal turns added after left turn proficiency is demonstrated.**

### Left Pedal Turn (Nose Left)

| Step | Action | Expected Result | Coordination |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | From stable hover — apply smooth **left pedal** | Nose begins turning left | Slow, controlled input — not a stomp |
| 2 | As nose rotates left, **slight right cyclic** | Counter tail swing (tail moves right as nose goes left) | Position over spot maintained |
| 3 | Adjust **collective** as needed | Altitude maintained | Power demand may change as tail rotor loading changes |
| 4 | Continue through 360° at steady turn rate | Complete circle returns to original heading | ~15–20 seconds for a full 360° is appropriate; faster risks position control |
| 5 | **Stop turn** by neutralizing or slight right pedal; stabilize | Nose on original heading; position held | End in the same hover position |

### Right Pedal Turn (Nose Right)

Same procedure with right pedal. Additional considerations:

- Right pedal reduces tail rotor thrust (less anti-torque needed as torque changes during the turn).
- Slightly **higher collective** may be needed during right turns — tail rotor is doing less work.
- **LTE risk:** Right pedal turn with wind from the rear quarter (210°–330° relative) can cause uncommanded acceleration of the right yaw.

### LTE Recognition and Recovery During Pedal Turns

| Indication | Meaning | Immediate Action |
|---|---|---|
| Right yaw rate increasing beyond pedal input | Entering LTE — tail rotor losing effectiveness | **Forward cyclic** — accelerate to >16 KIAS immediately; LTE does not persist in translational flight |
| Left pedal authority decreasing; right yaw accelerating | Tail rotor vortex ring state or weathercock instability | **Forward cyclic** — escape; reduce collective if altitude permits |

> **Rule:** If you cannot stop an uncommanded yaw rate with pedal, stop trying with pedal alone — apply forward cyclic to gain airspeed. Speed cures LTE.

## Part 5 — Forward, Sideward, and Rearward Hover Taxi

### Forward Hover Taxi

| Phase | Action | Key Technique |
|---|---|---|
| **Start** | Slight forward cyclic from stable hover | Nose dips ~2–3°; maintain altitude with small collective; heading with pedals |
| **Maintain** | Cyclic to steer along centerline; pedals to maintain heading | Look ahead; peripheral vision on altitude; speed ~5–10 knots |
| **Stop** | Aft cyclic to arrest motion | Expect nose-up pitch; anticipate slight collective increase to hold altitude; heading maintained |
| **Set-down** | Lower collective smoothly when stopped | Feel for skid contact; do not drop onto skids; hold heading to touchdown |

**Track standard:** Ground track within 5 ft of centerline for a minimum 200 ft of taxi.

### Sideward Hover Taxi

> **Instructor demonstration first.** Student observes before performing.

| Consideration | Technique |
|---|---|
| Direction | Lateral cyclic in desired direction; maintain heading with pedals |
| Speed | ≤20 knots (structural limit); keep to walking pace when near obstacles |
| Ground clearance | Maintain 3–5 ft — skid catch on obstacles is a dynamic rollover risk |
| Crew coordination | Pilot's view is limited laterally. **Verbally clear direction of movement** with IP before executing. In the real aircraft, crew chief provides obstacle clearance calls. |

### Rearward Hover Taxi

> **Instructor demonstration first.** Student observes before performing.

| Consideration | Technique |
|---|---|
| Direction | Gentle aft cyclic; very small speed increases |
| Speed | ≤20 knots (structural limit); minimal speed — less than 5 knots preferred |
| Tail rotor risk | Tail rotor extends well behind the fuselage and is low. **Any obstacles behind the aircraft can contact the tail rotor.** |
| Crew coordination | **Mandatory** — pilot has zero rearward visibility. Do not attempt rearward hover taxi without explicit crew clearance of the area. |
| Preferred alternative | If rearward taxi is needed for repositioning, consider a pedal turn to face the desired direction, then forward taxi. |

## Part 6 — Normal Takeoff (Hover Through ETL)

> **Prerequisites:** Stable 3–5 ft hover; power check complete and GO declared; departure path clear.**

### Normal Takeoff Procedure

| Step | Action | Expected Aircraft Response | Control Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Stable hover** — confirm heading, altitude, power margin | Aircraft stable; GO declared | No action until aircraft is fully stable |
| 2 | Smoothly apply **forward cyclic** | Nose dips; aircraft begins forward movement | Forward cyclic 2–3°; maintain altitude with collective; heading with pedals |
| 3 | Continue **accelerating** — airspeed increasing through 0–16 knots | Helicopter transitioning from hovering flight | Continuous forward cyclic pressure; maintain 3–5 ft AGL during acceleration |
| 4 | **Approaching ETL (~16–18 KIAS)** | Transverse flow effect vibration begins; aircraft starts getting light | Maintain forward cyclic; be ready for nose-up pitch |
| 5 | **Through ETL (18–24 KIAS)** | Aircraft "gets light" — translational lift onset; nose pitches up; left yaw tendency increases | **Forward cyclic** to prevent nose rise; **right pedal** to counter left yaw; **slight collective reduction** (rotor more efficient; less power needed to maintain altitude) |
| 6 | **Above ETL (24+ KIAS)** | Vibrations reduce; aircraft climbing; controls more responsive | Adjust cyclic for climb attitude; set collective for desired climb rate |
| 7 | Establish **Vy (60 KIAS)** | Climbing at 60 KIAS | Adjust cyclic to 60 KIAS; collective for climb rate (typically 500 fpm); monitor EGT and torque |
| 8 | **Positive rate of climb** confirmed | VSI shows climb; altimeter increasing | Continue climb; clear of obstacles |
| 9 | **Trim** — set force trim (`T`) for climb attitude | Magnetic brake holds attitude | Re-trim at Vy; scan engine instruments |
| 10 | **Engine instruments scan** | EGT, torque, Nr, oil P&T | All within limits; no cautions |

### ETL Transition — Control Summary

| Phase | Key Input | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-ETL | Forward cyclic (steady pressure) | Accelerate while maintaining low altitude |
| ETL onset | Maintain forward cyclic; add right pedal | Counter nose-up pitch; counter left yaw |
| ETL through | **Reduce collective slightly** | Rotor now 10–15% more efficient — less power needed; excess power causes climb above intended altitude |
| Post-ETL | Normal flight attitude; trim | Translational flight established |

> **IP Note:** Briefly pause after passing ETL to have the student verbalize what they felt and which inputs they made. This reinforces the physical understanding of the transition.

## Part 7 — No-Hover and Running Takeoff (Demonstration)

> **IP demonstrates; student observes. Student is not evaluated on these maneuvers in this event but must be able to describe each.**

### No-Hover Takeoff

**When used:** Power insufficient for sustained OGE hover but sufficient for IGE hover and acceleration.

| Step | Technique |
|---|---|
| 1 | Aircraft facing into wind; clear departure path |
| 2 | Raise collective until skids barely clear (1–2 ft) — stay at minimum altitude |
| 3 | Immediately apply forward cyclic to begin acceleration **within ground effect** |
| 4 | Remain below ~24 ft AGL until airspeed passes through ETL |
| 5 | Translational lift develops; aircraft climbs; continue acceleration to Vy |

**Key principle:** Ground effect reduces power required during the acceleration — this is the technique that makes departure possible when OGE hover power is unavailable.

### Running Takeoff

**When used:** Hover capability severely limited (high gross weight, high density altitude, extreme loads).

| Step | Technique |
|---|---|
| 1 | Aircraft on the ground, facing into wind, throttle at FLY |
| 2 | Raise collective to make aircraft light on skids — do not lift off |
| 3 | Apply forward cyclic while skids are still touching — aircraft begins to slide forward |
| 4 | As speed builds, aircraft lightens and skids skim or lift from the surface |
| 5 | Aircraft transitions through ETL and climbs in ground effect; continue to Vy |

**Risks:** Skid damage, dynamic rollover if a skid catches, requires long clear departure area.

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Hover stabilization** | Maintain ±2 ft altitude, ±10° heading, ±3 ft lateral drift over 30-second evaluation — must demonstrate this twice |
| **Hover scan** | Verbally describe the scan pattern and demonstrate it (80% outside; IP will note if student is fixating on instruments or ground below aircraft) |
| **Hover power check** | Verbally state hover torque, max continuous, and power margin before every departure; make GO/NO-GO declaration |
| **Pedal turns** | 360° left turn completed without departing hover spot by more than 5 ft; altitude within ±2 ft throughout. Right turn to same standard once left proficiency is demonstrated |
| **LTE** | State recovery action for uncommanded yaw rate without reference (forward cyclic — gain airspeed) |
| **Forward hover taxi** | Ground track within 5 ft of centerline for minimum 200 ft |
| **Sideward/rearward taxi** | Describe hazards and crew coordination requirements — evaluated verbally in ground brief |
| **Normal takeoff** | Departure from hover to Vy (60 KIAS) climbing flight with coordinated ETL transition; no altitude loss below the departure hover altitude; airspeed within ±10 KIAS of Vy at level-off |
| **Collective at ETL** | Must reduce collective through ETL transition (evaluated by IP — large altitude excursion upward indicates no reduction) |

> **Minimums to move to Event 4:** Student must independently achieve the hover stabilization standard on at least two consecutive 30-second evaluations. A student who cannot maintain stable hover will repeat this event before proceeding. Hover control is the foundation of all subsequent events.

## Common Errors

| Error | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Rapid collective raise during pick-up | Helicopter bounds off the ground; overshoots hover altitude | "Squeeze the collective" — 1 inch/second maximum; feel the aircraft get light |
| Failing to add left pedal with collective | Nose yaws right; heading lost immediately | Verbal callout: "Collective up — left pedal." Build as reflex before solo hover |
| Staring at the ground immediately below | Loss of positional awareness; over-controlling lateral position | "Eyes out — 50 feet ahead." IP can block downward view to force the student to look out |
| Chasing drift with large cyclic inputs | PIO (pilot-induced oscillation) builds; aircraft swings back and forth | Small input; wait 0.5–1 sec for stabilizer bar; assess; then decide. Never fight the stabilizer bar. |
| No collective reduction through ETL | Balloon climb above departure altitude; risk of low altitude stall on leveling | Brief pre-ETL: "At ETL, reduce collective slightly — you just got more efficient." |
| Missing the ETL nose-up pitch | Nose rises excessively; airspeed decreases; aircraft may settle back toward ground | Pre-brief ETL: "Be ready with forward cyclic at 16–24 knots." |
| Skipping the hover power check | Departure into a condition where power is insufficient; risk of settling with power or obstacle strike | Make the callout mandatory: "Hover torque ___, margin ___, GO." No departure without it. |
| Rearward taxi without crew coordination | Tail rotor strike on unseen obstacle | Never go rearward without crew clear. Emphasize this during briefing. |
