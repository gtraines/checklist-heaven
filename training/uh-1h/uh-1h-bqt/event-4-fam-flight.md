## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Maintain straight and level cruise flight at 90–100 KIAS within ±100 ft altitude, ±10 KIAS airspeed, and ±10° heading for a minimum 3-minute period
- [ ] Execute coordinated level turns at 15°, 30°, and 45° bank angles while maintaining altitude within ±100 ft and airspeed within ±10 KIAS; demonstrate the bank-angle/collective relationship verbally
- [ ] Perform climbs to a specified altitude at Vy (60 KIAS) and cruise climb (80 KIAS); perform a level-off within ±100 ft of the target altitude
- [ ] Perform a cruise descent to a specified altitude at 90–100 KIAS within ±100 ft altitude at level-off
- [ ] Demonstrate slow flight at 30–40 KIAS while maintaining altitude within ±100 ft; describe the power curve and why power increases with slower airspeed
- [ ] Perform acceleration and deceleration maneuvers at constant altitude; explain the collective/cyclic relationship during each
- [ ] Correctly use force trim after every speed, power, and configuration change; demonstrate re-trim after an IP-induced airspeed change
- [ ] Enter and stabilize an autorotation at 65 KIAS within 5 seconds of IP call; maintain Nr within 294–324 RPM; perform a smooth power recovery to cruise flight

## Ground Brief

### Mission Profile

1. Engine start (abbreviated callouts)
2. Departure from airfield — climb to training altitude (minimum 3,000 ft AGL)
3. Airspeed familiarization: 40, 60, 80, 100 KIAS (all four, straight and level)
4. Turns: 15°, 30°, 45° bank — all four cardinal directions
5. Climbs and descents
6. Slow flight
7. Speed change maneuvers
8. Force trim demonstration and practice
9. Autorotation entries with power recovery (minimum 3 entries)
10. Return to airfield; landing; shutdown

### Key Numbers Table

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cruise speed** | 90–100 KIAS | Moderate power; best range |
| **Vy (best rate of climb)** | 60 KIAS | Departure climb standard |
| **Cruise climb** | 80 KIAS | Better forward progress than Vy |
| **Autorotation airspeed** | **65 KIAS** | Best glide; memorize this number |
| **Autorotation Nr range** | 294–324 RPM | Below 294 = insufficient energy |
| **ETL** | ~16–24 KIAS | Aircraft efficiency changes; power required changes |
| **Torque continuous** | 40 PSI | Monitor in all maneuvers |
| **EGT continuous** | 610°C | Monitor in climb especially |
| **Lead-off altitude for level** | 10% of climb rate | 500 fpm climb → begin level-off 50 ft below target |
| **Bank limit (normal ops)** | 45° | Steeper turns require airspeed reduction |
| **Vne** | 124 KIAS (sea level) | Do not approach in any training maneuver |

### Approximate Cruise Power Settings (Sea Level, ~7,500 lbs)

| Airspeed | Torque (Approx) | EGT (Approx) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 40 KIAS | ~32–36 PSI | ~530–560°C | Backside of power curve; high nose attitude |
| 60 KIAS | ~28–32 PSI | ~520–560°C | Vy; moderately nose-up |
| 80 KIAS | ~26–30 PSI | ~500–540°C | Economical cruise; near level nose |
| 100 KIAS | ~28–32 PSI | ~520–560°C | Best range; slight nose-down |
| 120 KIAS | ~34–38 PSI | ~560–590°C | Near Vne at altitude; increased vibration |

> *These are approximate values for DCS. Build your own feel — note the actual values during flight and record them.*

### Force Trim — Brief

Force trim must be used after every airspeed, power, or attitude change:

1. Establish desired flight condition.
2. Hold controls in the position maintaining that condition.
3. Press and hold Force Trim Release (`T`).
4. Release — magnetic brakes lock.
5. Controls should now be "neutral" for this flight condition.

If you feel a sustained force on the cyclic, you need to re-trim.

## Part 1 — Departure and Climb

| Step | Action | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Standard startup and before-takeoff checks (ref Event 2) | All systems normal; Nr 321–327 RPM |
| 2 | Hover power check — callout: "Hover torque ___ PSI, margin ___ PSI, GO" | GO declared |
| 3 | Normal takeoff through ETL | Climbing flight established |
| 4 | Climb at **Vy — 60 KIAS** | Steady climb; Nr stable; EGT <610°C; torque <40 PSI |
| 5 | Level off at **3,000 ft AGL** (or IP-assigned training altitude) | Lead level-off by 10% of climb rate (e.g., ~50 ft below target at 500 fpm); hold altitude within ±100 ft |
| 6 | Reduce collective to cruise power | Speed builds to 90–100 KIAS |
| 7 | **Set force trim** for cruise attitude | Cyclic neutral; no sustained stick forces |
| 8 | Engine instrument check | Nr 324 RPM; EGT <610°C; torque 26–32 PSI; oil P&T in limits |

## Part 2 — Straight and Level at Multiple Airspeeds

> Purpose: Develop a feel for the aircraft's handling at different speeds. Note nose attitude, power required, and control feel at each.

### Airspeed Sequence: 100 → 80 → 60 → 40 KIAS

At each speed, establish and hold for a minimum of 60 seconds:

| Speed | Technique | Expected Indications |
|---|---|---|
| **100 KIAS** | Slight nose-down attitude; cruise power | Torque ~28–32 PSI; EGT ~520–560°C; slight nose-down; low vibration |
| **80 KIAS** | Near-level attitude; slightly reduced power | Torque ~26–30 PSI; very comfortable; most economical |
| **60 KIAS** | Slightly nose-up; same or slightly more power than 80 KIAS | Torque ~28–32 PSI; approaching ETL lower boundary at 40-knot deceleration band |
| **40 KIAS** | Nose high (~5–8° pitch up); higher power despite slower speed | Torque ~32–36 PSI; increasing vibration; pedal workload increases; **backside of power curve** |

**Callout after completing 40-KIAS segment:** Student verbalizes why power increased at 40 KIAS vs. 80 KIAS (loss of translational lift; rotor working harder; no vertical fin effectiveness).

### 2-Per-Rev Vibration — Brief to Student

The UH-1H two-blade rotor produces a characteristic "chop" vibration at twice the rotor frequency (~10.8 Hz). This is always present and increases above 100 KIAS. It is **normal** — not a malfunction. Students should learn to distinguish this from irregular vibration that would indicate blade damage or imbalance.

## Part 3 — Turns (15°, 30°, and 45°)

### General Turn Technique

| Phase | Action |
|---|---|
| **Entry** | Clear turn direction; lateral cyclic in direction; slight aft cyclic to prevent nose drop; **add collective** for bank angle load factor; add pedal to keep ball centered |
| **In the turn** | Maintain bank with cyclic; maintain altitude with collective; maintain coordination with pedals; monitor airspeed (tends to decay) |
| **Rollout** | Lead by ~10° before desired heading; opposite lateral cyclic; **reduce collective** as bank decreases; center pedals; re-trim |

### Turn Exercises

| Exercise | Bank | G-Load | Collective Change Required | Evaluation Standard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Shallow turn** | 15° | ~1.04G | Negligible | ±100 ft altitude; ±10° heading on rollout |
| **Medium turn** | 30° | ~1.15G | Slight increase | ±100 ft altitude; ±10° heading on rollout |
| **Steep turn** | 45° | ~1.41G | Noticeable increase | ±100 ft altitude; ±10° heading on rollout; airspeed must be reduced |

**Required before 45° turns:** Reduce airspeed to ≤80 KIAS. At 45° bank and 100 KIAS, effective load factor is equivalent to approximately 119 KIAS straight-and-level — approaching retreating blade stall conditions.

**Callout requirement:** Before entering any turn ≥30°:
> *"Clearing turn [left/right], [degree] bank, airspeed [XX] KIAS."*

**Student verbal after completing a 45° turn:**
> Student states: (1) how much collective they added, (2) whether the ball stayed centered, (3) heading lead used for rollout.

## Part 4 — Climbs and Descents

### Climb Profiles

| Profile | Airspeed | Technique |
|---|---|---|
| **Best Rate (Vy)** | 60 KIAS | Increase collective to ~38–40 PSI; aft cyclic to reduce speed to 60 KIAS; coordinate left pedal; trim; monitor EGT |
| **Cruise Climb** | 80 KIAS | Increase collective to ~32–36 PSI; small aft cyclic; less speed reduction; trim |

**Level-Off Procedure:**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Lead by 10% of climb rate (at 500 fpm, begin 50 ft below target) |
| 2 | Smoothly apply forward cyclic to prevent further climb |
| 3 | As speed builds toward cruise, reduce collective |
| 4 | Hold altitude while speed reaches 90–100 KIAS |
| 5 | Set force trim |
| 6 | Instrument cross-check: altitude, airspeed, heading, engine gauges |

### Descent Profiles

| Profile | Airspeed | Power | Rate of Descent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cruise Descent** | 90–100 KIAS | ~20–25 PSI | 500–1,000 fpm |
| **Approach Descent** | 60–80 KIAS | Progressively reducing | 300–500 fpm |

**Descent Cautions:**
- Nr tends to rise during descents — monitor the dual tach. Governor compensates, but **avoid rapid collective reductions** which can cause Nr overshoot.
- Avoid the VRS entry conditions: airspeed <30 KIAS + RoD >300 fpm + moderate-to-high power applied simultaneously.

**Level-Off from Descent:**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Lead by 10% of descent rate |
| 2 | Smoothly increase collective to arrest descent |
| 3 | Forward cyclic if needed to maintain airspeed |
| 4 | Set force trim at level-off altitude |

## Part 5 — Slow Flight

> **Purpose:** Teach the "backside of the power curve." This is a key conceptual exercise.

**Setup:** Begin at 90 KIAS cruise, level at training altitude.

| Step | Action | Expected Indication |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Smoothly apply aft cyclic to decelerate | Nose rises; speed begins decreasing |
| 2 | Increase collective as speed decreases | Altitude maintained; torque increases as ETL efficiency is lost |
| 3 | Pass through **ETL (~16–24 KIAS)** — do not hover | Vibrations increase; power required increases noticeably; pedal workload increases |
| 4 | Establish **30–40 KIAS** | Nose high; high torque (possibly 32–36 PSI) despite slow speed |
| 5 | Hold altitude and heading for **30 seconds** | Note: more power required at 40 KIAS than at 80 KIAS |
| 6 | **Student verbalization:** "Why is power higher at 40 KIAS than 80 KIAS?" | Expected answer: loss of translational lift; rotor working harder without efficient airflow; vertical fin no longer effective (increased pedal workload) |
| 7 | Re-accelerate to 90–100 KIAS | Forward cyclic; **reduce collective as ETL efficiency returns**; left pedal coordination |
| 8 | Set force trim at cruise | Normal cruise resumed |

**Slow Flight Hazards:**
- **LTE exposure:** Slow flight reduces directional stability; any wind shift to the rear increases LTE risk.
- **VRS proximity:** If altitude is not maintained and a descent begins below ETL, VRS entry conditions can develop rapidly. Immediately apply forward cyclic if altitude is not held.

## Part 6 — Speed Changes at Constant Altitude

### Acceleration (From 60 to 100 KIAS)

| Phase | Control Input | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Begin | Forward cyclic | Nose drops; speed begins building |
| Through ETL | **Reduce collective slightly** | Rotor more efficient above ETL; less power needed to maintain altitude |
| At target speed | Aft cyclic to level nose | Hold 100 KIAS |
| Re-trim | Force trim at new speed | Cyclic neutral at 100 KIAS attitude |

**Student callout at 100 KIAS:** "Speed 100, torque ___ PSI, trim set."

### Deceleration (From 100 to 60 KIAS)

| Phase | Control Input | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Begin | Aft cyclic | Nose rises; speed begins decreasing |
| Below ETL | **Increase collective** | Rotor less efficient; more power needed |
| At target speed | Forward cyclic to level nose | Hold 60 KIAS |
| Re-trim | Force trim at new speed | Cyclic neutral at 60 KIAS attitude |

**Student callout at 60 KIAS:** "Speed 60, torque ___ PSI, trim set."

**Key relationship to memorize:**
- Accelerating → collective DOWN (as ETL efficiency increases)
- Decelerating → collective UP (as ETL efficiency decreases)

## Part 7 — Force Trim Practice

### IP-Directed Exercise

1. Student establishes cruise at 90 KIAS; sets trim.
2. IP changes collective (adds or removes 5 PSI).
3. Student must: re-establish altitude, adjust cyclic for new cruise attitude, **set force trim** at new condition.
4. Repeat at least three times with different changes (speed, collective, heading).

**Student demonstrates trim workflow verbally:**
1. "Establish flight condition."
2. "Hold controls in position."
3. "Release trim" (`T`).
4. "Re-engage — controls neutral."

**Out-of-Trim Indications (student identifies):**
- Sustained forward/aft pressure on cyclic = out of pitch trim
- Sustained left/right pressure = out of roll/lateral trim
- Sustained left/right foot pressure on pedals = yaw out of trim (different from LPedal for torque — sustained pressure means uncorrected torque condition)

## Part 8 — Autorotation Entry and Power Recovery

> **All autorotation practice at minimum 3,000 ft AGL. IP demonstrates first. Student performs minimum 3 entries.**

### Pre-Entry Briefing

Before each autorotation practice entry:

| Item | Brief |
|---|---|
| Entry altitude | ≥3,000 ft AGL |
| Power recovery altitude | ≥500 ft AGL (never below) |
| Abort word | "MY AIRCRAFT" — IP takes controls; collective up; power recovery |
| Nr limits | Min 294 RPM; Max 345 RPM |
| Entry airspeed | Will vary: practice at 60 KIAS, 80 KIAS, 100 KIAS to experience different entry characteristics |
| Two-blade rotor warning | Nr decays quickly — **the only acceptable reaction time is immediate** |

### Autorotation Entry Procedure

| Step | Action | Timing | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | IP calls "Engine failure" (or simulates throttle chop) | T=0 | RPM audio warning sounds; Nr begins to decay |
| 2 | **Collective — LOWER to near full down** | **Within 2 seconds** | Nr stabilizes or begins to recover; autorotation established |
| 3 | **Right pedal** — counter left yaw (no torque = natural right yaw; right pedal needed) | Simultaneous with collective | Heading stabilized; ball centered |
| 4 | **Cyclic** — establish **65 KIAS** | Within 5 seconds | Best glide speed; maximum range in autorotation |
| 5 | Adjust collective to hold **Nr 294–324 RPM** | Continuous | Collective is the Nr control; higher collective = lower Nr; lower collective = higher Nr |
| 6 | **Trim** for 65-KIAS descent | Once stabilized | Steady autorotation glide; ~1,500–2,000 fpm descent |
| 7 | Verbalize: "Nr [X], airspeed 65, rate of descent [Y], area [good/no-go]" | At stable autorotation | IP assessment |

### Power Recovery

> *Performed at or above 500 ft AGL — never below.*

| Step | Action | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Smoothly increase collective | Nr may momentarily decrease as load increases; throttle to FLY position already set |
| 2 | Add left pedal as torque returns | Heading maintained |
| 3 | Forward cyclic as needed to prevent nose-up | Smooth transition back to powered flight |
| 4 | Climb to re-establish training altitude | Standard climb profile |
| 5 | Reset force trim | Cruise established |

### Autorotation Entries — Multiple Speeds

| Entry Airspeed | Differences Noted |
|---|---|
| **60 KIAS** | Little speed adjustment needed; Nr decay manageable; less energy |
| **80 KIAS** | Slight forward cyclic hold to establish 65 KIAS descent; more energy in the rotor at entry |
| **100 KIAS** | More significant deceleration required; Nr tends to spike as collective lowers at speed; aft cyclic to slow to 65 KIAS while managing Nr |

**Student debrief question after each entry:** "What was your Nr at the point you lowered the collective? What would happen if you delayed 3 more seconds?"

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Straight and level** | ±100 ft altitude, ±10 KIAS, ±10° heading over a 3-minute evaluation at cruise |
| **Turns** | All bank angles flown; altitude within ±100 ft throughout; heading within ±10° on rollout; ball centered in 30° and 45° turns |
| **Climbs/descents** | Level-off within ±100 ft of target altitude; correct climb speed (60 or 80 KIAS ±10 KIAS); descent within ±10 KIAS |
| **Slow flight** | Maintains altitude ±100 ft at 30–40 KIAS for 30 seconds; correctly verbalizes power curve explanation |
| **Speed changes** | Demonstrates collective reduction on acceleration and collective increase on deceleration, with no altitude deviation >150 ft |
| **Force trim** | Re-trims after every IP-directed change; zero instances of holding out-of-trim sustained stick forces without re-trimming when directed |
| **Autorotation entry** | Collective down within 2 seconds of IP call; Nr remains ≥294 RPM; 65 KIAS established within 10 seconds; minimum 3 entries performed |
| **Power recovery** | Smooth; no Nr spike >339 RPM; no torque exceedance >40 PSI |

> **Go/No-Go for Event 5:** Student must demonstrate both stable cruise flight (±100 ft, ±10 KIAS, ±10°) and a correct autorotation entry (collective within 2 sec, Nr ≥294 RPM) before progressing to Event 5.

## Common Errors

| Error | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Not adding collective in turns | Altitude loss in turns | "Bank up = collective up." Verbalize before each turn entry |
| Uncoordinated turns (ball out) | Excess drag; less efficient; uncomfortable for passengers | "Step on the ball." Check ball every scan cycle in a turn |
| Skipping force trim after speed changes | Sustained stick forces; fatigue; control imprecision | Make trim a verbal callout: "Speed set — trimming" |
| Late autorotation entry (>2 sec) | Nr decays below 294 RPM; autorotation energy insufficient | Make entry reflex — drill the call-and-response until collective drops involuntarily on "engine failure" |
| Wrong pedal in autorotation | Heading departure; uncoordinated descent | Drill verbally: "No torque = right pedal" before every autorotation practice |
| Chasing Nr in autorotation | Constant collective oscillation; unstable descent | "65 KIAS controls your descent glide; collective controls Nr. Set collective for Nr, not for airspeed." |
| Not reducing collective through ETL on acceleration | Balloon climb during acceleration | Pre-brief every acceleration: "I will reduce collective when I feel the ETL efficiency." |
| Overshooting level-off | Altitude bust | "Lead by 10% of climb rate. At 500 fpm, that's 50 feet. Watch the VSI trend, not the altimeter lag." |
