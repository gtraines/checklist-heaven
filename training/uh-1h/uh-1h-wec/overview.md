## Course Description

The UH-1H Huey Weapons Employment Course (WEC) qualifies a pilot to safely and effectively employ the full range of unguided weapons available on the DCS UH-1H gunship: 2.75-inch FFAR rockets (XM-158 and XM-159 launchers), the M134 Minigun pod, and the M60D door gun at both crew stations. The course also covers Close Combat Attack (CCA) integration with ground forces and night weapons employment using NVG and illumination rockets.

> **DCS Module Note:** The DCS UH-1H (Belsimtek / Eagle Dynamics) is a **full-fidelity, clickable cockpit** module. There are no MFDs, no digital weapons management displays, no CCIP pipper, and no targeting sensors. All weapons delivery is **visual/optical** using the AN/M2A1-type optical deflection sight and tracer feedback. Accuracy depends entirely on mastery of the ballistic tables, sight depression settings, airspeed discipline, and attack profile geometry.

This is fundamentally different from a glass-cockpit attack helicopter course. The student must internalize ballistic fundamentals, memorize key depression angles, and develop a disciplined attack geometry — there is no computer to compensate for parameter errors. A 15-knot airspeed error produces a 30–60 meter range error at 1,000 meters slant range.

Upon successful completion, the student will be qualified to:

- Perform the complete weapons arming and safe sequence from the cockpit armament panel
- Set sight depression from the ballistic tables and execute level, diving (10° and 20°), and wagon wheel rocket attack profiles
- Walk tracers onto a target using the M134 minigun; compensate for recoil and asymmetric fire
- Switch to port and starboard door gun crew stations; employ the M60D with correct gunner technique and sector discipline
- Conduct a full wagon wheel attack pattern with door gun integration
- Execute a CCA mission: receive a ground fire request, perform talk-on target acquisition, brief the attack, and obtain "Cleared hot"
- Employ M257 illumination rockets and conduct weapons employment on NVG
- Execute immediate action procedures for weapons malfunctions

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this course, the student must:

- [ ] Hold a **UH-1H BQT qualification** — this course is not suitable for students who cannot yet fly a stable hover, perform a normal takeoff/landing, or execute emergency procedures without prompting
- [ ] Demonstrate **stable hover and basic airwork** to BQT completion standards
- [ ] Understand the DCS UH-1H cockpit layout, all instrument gauges, and the engine/rotor system from Event 1 academics (BQT)
- [ ] Have a **functional HOTAS** including collective axis, cyclic, and anti-torque pedals; the weapons fire trigger should be mapped to a physical button
- [ ] Be able to **click cockpit switches** fluently — the armament panel requires two-click sequences (guard lift + switch actuation) that must be performed without diverting attention from aircraft control

> **Recommended:** Before Event 2, practice the arming sequence (armament panel) and sight depression adjustment in the DCS cockpit on the ground, cold-dark, until it is fluent by memory. The arming checklist must be executable without consulting notes during a live attack run.

> **Note on Weapons Trigger:** By default the weapons fire trigger in DCS UH-1H is `Space`. If using HOTAS, assign the trigger to a physical button. Do not use keyboard `Space` for weapons fire during attack runs — keyboard input during maneuvering degrades aircraft control.

## Syllabus Events

Complete events **in sequence**. Each event must pass before progressing.

| Event | Title | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Event 1](event-1-academics.md) | Weapons Systems & Academics | Ground | Weapons inventory, armament panel, arming/safe procedures, ballistic fundamentals, weight/performance, malfunction overview |
| [Event 2](event-2-first-rockets.md) | Arming, Sight & First Rockets | Flight | Arming sequence, sight depression setting, level fire familiarization, first diving pass with observation and correction |
| [Event 3](event-3-advanced-rockets.md) | Advanced Rocket Employment | Flight | Diving attack (10° and 20°), wagon wheel introduction, salvo/ripple technique, wind and DA correction, BDA |
| [Event 4](event-4-minigun.md) | M134 Minigun Employment | Flight | Tracer walk technique, diving/running/wagon wheel profiles, recoil compensation, asymmetric fire |
| [Event 5](event-5-door-gun.md) | Door Gun Employment & Crew Coordination | Flight | Crew station switching, gunner technique, sector discipline, crew coordination calls, wagon wheel door gun integration |
| [Event 6](event-6-attack-cca.md) | Attack Profiles & CCA Integration | Flight | Full wagon wheel with all weapons, LZ prep mission, CCA fire request format, talk-on, cleared hot/abort |
| [Event 7](event-7-night.md) | Night Weapons Employment | Flight | NVG setup and limitations, M257 illumination, WP marking at night, tracer walk on NVG, visibility minimums |
| [Event 8](event-8-check-ride.md) | Check Ride | Evaluation | Full-profile pass/fail evaluation across all weapon systems and mission types |

**Estimated Completion:** 10–16 simulator sessions.

> **Note:** All flight events begin from a pre-planned mission profile in the DCS Mission Editor with the correct loadout pre-selected. The student must complete the full arming checklist before every weapons employment — no weapons may be fired without completing arming sequence verbalization. Autostart is not permitted during graded events.

## Grading Standards

### Grades

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All standards met; student may progress to the next event |
| **REPEAT** | One or more standards not met; event must be re-flown before progressing |
| **DOWN** | Unsafe act or weapons safety violation observed; event grounded pending debrief and IP review |

### Incomplete Item (II)

An Incomplete Item (II) is issued when the student fails one specific standard but otherwise passes the event. All IIs must be resolved **before the Check Ride (Event 8)**. A student accumulating more than three unresolved IIs is not ready for the check ride.

### Weapons Safety Standards (Zero Tolerance)

The following constitute an automatic **DOWN** regardless of other performance:

- Weapons fire without completing arming checklist verbalization
- Fire without "Cleared hot" from IP or ground controller
- Engagement inside danger close distance without explicit IP/controller clearance
- Maneuvering the aircraft below the minimum release altitude on a diving attack
- Pointing the aircraft toward simulated friendly forces with Master Arm in ARM

### Grading Tolerances

| Task | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Arming sequence** | Complete; in order; verbalized; guard lifted before switch actuation |
| **Safe sequence** | Complete; Master Arm SAFE before any other action post-engagement |
| **Sight depression setting** | Within ±2 mils of table value before each attack run |
| **Attack airspeed at release** | ±10 KIAS of planned profile airspeed |
| **Attack dive angle** | ±3° of planned dive angle |
| **Release altitude (diving attack)** | At or above minimum; no press below 300 ft AGL (10° dive), 500 ft AGL (20° dive) |
| **Pull-off altitude** | Above 200 ft AGL for all diving profiles |
| **Rocket impact (first adjustment)** | Within 50 m of intended aim point after one correction pass |
| **Tracer walk onto target (M134)** | On target within 3 seconds of initial tracer contact |
| **CCA arming sequence** | Correct fire request readback; cleared hot obtained before trigger press |
| **Danger close procedure** | Verbalized readback of "danger close" acceptance before every danger close engagement |
| **BDA report** | Submitted within 30 seconds of attack sequence completion |
