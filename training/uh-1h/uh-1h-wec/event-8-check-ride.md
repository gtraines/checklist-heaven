## Check Ride Overview

The WEC Check Ride is the final evaluation for the UH-1H Weapons Employment Course. It is a comprehensive evaluation of all skills developed across Events 1–7. The check ride is conducted by the IP or a designated evaluator without instruction or prompting.

### Check Ride Rules

- **No assists:** The evaluator does not provide reminders, hints, or corrections during the flight. The student performs all procedures independently.
- **No grading-in-progress comments:** The evaluator does not comment on quality or errors during the flight. Feedback is reserved for the debrief.
- **The student briefs themselves:** At the start of the flight, the student reviews the mission profile, states the key numbers for each planned attack, and identifies any considerations for the day's conditions (simulated weather, visibility, load).
- **All checks are real:** Every arming checklist, safe sequence, HOT/COLD call, fire request, and crew coordination call is graded. There are no "practice" passes in the check ride.

### Evaluator Actions

The evaluator:
- Occupies the IP seat (front-right) and monitors.
- Does **not** call out altitude floors, remind the student to arm, or prompt safe sequences.
- **Does** call *"CEASE FIRE"* and take control if a safety-of-flight or down-criterion condition occurs.
- **Does** call the abort during Phase 4 as a graded element.
- **Does** inject the malfunction scenario in Phase 2 (verbal only — calls "M134 stoppage").

## Ground Evaluation

Before the flight begins, the evaluator administers a verbal ground evaluation. The student must pass the ground evaluation before the flight proceeds.

### Ground Evaluation Questions (Evaluator selects 6 of 10)

| # | Question | Pass Standard |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | State the arming checklist for rockets, in order. | 6 steps; correct sequence; no prompting |
| 2 | State the safe sequence, in order. | Master Arm SAFE first; all 5 steps; no prompting |
| 3 | What is the depression for a 10° dive, 80 KIAS, 1,000 m? | 32 mils |
| 4 | What is the depression for a 20° dive, 80 KIAS, 1,000 m? | 22 mils |
| 5 | What are the danger close distances for HE rockets, WP, and 7.62mm? | HE 230 m; WP 300 m; 7.62mm 200 m |
| 6 | State the five elements of a fire request in order. | IP/Hazards/Target/Location/Remarks |
| 7 | What are the minimum pull-off altitudes for 10° and 20° dive, day and night? | 10°/day: 300 ft; 20°/day: 500 ft; 10°/night: 400 ft; 20°/night: 600 ft |
| 8 | What does *"Cleared hot"* authorize, and who may give it? | Authorizes weapons fire; given by the CCA controller or ground JTAC |
| 9 | What is the M134 maximum burst duration, and why? | 4–5 seconds; barrel temperature management |
| 10 | A danger close scenario: friendly at 250 m; you are using M151 HE rockets. What do you do? | State "danger close"; coordinate with controller; modify engagement or decline |

**Pass standard:** 5 of 6 questions correct. One incorrect answer is permitted with evaluator discretion. Two incorrect answers = ground evaluation FAIL = flight deferred. Student must review and re-sit ground evaluation before flying.

## Pre-Flight & Departure

### Student Self-Brief

Before startup, the student states:

1. Today's mission: *"I am conducting the WEC Check Ride. Planned attacks: [list phases and profiles]."*
2. Key numbers for each phase: depression, altitude, airspeed, pull-off floor.
3. Danger close distances for loaded munitions.
4. Today's considerations: *"Load is [rockets + minigun + door guns]. Power check GO/NO-GO at hover. [Any weather or visibility notes]."*

**Evaluator grades:** Is the self-brief complete? Does the student know the key numbers?

### Startup & Departure

- Cold-dark startup without prompting.
- Hover power check: torque stated; GO/NO-GO verbalized before departure.
- Armed departure: arming checklist executed before entering the range (no reminders).

## Phase 1 — Rocket Employment

### Scenario

The evaluator provides a target description and grid. The student independently:
1. Selects the appropriate attack profile (stated aloud before roll-in).
2. Sets the correct depression from memory.
3. Executes the arming checklist verbally before each pass.
4. Fires rockets; observes impact; verbalizes correction.
5. Executes the safe sequence after the final pass.

### Graded Elements

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Attack profile selection | Correct profile stated before roll-in with reasoning |
| Depression setting | Correct table value ±2 mils; stated before pass |
| Arming checklist | Complete; in order; verbal; no omissions |
| Altitude floor | 10° dive: above 300 ft AGL; 20° dive: above 500 ft AGL |
| Accuracy | At least 1 of 3 passes within 50 m of aim point |
| Impact verbalization | Impact called after each pass with direction and distance |
| Safe sequence | Master Arm SAFE first; complete; verbal |

### Minimum Requirement for Phase 1

- 3 diving passes completed.
- At least 1 pass within 50 m.
- No altitude floor busts.
- All arming and safe sequences complete and correct.

## Phase 2 — Minigun Employment

### Scenario

Same target or a new target designated by the evaluator. Transition to minigun.

1. Student selects GUNS on the armament panel.
2. Executes arming checklist for minigun verbally.
3. Conducts 2 diving passes; applies tracer walk technique.
4. Mid-event: evaluator calls *"M134 stoppage"* — student executes the minigun stoppage procedure.

### Minigun Stoppage Drill (Graded)

When evaluator calls *"M134 stoppage"*:

1. **Trigger released** — stop firing immediately.
2. **Master Arm — SAFE.**
3. **Call:** *"M134 stoppage — weapons safe — breaking off attack."*
4. **Pull off** — climb to orbit altitude.
5. *"Attempting to clear: weapon selector safe, checking panel."*
6. In DCS, no clearing mechanism is modeled — the student acknowledges the malfunction is unrecoverable in this engagement and transitions to rockets or declares weapons dry.
7. **Call:** *"M134 stoppage not cleared — transitioning to rockets [or: weapons employment complete this pass]."*

### Graded Elements

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Weapon selector change | GUNS selected; stated verbally |
| Arming checklist — minigun | Complete; in order; verbal |
| Tracer walk | Rounds on or within 25 m of target on at least 1 pass |
| Stoppage response | Trigger released within 1 second; Master Arm SAFE called; break-off executed |
| Safe sequence | Correct after each pass |

## Phase 3 — Door Gun & Crew Coordination

### Scenario

Evaluator (as pilot) establishes the orbit. Student cycles to door gun station and engages a target designated by the evaluator.

Alternatively, if the student is evaluated as pilot with an AI gunner: the student flies the orbit pattern and coordinates with the gunner using standard calls.

### Door Gun Evaluation (Student as Gunner)

1. Student enters port gun station (`F5`).
2. *"Port gun — manned and ready."*
3. Evaluator gives *"Cleared to engage."*
4. Student acquires target; calls *"NVG tally"* (if night) or *"Target acquired."*
5. *"Engaging — port gun."*
6. Fires; tracer walk; calls result.
7. Evaluator calls *"CEASE FIRE"* mid-burst — student responds within 1 second.

### Pilot Evaluation (Student as Pilot — Door Gun)

1. Student establishes right-hand orbit at 400 ft AGL; 70 KIAS.
2. Evaluator (as gunner) calls *"Target — 9 o'clock."*
3. Student: *"Roger — cleared to engage."*
4. Evaluator fires; student maintains orbit.
5. Student calls *"CEASE FIRE"* when evaluator designates (or if a safety condition is injected).

### Graded Elements

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Station manned call | Made within 10 seconds; correct format |
| Target acquired call | Before first trigger press |
| Engaging call | Made before fire; correct format |
| Orbit altitude | 300–500 ft AGL maintained |
| Sector discipline | No forward-of-nose fire |
| Cease fire response | ≤1 second from command to trigger release |
| Safe call | *"Weapons safe"* after each engagement |

## Phase 4 — CCA Integration

### Scenario

Evaluator simulates a CCA controller. Student must:

1. Receive a fire request from the evaluator and read it back.
2. Transit to the IP; fly the attack heading.
3. Receive a talk-on; identify the target; call *"Tally"*.
4. Report *"In-bound"*; receive *"Cleared hot"*; attack.
5. Mid-run: evaluator calls *"Abort, abort"* — student executes abort.
6. Student completes the abort procedure and reports status.
7. If the evaluator clears the student hot again: complete the attack; BDA call after.

### Graded Elements

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| Fire request read-back | All 5 elements present; correct order |
| Talk-on response | *"Tally"* or *"No joy"* with correct description |
| In-bound call | Made before the fire point |
| Cleared hot usage | Exact phrase acknowledged; no substitute |
| Abort execution | Trigger released; pull-off; *"aborting, off dry/wet"* called |
| BDA call | Content: threats; vehicle/personnel status; LZ/target clear or unclear |

## Phase 5 — Night Operations (Optional)

Night operations are included in the check ride at the evaluator's discretion based on:
- Student completion of Event 7.
- Mission time configured for night.
- Student NVG proficiency demonstrated in training events.

If included, the evaluator selects:
- 1 M257 illumination rocket pass (mark call required).
- 2 night rocket passes (NVG; 400 ft floor).

**Graded elements:** same as Event 7 standards; apply night-specific tolerances (75 m accuracy; 400 ft floor).

## Grading Standards

### Grades

| Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All completion standards met; no DOWN criteria triggered; no more than 2 Incomplete Items (II) |
| **INCOMPLETE** | One or more graded elements not demonstrated due to mission conditions, not student error; re-fly the incomplete element only |
| **DOWN (Unsatisfactory)** | Any DOWN criterion triggered, OR 3+ Incomplete Items, OR inability to meet accuracy standards after all passes |

### Scoring by Phase

Each phase is scored independently. A DOWN in one phase does not automatically fail the entire check ride — the evaluator records the DOWN criterion and debriefs all phases. However, any zero-tolerance violation (below the altitude floor, fire without arming, failure to respond to cease fire) results in an immediate DOWN for the entire check ride.

### Incomplete Items (II)

An II is awarded for a graded element that was not performed correctly but does not rise to the level of a DOWN:
- Missed a call (not a safety-critical call)
- Impact more than 50 m from aim point on all passes
- Correct response but not verbalized
- Fire request missing one element but otherwise correct

Maximum 2 II across the entire check ride for a PASS. Three or more II = DOWN.

## Check Ride Bust Criteria

The following are automatic DOWN criteria regardless of other performance:

| Criterion | Standard |
|---|---|
| Weapons fire without arming checklist | Fire any weapon without completing the verbal arming checklist — DOWN |
| Altitude floor bust | Any attack pass where pull-off is below the specified floor — DOWN |
| Failure to respond to CEASE FIRE | Trigger not released within 2 seconds of CEASE FIRE command — DOWN |
| Continued attack after ABORT call | Any trigger press after *"Abort, abort"* is called — DOWN |
| Wrong weapon fired | Rockets when minigun selected, or vice versa; any weapon fired at wrong target — DOWN |
| Friendly fire incident | Any rounds impact a designated friendly position — DOWN |
| Danger close fire without coordination | Firing within danger close distance without declaring and coordinating — DOWN |

## Debrief Standards

The evaluator conducts a structured debrief after the check ride regardless of outcome.

### Debrief Structure

1. **Overall grade:** PASS / INCOMPLETE / DOWN — state first.
2. **Phase-by-phase review:** what was accomplished in each phase; what grade was awarded.
3. **Specific events:** each II and DOWN criterion identified; exact occurrence described.
4. **Positive observations:** elements performed well — state at least two.
5. **Areas for improvement:** even for a PASS; identify the 1–2 highest-priority items for continued development.
6. **Re-ride requirements (if DOWN):** state which phase(s) must be re-flown and which criteria must be met.

### If DOWN

If the student receives a DOWN:
- Evaluator reviews the specific criterion with the student.
- Student must acknowledge understanding of the criterion.
- A partial re-ride covers only the phases or elements that resulted in DOWN or II accumulation.
- Partial re-rides may be conducted at the evaluator's discretion without repeating the entire check ride.
