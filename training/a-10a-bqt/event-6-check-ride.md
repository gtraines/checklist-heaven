## Check Ride Overview

The Check Ride is a **pass/fail evaluation** of the complete A-10A Basic Qualification Training syllabus. The student flies an unassisted full profile — from cold start through full-stop landing — without coaching from the instructor.

The check ride examines everything covered in Events 1–5. No new maneuvers or procedures are introduced.

> **This is not a training event.** The student flies the profile independently. The instructor evaluates but does not coach. Verbal or non-verbal coaching from the instructor automatically converts the associated item to an Incomplete (II).

---

## Prerequisites

Before beginning the Check Ride, the student must:

- [ ] Have completed Events 1–5 with no unresolved Incomplete Items (IIs)
- [ ] Be able to recite all critical limitations from memory (see Event 1)
- [ ] Have performed a minimum of **five full-stop landings** across Events 2–4
- [ ] Have successfully completed at least one single-engine approach in Event 5
- [ ] Not have received a DOWN grade in any prior event

If any prerequisite is unmet, defer the Check Ride. Re-fly the deficient event.

## Grading Criteria

### Grades

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All standards met; student is Basic Qualified on the A-10A |
| **INCOMPLETE (II)** | One or two standards not met, but no unsafe acts; student re-flies deficient portions only |
| **FAIL** | Three or more standards not met, OR any unsafe act |
| **DOWN** | Unsafe act resulting in simulated aircraft loss or operational hazard |

### Unsafe Acts (Automatic DOWN)

The following automatically result in a DOWN grade regardless of overall performance:
- Gear-up landing or retraction before confirmed positive rate of climb
- Takeoff above maximum gross weight (>50,000 lbs)
- Stall departure / spin departure below 5,000 ft AGL during airwork
- Runway excursion on takeoff or landing
- Shutting down the wrong engine during EP scenario
- Delaying ejection decision call below the stated threshold in EP5 knowledge check

## Check Ride Profile

The student flies the following sequence, uninterrupted, without reference to notes:

```
 1. Cold Start & Post-Start Checks
 2. Taxi to Runway
 3. Normal Takeoff
 4. Departure Climb to 10,000 ft MSL
 5. ── Airwork Area ──────────────────────────────────────────
    a. 30° banked level turns (both directions)
    b. 45° banked level turns (both directions)
    c. Wings-level approach-to-stall and recovery
    d. Speed brake descent demonstration
 6. ── Emergency Procedures ──────────────────────────────────
    a. Instructor calls simulated engine failure (one engine)
       → Student identifies, secures, and establishes single-engine flight
    b. Engine restart attempted (or single-engine plan declared)
    c. Ejection criteria verbalized (instructor asks 2 questions)
 7. ── Return ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
 8. Traffic Pattern — first approach
    a. Instructor calls "Go-Around" at 200 ft AGL
       → Student must execute correctly without prompting
 9. Traffic Pattern — second approach (full stop)
10. Post-landing rollout: speed brakes, progressive braking, full stop
11. Taxi clear of runway
```

## Post-Check Ride Debrief

### If PASS

Congratulations — the student is **Basic Qualified on the A-10A Thunderbolt II**.

Recommended follow-on training:
- [Basic & Intermediate Surface Attack Course](../surface-attack-course.md) — weapons employment and CAS tactics
- [Formation Flying Training Course](../formation-flying-training.md) — multi-ship operations

### If FAIL or DOWN

1. Debrief all failed items in detail — identify root cause (knowledge gap, technique, or habit pattern)
2. Return to the training event that covers the deficient area and re-fly it
3. Schedule a re-check ride after completing at minimum one additional training event per failed area
4. A student receiving two consecutive DOWN grades requires a mandatory instructor review before further flight
