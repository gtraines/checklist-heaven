## Ground Brief

### Semi-Active Laser Homing — How It Differs from Vikhr

The Kh-25ML and Kh-29L are **semi-active laser homers (SALH)**. This is fundamentally different from the Vikhr's beam-riding guidance.

| Feature | Vikhr | Kh-25ML / Kh-29L |
|---|---|---|
| Guidance | Beam-riding (looks back at aircraft) | Laser reflection (looks forward at target) |
| Post-launch | Must fly straight — beam discipline mandatory | Can maneuver — within Shkval gimbal limits |
| Range | 800 m – 8 km | 2 km – 12 km |
| Warhead | Tandem HEAT (anti-tank) | HE/fragmentation |
| Best target | Tanks, APCs | Bunkers, bridges, ships, heavy vehicles |

**Key concept:** In SALH, the laser illuminates the target and the missile homes on the reflected laser energy. You do **not** need to point the aircraft at the missile — you need to keep the **Shkval on the target**. As long as the target stays within the Shkval's gimbal limits (±35° azimuth), you can maneuver.

### Weapon Selection Guide

| Target | Weapon |
|---|---|
| Main Battle Tank (ERA protected) | Vikhr (tandem HEAT) |
| APC, IFV, light vehicle | Kh-25ML |
| Concrete bunker, hardened shelter | Kh-29L |
| Bridge, ship, large structure | Kh-29L |
| SA-9/SA-13 launcher | Kh-25ML |
| SAM radar van | Kh-25ML or Kh-29L depending on range |

## Part 1 — Kh-25ML Employment (Light Tactical)

### Weapon Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Warhead | ~90 kg HE/fragmentation |
| Min range | ~2 km |
| Max range | ~8–10 km |
| Speed | Subsonic (~0.7 Mach) |
| Time of flight (6 km) | ~18–22 seconds |

### Switchology

1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Mode** — A-G (`7`)
3. - [ ] **Select** — Kh-25ML pylon
4. - [ ] **Shkval** — ON (`O`)
5. - [ ] Slew to target, zoom in, adjust gate, **Lock** (`Enter`)
6. - [ ] **Laser** — ON (`RShift + O`)
7. - [ ] Wait for **"LA" (Launch Authorized)** cue on HUD
8. - [ ] **Launch** (`Space`)
9. - [ ] Continue to illuminate — keep Shkval on target until impact

### Attack Procedure

**Entry:** 2,500 m MSL, 500 km/h, target at 12 o'clock 8 km.

1. - [ ] Acquire target at 8–10 km — slew Shkval, zoom, gate, lock
2. - [ ] Laser ON — confirm "LA" cue
3. - [ ] Launch at 6–8 km range
4. - [ ] **After launch:** you may turn up to 25–30° left or right to avoid threat — Shkval will track if target remains within its field
5. - [ ] Monitor Shkval image — keep the locked box on target
6. - [ ] Impact in ~18–22 seconds
7. - [ ] Break lock after confirmed impact
8. - [ ] Master Arm — SAFE

**Instructor Note:** Students familiar with the Vikhr will try to fly perfectly straight after launch. Brief: "This is not a beam rider. You can turn — gently. Just keep the gate on the target in the Shkval display."

## Part 2 — Kh-29L Employment (Heavy Strike)

The Kh-29L carries a massive 320 kg warhead. It is overkill for vehicles but essential for hardened targets.

### Weapon Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Warhead | ~320 kg HE penetrating |
| Min range | ~2–3 km |
| Max range | ~10–12 km |
| Speed | Subsonic (~0.6 Mach) |
| Time of flight (8 km) | ~28–35 seconds |
| Weight | 657 kg — affects aircraft handling |

### Loadout Consideration

The Kh-29L is heavy. Carrying two significantly affects:
- Takeoff distance (rotate at 250–260 km/h vs. 210 km/h clean)
- Turn rate (notable increase in corner velocity)
- Fuel consumption (drag)

Do not carry Kh-29L if the target does not warrant it.

### Switchology (same as Kh-25ML, with longer TOF)

1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Mode** — A-G (`7`)
3. - [ ] **Select** — Kh-29L pylon
4. - [ ] **Shkval** — ON (`O`)
5. - [ ] Slew to target, zoom, gate, **Lock**
6. - [ ] **Laser** — ON (`RShift + O`)
7. - [ ] Wait for **LA** cue
8. - [ ] **Launch** at 8–10 km range
9. - [ ] Continue illumination for **28–35 seconds** — significantly longer than Kh-25ML

### Illumination Patience

The longer time of flight is the key discipline challenge for the Kh-29L. Students often break lock at ~20 seconds thinking the missile has reached the target. At 10 km range it may still have 15 seconds of flight remaining.

**After launch, establish a stable hold:**
- Trim the aircraft (reduce stick workload)
- Maintain 500 km/h cruise
- Gentle turns only (< 25° bank)
- Watch the Shkval — locked box must stay on target

## Part 3 — Post-Launch Maneuvering

This exercise specifically trains the student to maneuver after launch while maintaining illumination — taking advantage of the SALH's flexibility over the Vikhr.

### Exercise Profile

1. - [ ] Launch Kh-25ML at 7 km against APC target
2. - [ ] Immediately after launch — turn 20° LEFT (away from threat axis)
3. - [ ] Maintain turn for 8 seconds, then roll wings-level
4. - [ ] Continue illumination to impact
5. - [ ] Assess: did the missile hit? (it should — Shkval tracked through the turn)

6. - [ ] Repeat with 20° RIGHT turn
7. - [ ] Repeat with gentle 15° nose-low push (descent while illuminating)

### Gimbal Limit Awareness

If you maneuver too aggressively, the Shkval may reach its azimuth limit (±35°) and lose the lock. The HUD will indicate loss of lock.

**Signs of gimbal limit approach:**
- Shkval slew indicator at extreme position
- Target drifting toward edge of Shkval FOV
- Lock box begins to drift

If gimbal limits are exceeded:
1. Roll toward the target to bring it back into FOV
2. Re-acquire if lock was lost
3. The missile has likely missed if lock broke during terminal phase (< 5 km)

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Treating Kh-25ML like a Vikhr (flying straight) | Brief the SALH concept; demonstrate the difference |
| Breaking illumination at 20 sec (Kh-29L) | Memorize TOF: "10 km = 30 seconds minimum" |
| Banking past gimbal limits | Train with ≤ 20° banks; watch the Shkval edge |
| Launching inside min range (< 2 km) | Kh-25ML cannot arm — abort pass if too close |
| Using Kh-29L against soft targets | Overkill and operationally wasteful; save for hardened targets |
| Not zooming in before lock | VID requirement — confirm target type before firing |
