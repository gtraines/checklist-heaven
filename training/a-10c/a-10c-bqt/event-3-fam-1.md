## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Complete the full startup procedure (from Event 2) without instructor assistance
- [ ] Execute a normal takeoff and achieve a clean aircraft configuration by 500 ft AGL
- [ ] Maintain assigned altitude (±150 ft) and airspeed (±15 KIAS) in level flight
- [ ] Perform 30° and 45° banked level turns with ball centered
- [ ] Execute 500 ft/min and 1,000 ft/min climbs and descents
- [ ] Demonstrate correct trim technique for speed and configuration changes
- [ ] Fly a rectangular traffic pattern and execute one full-stop landing

## Ground Brief

### Mission Profile

| Phase | Activity |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Cold-dark startup and taxi (full procedure from Event 2) |
| Phase 2 | Takeoff and departure climb to 10,000 ft MSL |
| Phase 3 | Basic airwork: level flight, 30°/45° turns, climbs/descents, trim |
| Phase 4 | Return, traffic pattern, full-stop landing |

### Key Numbers

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Fuel Load | 75% internal (~8,000 lbs) |
| Stores | Clean |
| Gross Weight (approx.) | ~36,000 lbs |
| Vr (Rotation) | ~135 KIAS |
| Vy (Best Climb) | 200–220 KIAS |
| Airwork Altitude | 10,000 ft MSL |
| Cruise Speed | 250–280 KIAS |
| Pattern Altitude | 1,500 ft AGL |
| Downwind Speed | 200 KIAS |
| Final (On-Speed) | ~130 KIAS (green donut) |
| Touchdown Speed | ~120 KIAS |

### Spool-Up Reminder

Before taxiing, briefly advance both throttles from idle to ~90% and count the seconds. You should feel usable thrust arrive within **4–6 seconds**. This is the delay you must anticipate on every approach — power corrections on final must be made **before** you need them.

## Pre-Mission Setup

- [ ] Load DCS; select A-10C or A-10C II at a familiar airfield (Batumi, Kobuleti, Nellis, or Creech recommended)
- [ ] Set aircraft to **cold and dark**
- [ ] Set weather: **day VMC, calm winds (< 10 kts), no cloud**
- [ ] Confirm HOTAS and mouse controls functional

## Part 1 — Startup & Taxi

The student performs the complete startup and taxi procedure **from memory** (referencing Event 2). No checklist reading — verbalize each action.

### Startup Flow (Summary)

1. Battery ON → Inverter STBY → Warning Light Test
2. APU START → APU GEN PWR
3. Left engine start (IGN → fuel at 12–15% N2 → NORM after idle)
4. Right engine start (same)
5. Left GEN PWR → Right GEN PWR → APU GEN OFF → APU OFF
6. Post-start: HYD A/B check, flight control check, warnings clear
7. Avionics: CDU → EGI → UFC → MFCDs → TGP STBY → IFFCC → CMSP → Pitot Heat
8. EGI alignment: remain stationary → wait NAV READY
9. Pre-taxi: SAS ON (all 3) → EAC ARM → Trim neutral → Altimeter set

### Taxi

- NWS HI for ramp, NWS LO for taxiway
- Brake check within 50 ft
- 10–15 kts taxiway; < 10 kts turns
- Pre-takeoff checks at hold short (all items from Event 2)

## Part 2 — Takeoff & Departure

### Takeoff Roll

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Line up** on centerline | Verify runway heading matches HSI |
| 2 | **NWS LO** — engaged | For takeoff roll directional control |
| 3 | **Throttles** — FULL (military) | Smooth 2–3 second push from idle to max |
| 4 | Verify N1 matching, symmetric | No asymmetric thrust; ITT within limits |
| 5 | Maintain centerline with rudder | NWS authority fades above ~60 kts; transition to rudder |
| 6 | Call out airspeed mentally: "80…100…120…" | |
| 7 | At **~135 KIAS** — smooth rotation | 2–3°/sec pitch rate; target 10° nose-up attitude |

> **Warning:** Do NOT jerk the nose up. The A-10C sits tail-low on its main gear — an aggressive rotation will cause tail buffet and reduce acceleration. Smooth, deliberate pitch rate.

### Clean-Up

| Step | Action | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Positive rate confirmed — **GEAR UP** | `G` key or cockpit handle; verify 3 green lights extinguish |
| 2 | Accelerate to 200 KIAS — **Flaps UP** | Left console flap switch to UP; confirm indicator |
| 3 | **Trim** — nose-down as flaps retract | Flap retraction causes nose pitch-up tendency |
| 4 | Climb at 200–220 KIAS (Vy) | Maintain heading; NWS auto-disengaged |

### Departure Climb

- Climb at **200–220 KIAS** (Vy) to **10,000 ft MSL**
- Power: Military (full throttle) for the climb
- Trim continuously as speed builds — the nose rises as power is set; requires nose-down trim
- At 10,000 ft: level-off begins 100 ft early; reduce pitch, allow speed to build, reduce power to cruise (~82–85% N1 at 10,000 ft)
- Target cruise: **260 KIAS, level, trimmed**

## Part 3 — Basic Airwork

Conduct all exercises at **10,000 ft MSL** with adequate recovery altitude below. Announce each maneuver before beginning: *"Entering 30-degree left turn."*

### Exercise A: Straight & Level

- [ ] Establish level flight at 10,000 ft (**±100 ft**), 260 KIAS (**±10 KIAS**)
- [ ] **Trim to hands-off:** no stick pressure required to hold altitude and heading
- [ ] Hold for 60 seconds — verify aircraft flies itself

**Instructor Note:** If the student is holding constant stick pressure, they are out of trim. Have them release the stick briefly and observe the deviation. Then trim to correct. Repeat until the aircraft is genuinely hands-off.

### Exercise B: Level Turns

Perform each turn at constant altitude. Ball must remain centered throughout.

| Maneuver | Bank Angle | G-Load | Altitude Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level turn — left and right | 30° | 1.15 G | ±100 ft |
| Level turn — left and right | 45° | 1.41 G | ±150 ft |
| Demonstration: observe G-meter | 60° | 2.0 G | Observe only; do not attempt to hold altitude |

**Technique:**
1. Roll to desired bank; smoothly apply back pressure to maintain altitude.
2. With **EAC engaged**: turn coordination is largely automatic.
3. Monitor AoA indexer in steep turns — AoA increases with bank; do not allow into slow flight regime unintentionally.
4. Roll out on entry heading ±15°.

> **60° demonstration only:** At 60° bank, note the 2.0 G load, the rapid altitude loss required to maintain it, and how the AoA indexer begins rising. The student should understand the relationship between bank angle and load factor before practicing steep turns in Event 4.

### Exercise C: Climbs & Descents

| Maneuver | Speed | Power | Target Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **500 ft/min climb** | 220 KIAS | ~90–92% N1 | 500 ft/min on VSI |
| **1,000 ft/min climb** | 210 KIAS | Military (full) | 1,000 ft/min on VSI |
| **500 ft/min descent** | 250 KIAS | ~75% N1 | 500 ft/min on VSI |
| **1,000 ft/min descent** | 250 KIAS | Idle + speed brakes | 1,000 ft/min on VSI |

**Level-off technique:** Begin level-off 100 ft before target altitude (climbing) or 50 ft before (descending). Add/reduce power; lower/raise nose; retrim.

### Exercise D: Trim Demonstration

This exercise builds the habit of continuous trimming.

1. From level flight at 260 KIAS: **Accelerate to 350 KIAS** — observe nose rising; trim nose-down to maintain altitude; verify hands-off
2. **Decelerate from 350 KIAS to 200 KIAS** — observe nose dropping; trim nose-up; verify hands-off
3. **Extend speed brakes** — observe nose-down pitch change; trim to correct; retract speed brakes; trim back
4. **Flaps to MVR** (200 KIAS) — observe pitch change; trim to correct; retract flaps; trim back

**Lesson:** Every configuration change requires re-trimming. The aircraft will not fly itself until the pilot trims it.

### Exercise E: Speed Brake Operation

- [ ] From level flight at 260 KIAS: deploy speed brakes (cockpit switch on left console / HOTAS / `B` key)
- [ ] Observe: nose pitches down slightly; airspeed decreases; descent begins if not countered
- [ ] Apply back stick / nose-up trim to maintain altitude
- [ ] Retract speed brakes; observe nose pitch-up; trim to correct
- [ ] Practice several cycles until pitch transients are anticipated, not corrected after the fact

## Part 4 — Return & Landing

### Descent & Approach Setup

- [ ] At 10,000 ft: reduce power to ~75% N1; descend at 250 KIAS toward the field
- [ ] At 15 nm from field: request landing if ATC present
- [ ] At 1,500 ft AGL and 10 nm: set up for pattern entry

### Traffic Pattern (Rectangular)

> **Note:** Event 6 focuses exclusively on overhead break pattern. This event uses a **rectangular VFR pattern** to focus on one approach and landing without the complexity of the overhead break.

| Phase | Speed | Config | Altitude | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Downwind** | 200 KIAS | Clean | 1,500 ft AGL | Parallel to runway, opposite direction |
| **Abeam threshold** | 200 KIAS | Clean | 1,500 ft AGL | Begin configuration: Gear DOWN, Flaps MVR |
| **Downwind (configured)** | 160–170 KIAS | Gear + MVR flaps | 1,500 ft AGL | Stabilizing — cross-check AoA indexer |
| **Base turn** | 150 KIAS | Gear + MVR/DN flaps | Descending | Turn when threshold is 45° behind wing |
| **Final** | 130–140 KIAS | Gear + DN flaps | Descending ~3° | **AoA indexer: green donut** |

### Downwind Configuration

- [ ] Reduce throttle to ~70% N1
- [ ] **Gear DOWN** (`G` or cockpit handle) — verify **3 green lights**
- [ ] Verify gear warning horn silenced
- [ ] **Flaps MVR** (left console flap switch to MVR) — verify flap indicator
- [ ] Trim nose-up slightly for approach attitude
- [ ] Cross-check: speed settling to 160–170 KIAS

### Base Leg

- [ ] Turn base when threshold is approximately 45° behind the wing
- [ ] Reduce to **150 KIAS**; begin descent (~500 ft/min)
- [ ] **Flaps DN** if below 170 KIAS and committed to full-stop
- [ ] AoA indexer should show lower chevron (slow approach) to donut as speed settles

### Final Approach

- [ ] Roll out aligned with runway centerline
- [ ] **Primary reference: AoA indexer green donut** — approximately 130–135 KIAS at this weight
- [ ] **Secondary reference:** Aim point at 1,000 ft runway markers; constant visual angle
- [ ] Stabilized by **500 ft AGL**: correct speed, glidepath, and lineup
- [ ] If **NOT** stabilized by 500 ft AGL → **GO AROUND** (see below)
- [ ] Speed brakes — confirm CLOSED
- [ ] Do NOT allow airspeed to decay below 120 KIAS on final

### Go-Around (If Required)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Throttles** — MILITARY (full power) |
| 2 | Pitch — arrest descent; establish positive rate |
| 3 | Positive rate confirmed — **GEAR UP** |
| 4 | **Flaps — MVR** |
| 5 | Accelerate to 200 KIAS — **Flaps UP** |
| 6 | Climb to pattern altitude; re-enter on downwind |

> **Decision rule:** If you are not stabilized by 500 ft AGL — go around. There is always another approach.

### Flare & Touchdown

- [ ] At **50 ft AGL**: slight back pressure — arrest descent rate
- [ ] Do NOT pull aggressively — the A-10C lands **flat** relative to fighters
- [ ] Allow main gear to touch down firmly at **~120 KIAS**
- [ ] Do NOT try to "grease it on" — a firm, controlled touchdown is correct technique

### Rollout

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Main gear contact — **Speed Brakes OPEN** (`B` or cockpit switch) |
| 2 | Hold slight back pressure — aerodynamic braking above 100 KIAS |
| 3 | At **100 KIAS** — lower nose gear gently; transition to wheel brakes |
| 4 | **Progressive braking** — smooth, increasing pressure (`W` or toe brakes) |
| 5 | **NWS** — engage after nosewheel contact (NWS button on stick) |
| 6 | Maintain centerline to full stop |
| 7 | Exit runway at first available taxiway — do NOT stop on the runway |

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Engine start | No ITT overtemp; stable idle; warnings clear |
| Startup sequence | No major steps missed without recovery |
| Rotation speed | Within ±15 KIAS of 135 KIAS target |
| Gear retraction | On positive rate; before 200 KIAS |
| Flap retraction | At or above 200 KIAS |
| Level flight | Altitude ±150 ft; airspeed ±15 KIAS |
| 30° turns | Altitude ±100 ft; ball centered |
| 45° turns | Altitude ±150 ft; ball centered |
| Trim technique | Aircraft hands-off after each exercise |
| Stabilized approach | Stabilized (speed, glidepath, lineup) by 500 ft AGL |
| Final AoA | Green donut on AoA indexer for ≥ 15 seconds |
| Touchdown | Within first 1,000 ft; centerline ±15 ft |
| Speed brakes | Deployed within 3 seconds of main gear contact |
| Braking | No wheel lockup; stopped within runway length |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Aggressive rotation — porpoise on takeoff | 2–3°/sec pitch rate; smooth, deliberate. Do not jerk the nose up. |
| Gear left down after liftoff | "Positive rate — gear up" must be a verbal callout and immediate action. |
| Not trimming after every configuration change | Constant stick pressure = out of trim. Release stick; observe deviation; trim; verify. |
| Power corrections too late on final | Set power on base. Make small anticipatory adjustments. Do not react to a sinking aircraft — anticipate it. |
| Aggressive flare — float | Slight check of descent only. The A-10C does not flare aggressively. Fly it onto the runway. |
| Speed brakes forgotten on rollout | "Mains on — speed brakes open" must be reflexive. Practice the callout every landing. |
| Not going around when not stabilized | If the approach is bad by 500 ft AGL, it won't fix itself in the next 400 ft. Go around without hesitation. |
