## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Configure the aircraft for slow flight and maintain controlled flight at low airspeeds
- [ ] Identify the aerodynamic signs of an approaching stall before it occurs
- [ ] Execute a stall recovery with minimum altitude loss using the AoA indexer
- [ ] Explain the function of each flap setting and associated airspeed limits
- [ ] Demonstrate correct energy management: transition between slow/fast configurations smoothly
- [ ] Apply trim technique at low airspeeds and high AoA
- [ ] Execute a second full-stop landing with improved glidepath control

| Phase | Activity |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Startup, taxi, takeoff — standard (no checklist reading) |
| Phase 2 | Climb to 12,000 ft MSL; slow flight entry, exercise, recovery |
| Phase 3 | Stall recognition (power-off and turning), recovery |
| Phase 4 | Energy management transitions |
| Phase 5 | Return, traffic pattern, landing |

### Key Numbers for This Event

| Item | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Stall speed (Clean, ~36,000 lbs) | ~130 KIAS | Buffet onset ~15 KIAS prior |
| Slow flight speed | 140–145 KIAS | Just above stall in clean config |
| Slow flight speed (Gear + Full Flap) | ~110–115 KIAS | **Power required will be high** |
| Flap limits | UP: any speed; MVR: ≤ 220 KIAS; DN: ≤ 170 KIAS | |
| Gear limit (extension) | ≤ 250 KIAS | |
| Min airspeed on approach | 120 KIAS | Hard floor; below this go around |

### AoA Indexer Reference

The AoA indexer (left glareshield) is the **primary instrument** for on-speed approach and slow-flight management. Know all 5 segments:

| Display | Meaning | Approximate AoA |
|---|---|---|
| Upper chevron (double) | Very fast — pitch up | < 9° AoA |
| Upper chevron (single) | Fast | 9–13° AoA |
| **Green donut** | **On-speed** | **~13–15° AoA** |
| Lower chevron (single) | Slow | 15–17° AoA |
| Lower chevron (double) | Very slow / stall approaching | > 17° AoA |

> **Rule:** The AoA indexer tells you where you are relative to optimum approach AoA. Flying on speed regardless of weight means you don't need to recalculate the airspeed for every landing.

## Part 2 — Slow Flight

> **Purpose:** To demonstrate the aircraft's handling at low airspeeds and high AoA before introducing stall. Students must be comfortable with high-power, low-speed, high-AoA flight before practicing recovery.

### Slow Flight — Clean Configuration

1. From level flight at 260 KIAS: **reduce power to ~70% N1**
2. Allow speed to decrease; apply back pressure to **maintain altitude**
3. As speed decreases below 200 KIAS: trim nose-up to reduce stick pressure
4. Continue decelerating — monitor AoA indexer
5. At **145–150 KIAS**: the indexer shows the **lower chevron** (slow)
6. Increase power to ~85% N1 to maintain altitude at this low speed
7. The student should notice: very high pitch attitude, high power, mushy controls
8. - [ ] Maintain **145 KIAS ±10 KIAS, altitude ±200 ft** for 30 seconds
9. Apply **left and right 30° turns** at slow flight speed
10. Note: control effectiveness is reduced; ball may deviate; proverse/adverse yaw is more pronounced

**Recovery from slow flight (clean):**
1. Power — Military (smoothly)
2. Lower nose slightly to maintain altitude as speed builds (resist pulling)
3. Trim nose-down as speed builds
4. At **200 KIAS** — normal cruise configuration

### Slow Flight — Gear & Full Flap

1. From level flight at 12,000 ft: reduce to 200 KIAS
2. **Gear DOWN** — confirm 3 green lights
3. Reduce to 170 KIAS — **Flaps MVR**
4. Reduce to 145 KIAS — **Flaps DN**
5. Maintain altitude with **high power (~90% N1)**
6. Note: aircraft very slow, very high pitch attitude, excellent low-speed stability but very limited energy
7. - [ ] Maintain **115–120 KIAS ±10 KIAS, altitude ±200 ft** for 20 seconds
8. AoA indexer should show green donut or slight slow indication

**Recovery from slow flight (gear + full flap):**
1. Power — Military (smoothly)
2. Positive climb rate — **Gear UP**
3. Accelerate to 170 KIAS — **Flaps MVR**
4. Accelerate to 200 KIAS — **Flaps UP**
5. Resume climb to 12,000 ft; retrim

## Part 3 — Stall Recognition & Recovery

> **Safety:** All stalls are practiced at **12,000 ft MSL**. If below 10,000 ft MSL, **abort the exercise and recover**. Stall recovery requires a minimum of 500–1,000 ft AGL below entry altitude — at 12,000 ft MSL this is well within limits.

### What the A-10C Stall Looks and Feels Like

| Stage | Speed | Signs |
|---|---|---|
| **Buffet onset** | ~145 KIAS clean | Airframe buffet — first warning |
| **Pre-stall** | ~135 KIAS | Buffet increases; controls feel mushy |
| **Stall** | ~130 KIAS | Nose drops; possible roll-off; buffet peaks |
| **Post-stall** | < 130 KIAS | Aircraft departs if not recovered |

> **DCS Note:** The A-10C in DCS simulates stall buffet and nose-drop behavior. The departure tendency is present but less severe than real life. Treat the buffet as the real aircraft would — recover immediately at buffet onset, not after the nose drops.

### Exercise A: Power-Off Stall (Clean)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Level flight at 12,000 ft; 200 KIAS; confirm area clear (look left, right, check altitude) |
| 2 | **Throttles — IDLE** |
| 3 | Maintain altitude with **increasing back pressure** as speed decreases |
| 4 | Announce verbally: *"Speed 160... 150... 140... buffet..."* |
| 5 | **At first buffet** — recognize the stall approach; **initiate recovery** |
| 6 | Recovery: **power — Military; pitch — lower nose to level; DO NOT pull** |
| 7 | Allow speed to build; return to level flight; trim |

> **Recovery Principle:** The only way out of a stall is to **reduce AoA** (lower the nose). Adding power alone will not recover a stalled wing. Lower the nose FIRST, add power simultaneously.

**Do NOT practice full stall departure on first event.** Buffet recognition and recovery is the objective.

### Exercise B: Power-Off Stall (Turning)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Enter a **30° banked level turn** at 160 KIAS; throttles IDLE |
| 2 | Maintain altitude (increasing back pressure) as speed decreases |
| 3 | At first buffet — recognize; initiate recovery |
| 4 | Recovery: **Power — Military; WINGS LEVEL first; lower nose** |
| 5 | Allow speed to build; level flight; retrim |

> **Turning stall key lesson:** In a bank, stall speed is **higher** than in level flight. At 30° bank, stall speed increases by ~8%; at 45° it increases by ~20%. This is why uncoordinated steep turns on base-to-final are dangerous.

### Exercise C: Approach-to-Landing Stall (Gear + Flaps)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Configure: Gear DOWN, Flaps DN; 130 KIAS level at 12,000 ft |
| 2 | Maintain altitude; slowly reduce power to ~70% N1 |
| 3 | AoA indexer will show lower chevron and then lower double chevron as speed falls |
| 4 | At buffet — **power Military; do NOT raise gear or flaps until positive rate** |
| 5 | Positive climb rate established — Gear UP; Flaps MVR at 170 KIAS; Flaps UP at 200 KIAS |

**Lesson:** Go-around from a stalled approach is possible but requires immediate, decisive power application. Do not delay.

## Part 4 — Energy Management

> **Purpose:** Students must learn to transition between energy states smoothly and predictably. A-10C can lose energy rapidly but gains it slowly — energy management is more critical here than in high-thrust jets.

### Exercise A: Rapid Configuration Change (Combat Ingress / Egress Scenario)

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | From cruise: 260 KIAS, 10,000 ft — **deploy speed brakes** | Feel the deceleration |
| 2 | Gear DOWN at 250 KIAS | Gear limit is 250 KIAS |
| 3 | Flaps MVR at 220 KIAS | |
| 4 | Slow to 130 KIAS, AoA green donut | Simulated final approach |
| 5 | GO AROUND: power Military; positive rate; gear up; flap retraction schedule | Full go-around sequence |
| 6 | Back to 260 KIAS, 10,000 ft, level, trimmed | Total exercise takes ~3 minutes |

### Exercise B: Acceleration from Slow to Fast

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Establish slow flight at 140 KIAS, 10,000 ft, clean |
| 2 | **Throttles — Military** |
| 3 | Lower nose 3–5° — hold altitude as speed builds (do not climb — convert power to airspeed) |
| 4 | At 260 KIAS — raise nose; establish cruise |
| 5 | Note: A-10C takes approximately **60–90 seconds** to accelerate from 140 to 300 KIAS. This is the slow acceleration the pilot must anticipate on every go-around. |

**Key lesson:** The A-10C is not a jet fighter. Power takes time to translate to airspeed. Go-around decisions must be made **early** — once inside 200 ft AGL, the option to go around and climb safely is severely degraded.

## Part 5 — Return & Landing

### Approach Planning

- [ ] At 12,000 ft and 30 nm from field: begin descent at idle power, 250 KIAS
- [ ] At 10,000 ft: estimate fuel; verify field weather if applicable
- [ ] At 1,500 ft AGL and within 10 nm: enter downwind

### Traffic Pattern (Rectangular — Second Event)

Student should demonstrate **improved** performance over Event 3:
- Smoother power transitions
- Gear and flaps on-schedule (no rushing)
- AoA indexer green donut sustained for ≥ 20 seconds before threshold
- Stabilized approach by 500 ft AGL (mandatory)

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Downwind speed | 200 KIAS ±10 KIAS |
| Final speed | AoA green donut (130–135 KIAS at this weight) |
| Glidepath | Aim point at 1,000 ft markers ±100 ft |
| Centerline | ±15 ft |
| Touchdown | Within first 1,000 ft of runway |

### Runway Rollout Improvement Focus

- Speed brakes deployed **within 3 seconds** of mains on runway
- Aerodynamic braking above 100 KIAS (do not ride the brakes above 100 KIAS)
- Smooth nose-gear lowering at 100 KIAS
- No runway excursion; exit at taxiway

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Startup | No ITT overtemp; no missed critical items |
| Slow flight — clean | 145 KIAS ±10 KIAS; altitude ±200 ft; 30 seconds |
| Slow flight — gear + flaps | 115 KIAS ±10 KIAS; altitude ±200 ft; 20 seconds |
| Stall recognition | Recognizes buffet and verbalizes; initiates recovery within 3 seconds |
| Stall recovery (clean) | Positive altitude trend; no secondary stall; < 1,000 ft altitude loss |
| Stall recovery (turning) | Wings level before or simultaneously with nose-down; < 1,200 ft altitude loss |
| Energy management | Complete rapid config change and go-around without altitude loss > 500 ft |
| Final approach | Green donut sustained ≥ 20 seconds prior to threshold |
| Touchdown | Within first 1,000 ft; centerline ±15 ft |
| Speed brakes | Within 3 seconds of mains contact |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Pulling back at stall recovery | **Lower the nose first**. Only pitch up when there is airspeed to work with. |
| Not recognizing buffet as the stall warning | Brief the student: first buffet = recovery NOW. Do not wait for the nose to drop. |
| Forgetting gear/flap retraction sequence on go-around | Practice the sequence verbally before every flight: "power, gear, flaps." |
| Slow-flight at too low an altitude (< 10,000 ft MSL) | Enforce the altitude gate. If below 10,000 ft, end the exercise. |
| AoA indexer not cross-checked during slow flight | "If you're not watching the indexer, you're not managing AoA." |
| Holding full aft stick in turning stall | Release back pressure; wings level first; lower nose. Stick must come forward, not back. |
| Power delayed on go-around | "Power is time. Every second of delay is energy you don't have." Say it on every go-around. |
