## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Execute the overhead break entry and break turn precisely
- [ ] Complete the downwind configuration sequence on-schedule without prompting
- [ ] Fly the base-to-final turn without altitude or airspeed deviations
- [ ] Maintain an on-speed, on-glidepath stabilized approach to touchdown
- [ ] Execute a go-around at a called point from a stabilized final
- [ ] Correct for crosswind using crab and de-crab (or wing-low) technique

## Ground Brief

### The Overhead Break Pattern

The overhead break (military traffic pattern) is the standard arrival and landing pattern for tactical aircraft. It is **faster than a rectangular pattern**, allows multiple aircraft to sequence efficiently, and is the pattern used at all military airfields.

```
                     INITIAL POINT
                         |
                   350 KIAS approach
                         |
                   ______|______
                  /             \
        BREAK    /               \
      (over end /                 \
      of runway)                   \
                \                  /
                 \                /
             DOWNWIND            /
           200 KIAS             /
           1,500 ft AGL        /
                 \            /
                  \     BASE /
                   \       /
                    \     /  150 KIAS
                     \   /
                      \ /
                   FINAL
                  130 KIAS
                (AoA donut)
```

### Overhead Break Key Numbers

| Phase | Speed | Config | Altitude |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Initial approach (Initial)** | 350 KIAS | Clean | 1,500 ft AGL |
| **Break turn** | 350 → 200 KIAS | Clean | 1,500 ft AGL |
| **Downwind (established)** | 200 KIAS | Clean → Gear + MVR flaps | 1,500 ft AGL |
| **Abeam threshold** | 180–200 KIAS | Gear + MVR flaps | 1,500 ft AGL |
| **Base** | 150 KIAS | Gear + DN flaps | Descending |
| **Final** | 130–135 KIAS (green donut) | Gear + DN flaps | Descending ~3° |

## Part 1 — Overhead Break Pattern

### Entry: The Initial

After takeoff (or arrival from a navigation route), set up the **Initial Point (IP)**: aligned with the runway, on the same side as the break direction, 3–5 nm from the runway end, 350 KIAS, 1,500 ft AGL.

| Step | Action | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Set airspeed: **350 KIAS** | Reduce from cruise or accelerate from airwork altitude |
| 2 | Set altitude: **1,500 ft AGL** | Hold precisely |
| 3 | Align with runway | Overfly runway centerline extension |
| 4 | Cross the runway end (numbers) | This is the break point; do not break early |
| 5 | **The Break Turn** | Immediate, aggressive action |

### The Break Turn

The break turn is an **aggressive 60° bank, 3–4 G turn** directly from the initial heading, away from the runway centerline (downwind direction). The goals are:
1. Rapidly decelerate from 350 KIAS to ~200 KIAS
2. Arrive abeam the threshold on downwind at pattern altitude
3. Have enough spacing to configure the aircraft before rolling final

| Break Turn Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cross runway threshold: **BREAK** — roll to 60° bank |
| 2 | Apply ~3–4 G of back pressure (smooth and sustained) |
| 3 | Speed brakes OPEN if needed to decelerate faster |
| 4 | Maintain 1,500 ft AGL throughout the turn |
| 5 | Roll out on downwind (runway parallel, opposite direction) at **200 KIAS** |

> **Break Turn Geometry:** The break must be aggressive enough that the aircraft arrives abeam the approach end of the runway with approximately **1 nm of lateral spacing**. Too early/gentle = overshot downwind; too late/steep = tightened pattern.

### Downwind Configuration

Begin configuring **immediately** after rolling out on downwind. The sequence is time-critical.

| Step | Speed | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 200 KIAS | Speed brakes CLOSED (if open) |
| 2 | 200 KIAS | **GEAR DOWN** — 3 green lights |
| 3 | 200 KIAS | **Flaps MVR** — confirm indicator |
| 4 | 180–190 KIAS | Power ~75% N1 to maintain altitude |
| 5 | Abeam threshold | Begin timing (or visual 45°) for base turn |

> **Pattern discipline:** Do not extend downwind more than necessary. The pattern should be **tight** — an A-10 with too much downwind distance makes an awkward base turn and long final.

### Abeam the Threshold — Visual Check

When abeam the runway threshold (90° off your wingtip):
- [ ] Altitude: **1,500 ft AGL** (or within 50 ft)
- [ ] Speed: **180–200 KIAS**
- [ ] Gear: **3 green lights confirmed** (verbalize)
- [ ] Flaps: **MVR confirmed** (verbalize)
- [ ] Power: stabilizing at ~75% N1

### Base Turn

Turn base when the threshold appears **45° behind the wing** (look over the shoulder).

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Roll into 30° bank toward runway |
| 2 | Reduce power to ~65–70% N1; begin descent |
| 3 | Reduce speed to **150 KIAS** |
| 4 | **Flaps DN** below 170 KIAS |
| 5 | Roll out aligned slightly past final course to account for wind |
| 6 | Roll final — intercept centerline |

### Final Approach

- [ ] Roll out on final with runway centerline
- [ ] **Primary reference: AoA indexer green donut** (130–135 KIAS at ~36,000 lbs gross)
- [ ] **Power management:** set power on base; fine-tune on final with **small** adjustments
- [ ] **Glidepath reference:** visual aim point at 1,000 ft runway markers; if the aim point moves up → you're going low; moves down → going high
- [ ] Speed brakes CLOSED (confirm)
- [ ] Call: *"Gear down, three green, flaps down — final"*
- [ ] Stabilized by **500 ft AGL** — mandatory (go-around if not)

### Touchdown & Rollout

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Flare: slight check of descent at 50 ft; fly on |
| 2 | Mains contact: **speed brakes OPEN** |
| 3 | Hold slight aft stick: aerodynamic braking > 100 KIAS |
| 4 | At 100 KIAS: lower nose gear gently |
| 5 | Toe brakes: smooth progressive application |
| 6 | NWS engaged: maintain centerline |
| 7 | Exit runway at taxiway; clear |

## Part 2 — Touch-and-Go Repetition

The student performs **a minimum of 3 break patterns** (touch-and-go allowed for efficiency):

| Pattern # | Focus |
|---|---|
| Pattern 1 | Break turn geometry — timing and bank angle |
| Pattern 2 | Downwind configuration timing — gear and flaps on-speed |
| Pattern 3 | Final approach — AoA indexer and glidepath control |

### Touch-and-Go Procedure

At touchdown:
1. **Do NOT deploy speed brakes** (leave them retracted)
2. **Throttles — Military** smoothly as main gear touch
3. Establish takeoff attitude; rotation at ~135 KIAS
4. **Gear UP** on positive rate
5. **Flaps UP** at 200 KIAS (MVR first if DN, then UP)
6. Re-enter break pattern on downwind or reposition for IP

> **Touch-and-Go config:** With DN flaps, go to MVR first before UP to avoid abrupt pitch change from full-up flap retraction at low speed.

## Part 3 — Go-Around Practice

The instructor will call a go-around at a point on final (typically 300–500 ft AGL) without warning. The student must respond immediately.

### Go-Around Procedure

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | *"Go around"* called (or student decision) |
| 2 | **Throttles — Military** (immediately) |
| 3 | **Pitch up** — establish positive rate; stop descent |
| 4 | Positive rate: **Gear UP** |
| 5 | Accelerate to **170 KIAS**: **Flaps MVR** |
| 6 | Accelerate to **200 KIAS**: **Flaps UP** |
| 7 | Climb to **1,500 ft AGL** pattern altitude |
| 8 | Re-enter downwind for another approach |

> **Go-around timing:** Power must be applied **before** the nose is raised. Raising the nose with idle power causes the aircraft to slow dramatically and increases stall risk. Power first, then attitude.

### Instructor Go-Around Cues

The instructor may call a go-around due to:
- Simulated runway incursion
- Approach not stabilized by 500 ft AGL (instructor exercises this authority)
- Student airspeed below 120 KIAS on final
- Glidepath deviation that places touchdown beyond 2,000 ft

## Part 4 — Crosswind Landing

### Crosswind Theory

The A-10C can accept crosswinds up to **30 kts** (dry runway) or **20 kts** (wet). For training:
- A 10 kt crosswind component builds technique without overwhelming the student
- Two methods are permitted: **Crab** and **de-crab at threshold**, or **Wing-low (sideslip)**

### Crab Method (Preferred for A-10C)

1. On final: **crab into the wind** — set heading such that the aircraft tracks the runway centerline even though the nose points slightly into the wind
2. Maintain crab throughout approach; AoA, glidepath, centerline are all managed normally
3. At flare (~50 ft AGL): **align nose with runway** (kick in rudder opposite crab) simultaneously with touchdown
4. On contact: **upwind aileron input** to prevent drift

### Wing-Low (Sideslip) Method

1. On final: **bank into wind** (upwind aileron) + **opposite rudder** to keep nose aligned with runway
2. Upwind main gear touches first
3. Lower remaining gear; maintain centerline with rudder and aileron
4. Pros: no de-crab required. Cons: sideslip in landing configuration increases drag/sink rate; requires more power.

### Rollout in Crosswind

- **Upwind aileron throughout rollout** — prevent upwind wing from lifting
- Differential braking may be required to counteract drift tendency
- Speed brakes OPEN on mains contact as normal

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Break turn — bank angle | 60° ±5° throughout break turn |
| Break turn — altitude | 1,500 ft ±100 ft throughout break turn |
| Break airspeed | 350 KIAS ±15 KIAS at break point |
| Downwind configuration | Gear and flaps configured before abeam threshold |
| Abeam altitude | 1,500 ft ±100 ft |
| Base turn initiation | Within 5 seconds of threshold 45° position |
| Stabilized approach | On-speed, on-glidepath, on-centerline by 500 ft AGL |
| AoA final | Green donut maintained ≥ 20 seconds prior to threshold |
| Go-around | Initiated within 3 seconds of callout; gear up on positive rate |
| Touchdown centerline | ±15 ft |
| Touchdown zone | Within first 1,500 ft of runway |
| Speed brakes (full-stop) | Within 3 seconds of mains contact |
| 3 patterns minimum | Complete without instructor intervention in final 2 patterns |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Breaking too early (before runway end) | "Cross the numbers, then break." Mark the threshold and enforce the discipline. |
| Break turn too shallow — long downwind | Bank 60°, apply 3 G. An aggressive break is required. A gentle turn leaves too much ground. |
| Configuration behind schedule | Gear comes out at roll-out on downwind, not abeam the threshold. Start early. |
| Late base turn — overshooting final | Threshold at 45° means NOW. Do not extend. |
| Power pulled too much on base | Sink rate becomes unrecoverable on short final. Keep ~65–70% N1 on base. |
| Go-around hesitation | "Go around" is an immediate action — throttles go forward in the same instant the words are said. |
| Losing altitude in break turn | The A-10C breaks at 1.0 G altitude = zero acceleration. Must maintain altitude with back pressure AND power awareness. |
| Crosswind — late de-crab | Practice at altitude first: set crab angle, then kick it straight. Know the control inputs before attempting at touchdown. |
