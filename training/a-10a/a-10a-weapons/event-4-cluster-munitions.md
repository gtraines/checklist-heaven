## Ground Brief

### Cluster Munitions — Area Weapons Philosophy

Cluster munitions are area weapons. They are not precision — they cover a footprint. The pilot's job is to place that footprint over the target area. Individual aim point accuracy matters less than correct altitude, speed, and target-area alignment.

### CBU-87 vs. CBU-97 — Key Differences

| Feature | CBU-87 (CEM) | CBU-97 (SFW) |
|---|---|---|
| Submunitions | 202 × BLU-97/B bomblets | 10 × BLU-108 → 40 Skeet warheads |
| Guidance | None (ballistic scatter) | Passive IR per Skeet (seeks heat) |
| Effect | Shaped charge + frag + incendiary | EFP (Explosively Formed Penetrator) |
| Pattern | ~200 × 400 m scatter zone | Searches area under canister descent |
| Best target | Mixed/soft targets in the open | **Massed armor** (MBTs, IFVs in formation) |
| Effectiveness vs. dug-in infantry | Excellent | Poor (no heat signature) |
| Effectiveness vs. tanks | Limited (light armor only) | **Excellent** — top-attack defeats MBT roof armor |

### How the CBU-97 Works

```
  Aircraft releases CBU-97 at 500 ft AGL, 350 kts
         |
         ↓
  Casing opens → 10 BLU-108 canisters separate
         |
         ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓  ↓
  Each canister descends by parachute
         |
         ↓
  Canister spins → ejects 4 Skeet warheads outward
         |
    🔍  🔍  🔍  🔍   ← Each Skeet scans for IR (heat) below
         |
         ↓
    Skeet detects tank engine heat → fires EFP downward
         |
        💥 ← Top-attack kill through roof armor
```

**Key insight:** The CBU-97 is a "smart" weapon that finds its own targets within the scatter zone. You do not need to aim at individual vehicles — you aim at the formation and let the Skeets do the hunting.

### Release Parameters

| Parameter | CBU-87 | CBU-97 |
|---|---|---|
| Delivery profile | Level or shallow dive (10–20°) | Level or shallow dive (10–20°) |
| Release altitude | 1,000–2,000 ft AGL | 1,000–2,000 ft AGL |
| Airspeed | 300–400 kts | 300–400 kts |
| Min safe altitude | 500 ft AGL | 500 ft AGL |
| Fuze time | Altitude-dependent (preset in loadout) | Altitude-dependent |

> **Warning:** Releasing below 500 ft AGL may result in submunitions not arming or Skeets not having time to scan. Releasing above 3,000 ft AGL spreads the pattern too wide, reducing area density.

## Part 1 — CBU-87 CEM Employment

### Target: Soft Target Cluster

The CBU-87 is your general-purpose area weapon. Use it against:
- Truck parks and logistics areas
- Infantry positions in the open
- Mixed columns (APCs, trucks, support vehicles)
- Fuel and ammunition storage areas

### Switchology
1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Weapon Select** — cycle to CBU-87 (`D`)
3. - [ ] Confirm HUD shows bomb delivery cues

### Attack Procedure (Level Delivery)

**Entry:** 1,500 ft AGL, 350 kts. Target at 12 o'clock, 2–3 NM.

1. - [ ] Establish level flight — wings level, 350 kts
2. - [ ] Align aircraft with the **long axis** of the target area (maximize footprint coverage)
3. - [ ] Use CCIP pipper or CCRP cue to time release
4. - [ ] **Release** (`Space`) when pipper is on the leading edge of the target cluster
5. - [ ] Continue straight and level for 3 seconds after release
6. - [ ] Turn off target — do not overfly the impact zone
7. - [ ] **Master Arm** — SAFE

### Pattern Orientation

Align your attack axis with the target's longest dimension:

```
  Good: Aircraft flies along the column
  ═══════►
  🚛 🚛 🚛 🚛 🚛 🚛 🚛 🚛     ← column axis
  ═══════════════════════════   ← CBU footprint covers column

  Bad: Aircraft flies across the column
  ═══════►
  🚛       ← Only 1–2 trucks in footprint
  🚛
  🚛
  🚛
```

### Alternative: Shallow Dive Delivery

For improved accuracy, use a 10–15° dive from 3,000 ft AGL:
1. Roll in at 10–15° nose-low
2. Place CCIP pipper on target area center
3. Release at 1,500 ft AGL
4. Pull off at 1,000 ft AGL minimum

## Part 2 — CBU-97 SFW Employment

### Target: Armored Formation

The CBU-97 is the premier anti-armor area weapon. Save it for confirmed armor concentrations — using it against soft targets wastes its capability.

### Switchology
1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Weapon Select** — cycle to CBU-97 (`D`)
3. - [ ] Confirm HUD shows bomb delivery cues

### Attack Procedure (Level Delivery)

**Entry:** 1,500 ft AGL, 350 kts. Armor formation at 12 o'clock, 2–3 NM.

1. - [ ] Establish level flight — wings level, 350 kts
2. - [ ] Align with the **long axis** of the armor formation
3. - [ ] **Release** (`Space`) when pipper is over the leading element of the formation
4. - [ ] Continue straight and level for 3 seconds
5. - [ ] Egress — turn off axis
6. - [ ] **Master Arm** — SAFE

### Post-Release — What to Expect

After CBU-97 release:
- 3–5 seconds: casing opens, canisters separate
- 5–10 seconds: parachute descent, canister spin-up
- 10–15 seconds: Skeet ejection and IR scanning
- 15–20 seconds: EFP impacts on detected armor

**Do not circle back for BDA until at least 20 seconds after release.** Skeets are still descending and firing during this window.

### CBU-97 Effectiveness Factors

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Hot engines (running vehicles) | Excellent — strong IR signature |
| Cold/parked vehicles | Reduced — Skeets may not detect |
| Rain / fog | Degraded IR detection |
| Spacing < 50 m | Overkill — wasted Skeets per target |
| Spacing > 200 m | Some vehicles may be outside scan zone |

> **Realism Note:** DCS is generous with CBU-97 effectiveness. In reality, cold or camouflaged vehicles, thermal decoys, and adverse weather significantly degrade Skeet acquisition. Expect near-100% kill rates in DCS against active armor — this would not be realistic against a prepared enemy.

## Part 3 — Weapon Selection Exercise

This exercise reinforces correct weapon selection. The instructor presents target descriptions and the student must identify the correct cluster weapon (or explain why a cluster weapon is inappropriate).

### Scenarios

| # | Target Description | Correct Weapon | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 × T-72 tanks in wedge formation, engines running | **CBU-97** | Massed armor with hot IR signatures |
| 2 | Truck park: 12 supply trucks, 4 fuel tankers, open area | **CBU-87** | Soft targets — CBU-97 Skeets may not detect cold trucks |
| 3 | Single hardened bunker | **Neither** — use Mk-84 or AGM-65K | Cluster weapons are area weapons; single point target needs precision |
| 4 | Infantry platoon in tree line, dug in | **CBU-87** | Frag/incendiary effect vs. personnel; CBU-97 Skeets need vehicle heat |
| 5 | 4 × BMP-2 IFVs, parked, engines cold, under camo nets | **CBU-87** or **Gun** | CBU-97 Skeets may not acquire cold/camouflaged targets |
| 6 | Mixed column: 3 tanks + 5 APCs + 8 trucks, moving | **CBU-97** on armor element, then **CBU-87** on soft vehicles (or gun) | Prioritize armor with smart weapon; clean up soft targets with area/gun |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Using CBU-97 on soft targets | CBU-97 Skeets seek IR; cold trucks may be invisible — use CBU-87 |
| Using CBU-87 on heavy armor | BLU-97 shaped charge is light — ineffective vs. MBT; use CBU-97 |
| Releasing too high (>3,000 ft AGL) | Submunitions scatter too wide — density drops below effective level |
| Releasing too low (<500 ft AGL) | Submunitions may not arm; Skeets have insufficient scan time |
| Flying perpendicular to target axis | Footprint misses most of the formation — align with target long axis |
| Circling back too quickly for BDA | CBU-97 Skeets need 15–20 sec; re-entering early is hazardous |
