## Course Description

The A-10A Weapons Employment Course (WEC) qualifies a pilot to safely and effectively employ the full range of air-to-ground weapons available on the A-10A "Thunderbolt II" against ground targets in day VMC conditions.

> **DCS Module Note:** The DCS A-10A is a **Flaming Cliffs 3 (FC3)** module with a simplified systems model — there is no clickable cockpit. All weapons are selected by cycling through stores (`D`), and targeting uses HUD CCIP/CCRP symbology and the Maverick's built-in seeker. The A-10A **does not have a Targeting Pod (TGP)** — AGM-65 Mavericks use their own seeker heads. The Pave Penny pod detects laser spots from external sources (JTAC/buddy lase) but cannot self-designate.

Upon successful completion, the student will be qualified to:
- Employ the GAU-8/A Avenger 30mm cannon against armored and soft targets from a diving attack profile
- Fire Hydra 70 rockets in CCIP from a diving attack
- Deliver Mk-82, Mk-82AIR, and Mk-84 gravity bombs in both CCIP (dive) and CCRP (level) modes
- Employ CBU-87 Combined Effects Munitions and CBU-97 Sensor Fuzed Weapons against area targets
- Employ AGM-65D/G (IR) and AGM-65H/K (TV) Maverick missiles against point targets

Defensive counter-air (AIM-9M employment), buddy lasing with Pave Penny, and multi-ship coordination are **not** covered in this course.

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this course, the student must:
- [ ] Have **completed the A-10A Basic Qualification Training (BQT)** ([link](../a-10a-bqt/overview.md))
- [ ] Be able to fly a stabilized dive and pull off target independently
- [ ] Understand basic DCS Master Arm, weapons cycling (`D`), and pickle procedures
- [ ] Have reviewed the **A-10A DCS Checklist** weapons section ([link](../../../checklists/aircraft/a-10a.md))

## Weapons Quick Reference

| Weapon | Type | Mode | Max Range | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GAU-8/A | 30mm Cannon | CCIP | < 2 NM | Short bursts; manage recoil |
| Hydra 70 (M151) | 2.75" HE Rocket | CCIP | 1–2 NM | Dive angle ≥ 15° |
| Hydra 70 (M274) | WP Smoke Rocket | CCIP | 1–2 NM | Target marking for FAC/CAS |
| Mk-82 | 500 lb GP Bomb | CCIP/CCRP | varies | Min safe release alt 600 m AGL |
| Mk-82AIR | 500 lb High Drag | CCIP | Low level | Ballute — safe for low-alt passes |
| Mk-84 | 2,000 lb GP Bomb | CCIP/CCRP | varies | Massive frag — high pull-off alt |
| CBU-87 | Cluster (CEM) | CCIP/Level | Area | 300–600 m AGL release alt |
| CBU-97 | Sensor Fuzed (SFW) | CCIP/Level | Area | Skeets seek heat — anti-armor |
| AGM-65D | IR Maverick | Guided | 6–8 NM | IR seeker lock; fire-and-forget |
| AGM-65G | IR Maverick (Heavy) | Guided | 6–8 NM | 300 lb warhead; ships/bunkers |
| AGM-65H | TV Maverick | Guided | 4–6 NM | Daylight only; 125 lb warhead |
| AGM-65K | TV Maverick (Heavy) | Guided | 6–10 NM | 300 lb warhead; bridges/bunkers |

## Grading Standards

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All standards met; student may progress |
| **REPEAT** | One or more standards not met; event must be re-flown |
| **DOWN** | Unsafe act — fratricide, uncontrolled flight into terrain, or weapons release outside safe parameters |

### Incomplete Item (II)
An II is issued when a student fails one weapons category but passes others. The student must re-demonstrate the failed weapon type before proceeding to the Check Ride.
