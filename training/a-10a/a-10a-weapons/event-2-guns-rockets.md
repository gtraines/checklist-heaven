## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Employ the GAU-8/A cannon against a stationary vehicle target from a diving attack, achieving at least one confirmed hit per pass
- [ ] Demonstrate burst discipline (1–2 second bursts) and manage recoil-induced airspeed loss
- [ ] Select and fire Hydra 70 M151 HE rockets in CCIP from a diving attack, impacting within 50 m of target
- [ ] Fire M274 White Phosphorus rockets to mark a target for follow-on attack

## Ground Brief

### The Diving Attack — Standard Profile

All unguided weapons in this course use the standard diving attack profile. The pilot controls three variables:

1. **Dive angle** — steeper is more accurate; shallower trades accuracy for survivability
2. **Airspeed** — too fast shortens time on target; too slow reduces escape energy
3. **Release range** — use HUD CCIP cue; do not estimate visually

```
     Entry: 8,000–12,000 ft MSL, 300–350 kts
            ↓
     Roll in — establish dive angle
            ↓
     [Pipper tracking target]
            ↓
     Fire / Release
            ↓
     Immediate pull-off (≥ 4 G)
            ↓
     Egress: Turn off threat axis
```

### Minimum Pull-Off Altitudes

| Weapon | Min Release / Cease Fire Alt |
|---|---|
| GAU-8/A Gun | 2,000 ft AGL |
| Hydra 70 rockets | 2,000 ft AGL |

> **Warning:** The A-10A is slow. A diving attack at 350 kts in a 20° dive covers ground rapidly. Do not fixate on the target — set a hard deck and honor it. The Warthog does not have the energy for a zoom climb if you get low.

## Part 1 — GAU-8/A Gun Employment

### Switchology
1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Weapon Select** — cycle to GUN (`D` until HUD shows Gun Cross)
3. - [ ] **HUD** — confirm CCIP Gun Cross is displayed

### Attack Procedure

**Set-up:** Enter the target area at 10,000 ft MSL, 300–350 kts. Position the target at 12 o'clock, 3–4 NM.

1. - [ ] Roll in: establish **15–20° nose-low dive**, wings level
2. - [ ] Place the CCIP Gun Cross on the target vehicle
3. - [ ] At **4,000–5,000 ft slant range** (~1.2–1.5 NM): open fire — **1–2 second burst** (`Space`)
4. - [ ] Observe: tracer stream converges on target area
5. - [ ] If miss — correct pipper placement, fire **one more burst**
6. - [ ] At **2,000 ft AGL minimum** — **pull off**: ≥ 4 G pull, wings level or slight bank away from target
7. - [ ] **Master Arm** — SAFE (`O`) after egress

### GAU-8/A Technique Notes

- **Burst discipline:** 1,174 rounds sounds like a lot — at 3,900 rpm, a 3-second burst consumes ~195 rounds. You have roughly 6 full bursts before empty. Do not fire continuously.
- **Recoil management:** The gun produces ~10,000 lbs of recoil. During a burst:
  - Airspeed drops noticeably (5–10 kts per burst)
  - The aircraft pitches slightly nose-up
  - The pipper drifts off target
- **Solution:** Fire short bursts. Release trigger. Re-acquire pipper on target. Fire again.
- **Multiple passes:** Plan 2–3 passes per target group. Each pass, fire 1–2 bursts. Save ammo for later engagements.

### Dive Angle Effects

| Dive Angle | Accuracy | Exposure Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10° | Moderate | Long | Easier to fly; pipper moves slowly |
| 20° | Good | Moderate | **Recommended for training** |
| 30° | Excellent | Short | Fast closure; requires quick trigger |

> **Realism Note:** Real A-10 pilots preferred 30° dive angles for gun work against armor, trading exposure time for accuracy. The API round's penetration improves at steeper impact angles. In DCS, 20° is adequate for training while managing the learning curve.

## Part 2 — Hydra 70 Rocket Employment (M151 HE)

The Hydra 70 is an area suppression weapon. Individual rockets are inaccurate — effectiveness comes from firing salvos into a target area.

### Switchology
1. - [ ] **Master Arm** — ON (`O`)
2. - [ ] **Weapon Select** — cycle to Rockets (`D` until HUD shows rocket CCIP pipper)
3. - [ ] Confirm CCIP pipper appears on HUD and moves with range

### Attack Procedure

1. - [ ] Enter at 10,000 ft MSL, 300–350 kts
2. - [ ] Roll in to **15–20° dive**
3. - [ ] Place CCIP pipper on target cluster
4. - [ ] At **5,000–6,000 ft slant range** (~1.5–1.8 NM): **fire** (`Space`) — hold for 1–2 seconds (salvo of 4–7 rockets)
5. - [ ] At 2,000 ft AGL — **hard pull-off**, exit threat axis
6. - [ ] **Master Arm** — SAFE

### Rocket Accuracy Table

| Dive Angle | Release Range | Expected Dispersion |
|---|---|---|
| 10° | 6,000 ft | ~80 m spread |
| 15° | 5,000 ft | ~50 m spread |
| 20° | 4,500 ft | ~30 m spread |

### Rocket Technique Notes
- **Salvo size:** Each LAU-68 pod carries 7 rockets. A 1-second press fires ~3–4 rockets. Two passes per pod.
- **Aim center of target cluster:** Rockets spread naturally — aim at the geometric center of the target group, not at individual vehicles.
- **Wind effects:** Rockets are ballistic after leaving the pod. Crosswind will drift the pattern. Compensate by aiming slightly upwind.

## Part 3 — Target Marking (M274 WP)

The M274 White Phosphorus ("Willie Pete") rocket produces a dense white smoke cloud on impact. It is used to mark target positions for other aircraft or for FAC/JTAC coordination.

### Employment

Same technique as M151 HE rockets, with two differences:
1. Fire **single rockets** (tap `Space` briefly) — you need a visible mark, not area saturation
2. Accept wider dispersion — the smoke cloud itself is the marker, not the impact point

### Practice Exercise
1. - [ ] Select an unmarked target area (no vehicles)
2. - [ ] Fire one M274 rocket to mark the position
3. - [ ] Observe: white smoke plume visible from altitude
4. - [ ] Circle back and use the smoke to orient a simulated follow-on gun pass

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Long continuous gun bursts (>3 sec) | Brief: "Two seconds on, release, re-aim, two seconds on" |
| Pipper drift during firing (recoil) | Expect it — fire in short bursts; re-acquire between bursts |
| Shallow dive (<10°) with rockets | Rockets scatter badly at shallow angles; minimum 15° dive |
| Fixating on target — late pull-off | Set 2,000 ft AGL as a hard deck; honor it every pass |
| Firing rockets at individual vehicles | Rockets are area weapons — aim at cluster center, not one truck |
| Master Arm left ON after pass | Build the habit: cease fire, safety on, egress |
