## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this event the student will be able to:
- [ ] Fly a consistent rectangular traffic pattern at correct altitudes and airspeeds on every lap
- [ ] Configure the aircraft correctly on each pattern leg
- [ ] Execute a stabilized final approach using the AOA indexer as primary reference
- [ ] Perform a safe full-stop landing using correct flare technique and progressive braking
- [ ] Execute a go-around from any point in the pattern, including short final
- [ ] Perform a minimum of five approaches, including at least one go-around and one touch-and-go

---

## Ground Brief

### Objective of Pattern Work
Repetition is the only path to landing proficiency. This event is primarily about building consistency — the same power settings, the same speeds, the same altitudes, every lap. A student who can fly the same approach twice in a row is ready for the Check Ride.

### Standard Traffic Pattern — A-10A

```
                    [UPWIND]
          Takeoff ←────────────────────────────────
                                                    ↑
                                                [CROSSWIND]
                                                    ↑
          [DOWNWIND] ←──────────────────────────────
          1,500 ft AGL
          180–200 KIAS
          (Gear/Flaps DOWN abeam threshold)
                |
                | [BASE]
                | Descending; 150–160 KIAS
                |
          [FINAL]
          Descending 3°
          AOA: green donut
          ~130–140 KIAS
               ↓
         [THRESHOLD]
```

### Pattern Altitudes and Speeds

| Leg | Altitude (AGL) | Speed (KIAS) | Configuration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upwind** | Climbing to 1,500 ft | 160–180 | Gear UP; Flaps MVR → UP |
| **Crosswind** | 1,500 ft | 180–200 | Clean |
| **Downwind** | 1,500 ft | 180–200 | Clean initially |
| **Abeam threshold** | 1,500 ft | 160–170 | Gear DN; Flaps DN |
| **Base** | Descending (1,000 ft) | 150–160 | Gear DN; Flaps DN |
| **Final** | Descending | ~135 | Gear DN; Flaps DN |
| **Touchdown** | 0 | 120–125 | — |

## Pattern Procedures

### Upwind / Departure
- [ ] After takeoff: climb straight ahead to **500 ft AGL** before any turns
- [ ] At 500 ft AGL: turn crosswind (90° from runway heading)
- [ ] At **1,500 ft AGL**: turn downwind (parallel to runway, opposite direction)
- [ ] Level off at 1,500 ft AGL; trim for 180–200 KIAS

### Downwind
- [ ] Fly parallel to runway at **1,500 ft AGL** and **180–200 KIAS**
- [ ] Keep runway in sight off the wing at approximately 1.0–1.5 nm lateral distance
- [ ] **Abeam the runway threshold:**
  - [ ] Note time (3-turn or 3-nm distance rule for base turn)
  - [ ] **Reduce power** to ~60% N2
  - [ ] **Gear DOWN** (`G`) — verify 3 green
  - [ ] **Flaps DN** (`LCtrl + F`) — verify position
  - [ ] **Trim** slightly nose-up
  - [ ] Allow speed to settle to **160–170 KIAS**

### Base Leg
- [ ] Turn base approximately 1.0 nm past the runway threshold (adjust for pattern size)
- [ ] Descend at ~500 ft/min; maintain **150–160 KIAS**
- [ ] Begin final approach briefing: speed check, fuel check, gear check
- [ ] **Before final turn:** "Gear down — three green. Flaps down. Speed 155."

### Final Approach
- [ ] Roll out aligned with runway centerline
- [ ] **Primary reference: AOA indexer — green donut**
- [ ] Secondary reference: glidepath (PAPI 2 red / 2 white = on glidepath, if available)
- [ ] **Stabilization gate:** by **500 ft AGL**, the aircraft must be:
  - Aligned with runway ± 5°
  - On glidepath (no large deviations)
  - Speed stable (green donut ± 1 indicator)
  - Descent rate steady (~600–800 ft/min)
- [ ] If NOT stabilized by 500 ft AGL: **GO AROUND** (see below)
- [ ] Speed brakes — confirm CLOSED before flare
- [ ] At **100 ft AGL:** no further large corrections — commit or go-around

### Flare & Touchdown
- [ ] At **50 ft AGL:** reduce power slightly; apply gentle back pressure (2–3° pitch increase)
- [ ] The A-10A lands **flat** — this is not a fighter flare; just arrest the descent rate
- [ ] Main gear contact at **~120–125 KIAS**
- [ ] Nosewheel down smoothly; engage NWS (`Q`)
- [ ] **Speed Brakes OPEN** (`LShift + B`) immediately after main gear contact
- [ ] Allow speed to decay to **~80 KIAS** before applying progressive wheel brakes

## Approaches

### Full-Stop Landing
Standard procedure as described above. Speed brakes + progressive braking to full stop.

### Touch-and-Go
1. Touchdown normally
2. Immediately: **Throttles MAX** + **Flaps MVR** simultaneously
3. Maintain runway centerline
4. Rotate at **125–130 KIAS**; climb straight ahead
5. Clean up on schedule (gear up positive rate; flaps up at 150 KIAS + 200 ft AGL)

**Touch-and-Go Standard:** Full power within 3 sec of touchdown; rotation within 1,500 ft from touchdown point.

### Low Approach (Go-Around Practice)
- Fly a full approach but do NOT land
- Go-around initiated at **100 ft AGL** (instructor calls "go-around" or student self-initiates)
- Evaluates go-around technique without the landing phase

---

## Go-Around Procedure

The go-around is an **immediate action** — it must be reflexive, not deliberated.

**At any point on approach:**

1. **POWER** — FULL (`PgUp` or throttle to max); do NOT wait
2. **ATTITUDE** — establish positive climb attitude (8–10° nose up)
3. **VERIFY** — positive rate of climb on VSI before gear retraction
4. **GEAR UP** (`G`) — only after confirmed positive rate
5. **FLAPS MVR** (`LShift + F`) — after gear is up and above 150 KIAS
6. **CLIMB** — maintain Vy (200–210 KIAS) and re-enter the pattern

> **Critical:** Do NOT retract gear until positive climb is confirmed. The most common go-around accident is gear retraction before the aircraft is actually climbing.

### Go-Around from Short Final (< 200 ft AGL)
- Full power immediately
- Maintain runway heading until above 500 ft AGL
- **Hold gear down** until confirmed climbing and above 200 ft AGL
- Climb straight ahead; do not attempt crosswind turn until 500 ft AGL

---

## Crosswind Landing Technique

When winds are > 5 knots across the runway, use the **wing-low / sideslip** method:

1. **Final approach:** Crab into the wind to track the runway centerline
2. **Short final (300–200 ft AGL):** Transition from crab to sideslip:
   - Lower the **upwind wing** (bank toward the wind)
   - Apply **downwind rudder** to align the nose with the runway centerline
3. **Touchdown:** Touch down on the **upwind main gear first**
4. **Rollout:** Lower the downwind gear; maintain direction with NWS and rudder

> **Warning:** Do NOT land in a crab — side-loads on the main gear are damaging at landing speeds. De-crab is mandatory.

## Completion Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Downwind altitude | 1,500 ft AGL ±100 ft for 3 of 5 patterns |
| Downwind configuration | Gear and flaps down abeam threshold every pattern |
| Stabilization gate | Stable by 500 ft AGL on ≥ 4 of 5 approaches |
| AOA on final | Green donut ±1 unit; maintained for ≥ 20 sec |
| Touchdown point | Within first 1,000 ft of runway |
| Touchdown speed | 115–135 KIAS |
| Go-around execution | Full power within 2 sec of call; gear up on positive rate only |
| Directional control | Runway centerline ±15 ft on rollout |
| Braking | No wheel lockup; stopped within runway length |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Pattern too large / too small | Use ground references; standardize the base turn point |
| Gear or flaps not down abeam | Build checklist discipline: "Abeam = gear down" as a call-out |
| Chasing the AOA donut with large power inputs | Small corrections; half-inch of throttle; anticipate don't react |
| Not stabilized by 500 ft AGL | If in doubt, go around — no shame in a go-around |
| Flaring too aggressively (float) | The A-10A lands flat; check the descent, don't flare |
| Heavy braking immediately | Speed brakes, then coast to 80 KIAS, then brake |
| Gear retraction on go-around before positive rate | Hard rule: wait for the VSI to show a climb before `G` |
| Landing in a crab (crosswind) | De-crab on short final; wing-low for the last 300 ft |
